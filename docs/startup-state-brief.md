# AI Builder Day — Utah GOEO ("Startup State")

> Source: <https://startupstate.netlify.app>
> Host: Utah Governor's Office of Economic Opportunity (GOEO)
> Format: Weekend hackathon, two days, working prototype required

Fintech.** There are more entrepreneurs building here right now than at any point in this state's history — and most of them have no idea what the state has waiting for them. Free capital. ## The Problem

> Founders don't need more resources. They need clarity.

The programs are here — free education, capital access, mentorship, counseling, community networks. The state and its partners have invested heavily to make Utah one of the best places in the world to build a company.

The problem is **findability**. Everything a founder needs is on `startup.utah.gov`, but it's built like a library, and founders don't need a library — they need a guide who already knows their situation. Right now there's no clear entry point, no way to filter for what's actually relevant, and no way to see the bigger picture of what's being built across the state.

> "We have world-class resources sitting on a shelf. A founder moving 100 miles an hour isn't going to find them in time — and that's on us to fix."

## The Challenge — Two Products, One Platform

> The best builds don't end on Sunday. They end up on `startup.utah.gov`.

> **Warning:** They'd rather have one exceptional product than two half-baked ones. If you build both, commit to both. If something has to give, let it show in polish — not in fundamentals.

### Part 1 — The Founder's Navigator (Resources)

Reimagine how a founder finds the right state resource in **under two minutes**. AI chatbot, smart filtering, guided quiz, or something nobody's thought of yet — make it fast, personal, and impossible to get lost in.

- **Personalization is everything.** A landscaping business owner in St. George needs different things than a pre-revenue founder in Lehi.
- **Data is provided.** A complete spreadsheet of every state resource (categories, descriptions, links) is yours to use.
- **Must be updatable** by non-technical staff (new programs launch constantly, no developer redeploys).
- **AI is encouraged.**

### Part 2 — The Utah Startup Map

An interactive, visual map of every business in Utah's startup ecosystem — beautiful enough to project for international investors, useful enough for a founder to find their next customer / partner / acquisition.

- **Self-service profiles** — companies can claim, create, and update their own page (with a lightweight verification method).
- **Required profile fields:** Name, Website, Employees, Sector, Year founded, LinkedIn, Description, Address, Hiring status, Job postings, Photo gallery.
- **Filterable and fast** — by sector, size, stage, hiring status, location.
- **Reference:** [pampam.city/utah-startup-map](https://pampam.city/utah-startup-map) for inspiration.

## Hard Requirements

- **Working prototype** — live, clickable demo. Not slides, not mockups.
- **Personalized experience** — should feel built for the specific user. First-time founder vs. scaling company should get very different surfaces.
- **Easily updatable** — non-technical content updates without redeployment.
- **Map: self-service profiles** — claim/add flow with basic verification, all required fields.
- **Dual audience ready** — works for a time-pressed founder *and* an investor seeing Utah for the first time.
- **Production quality** — may go live on a state government website. Should look like it belongs there.
