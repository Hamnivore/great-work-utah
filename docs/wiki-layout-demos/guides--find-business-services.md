# Find Business Services in Utah

**Status:** Draft
**Confidence:** Medium
**Audience:** Utah founders looking for legal, accounting, recruiting, or regulatory help
**Updated:** 2026-05-09
**Layout:** marginalia

## Start Here

Most Utah founders need three things in their first six months: incorporation, an accountant who understands R&D credits, and a recruiter or two who actually return calls. The order matters less than getting all three under contract before you raise.

:::margin
*Service providers are first-class helpers. Don't bury them in a generic resource list.*
:::

The list below is contextual — different sub-disciplines fit different stages. Treat it as a map, not a ranking.

## Legal

For early-stage venture work, default to a firm that has done at least a dozen Utah priced rounds in the last two years. Parsons Behle, Kirton McConkie, and Cooley's Salt Lake office are the obvious candidates. For pre-incorporation help, a couple of Utah solo practitioners have built a reliable practice doing flat-fee formations.

:::margin
fig. 1 — typical engagement: flat-fee formation ($1.5k–$3k), then hourly afterward. Ask before signing.
:::

If your venture is medical-device or biotech, ask specifically whether the partner has cleared a 510(k) or run an IND filing in the last three years. Most generalist partners haven't.

## Accounting

The Utah accountant problem is bimodal: there are a lot of generalists who handle small business well, and a small number of firms (Eide Bailly, WSRP) that do real R&D credit and venture-aware tax work.

:::margin
*Most Utah accountants are not deep-tech-fluent. Ask about R&D credits in the first call. If the answer is vague, keep looking.*
:::

For deep-tech ventures, R&D credits are not optional — they are often the single largest non-dilutive subsidy you'll qualify for in years one through three. The right accountant will know this. The wrong one will tell you it's complicated.

## Recruiting

Below series A, you don't need a retained recruiter. You need someone who knows the Utah market well enough to do referrals on a contingent basis. Above series A, hiring directors of engineering is genuinely hard in Utah and a retained search starts to make sense.

:::margin
fig. 2 — Utah salary expectations ran ~80% of Bay Area for senior IC roles in 2024. The gap is closing.
:::

The shortlist for retained recruiters in Utah deep tech is small enough to fit on one hand. Ask the founders of the most-recently-public Utah deep-tech companies who they used.

## Regulatory

Health-tech and medical-device ventures: the Utah regulatory consultant pool is shallow. Most credible work happens via Boston- and DC-based consultants who fly in. Plan for it.
