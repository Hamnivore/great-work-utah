# Why we rank publicly

**Status:** Draft
**Confidence:** High
**Audience:** Anyone wondering why our guides have opinions
**Updated:** 2026-05-09
**Layout:** manifesto

## Hedging is unkind

A founder who wants to know which Utah resources are worth their afternoon does not need to read a directory. They need someone to tell them where to start. We start.

## Trust requires evidence

Every recommendation cites a fact page. Every fact page cites a source. If you disagree with the ranking, you can follow the citation and argue with the evidence — not with the ranking itself.

## The alternative is a directory

LinkedIn, Wellfound, Indeed, Crunchbase. We are surrounded by directories. Directories are honest about being uncommitted. We want to be honest about being committed.

## Confidence is not certainty

Every page carries a confidence field. *Medium* means we believe it but we have not pressure-tested every claim. *Speculative* means we are guessing in public so a reader with better information can tell us we are wrong.

## Rankings are by audience

There is no universal best helper. There is the best helper for *a first-time medical-device founder pre-incorporation*. There is the best helper for *a senior ML engineer tired of California*. We rank inside a context, not above one.

## We expect to be argued with

If you read a guide and think we are wrong, raise your hand. The guide will get better. That is the deal.
