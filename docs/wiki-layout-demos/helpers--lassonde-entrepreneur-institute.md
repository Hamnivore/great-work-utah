# Lassonde Entrepreneur Institute

**Status:** Draft
**Confidence:** High
**Focus:** Student entrepreneurs, university commercialization
**Audience:** Student founders, first-time founders, early-stage Utah ventures
**Location:** University of Utah, Salt Lake City, UT
**Updated:** 2026-05-09
**Layout:** field-guide

## Summary

The University of Utah's startup studio. Houses a residential community (Lassonde Studios), a dozen-plus funding tracks, mentorship and coursework, and an annual roster of student-founded ventures that ship.

## How They Help

- Non-dilutive funding tracks: Get Seeded, Bench-to-Bedside, the Lassonde Founders program, the Hours Experience, and several focused awards per year.
- A residential building (Lassonde Studios, 400 beds) where students live, build, and run prototype shops.
- A staffed mentorship layer plus coursework that scaffolds students from idea to incorporation.
- A demo day cycle and end-of-year showcases that put student ventures in front of investors.

## Best-Fit Founders

- Undergrad and grad students at the U.
- First-time founders who need scaffolding, not just capital.
- Pre-revenue ventures that benefit from semester-rhythm milestones.

## Cost / Engagement Model

Free for U students. Funding awards are non-dilutive grants (program-dependent amounts).

## Proof Points

- Hundreds of student ventures launched since 2001.
- Bench-to-Bedside has produced multiple FDA-cleared medical devices.
- Alumni include founders of Owlet, Kodiak Cakes, and others.

## Cautions

- Best for student-stage; not for serious commercial scale-ups.
- Mentor quality varies — match to a mentor with relevant domain experience or risk generic advice.
- Programs assume student status; non-students looking for similar scaffolding should look at BoomStartup, Tech-X Foundry, or sector-specific accelerators instead.

## Good Matches

- A first-time student founder with a hardware idea — Get Seeded plus Lassonde Studios.
- A grad student with a research-derived medical device — Bench-to-Bedside.
- An undergrad with a consumer software idea — Hours Experience plus Lassonde Founders.
