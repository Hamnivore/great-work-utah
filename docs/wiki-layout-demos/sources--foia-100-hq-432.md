# FOIA Response, FBI File 100-HQ-432

**Status:** Source
**Confidence:** Medium
**Focus:** Historical record, partially redacted
**Location:** Originally Salt Lake City field office, 1971
**Updated:** 2026-05-09
**Layout:** erasure

## Excerpt One

The subject of the file, referenced throughout as "the engineer," appears in correspondence with parties whose names have been redacted under exemption (b)(7)(C). The available pages indicate the engineer was working in Salt Lake City between 1968 and 1972 on a project the file describes only as "of mutual interest to the Bureau and the parties named above."

## Excerpt Two

A memorandum dated April 17, 1971 references a meeting at an unspecified Utah location. Attendees include the engineer and at least three other individuals whose names are blacked out. The memo's stated purpose is redacted; its outcome is described as "satisfactory pending receipt of the materials."

## Excerpt Three

A second memorandum, undated but apparently from late 1971, indicates the engineer's connection to the project terminated under unspecified circumstances. A handwritten margin note, partially legible, reads "see also the report of," followed by a redacted name and a file reference also redacted.

## Excerpt Four

The remaining pages in the file are nearly entirely redacted, with only header information and routing stamps visible. A cover sheet indicates the file was reviewed for declassification in 1995 and again in 2014; both reviews resulted in the redactions remaining in place under the original exemptions.

## Caveats

Press releases and FOIA-released files are leads, not neutral evidence. The narrative shape suggested by the visible fragments may not match the actual content of the redacted material.

## Evidence

- FBI FOIA Reading Room, file 100-HQ-432, partial release 1995 and supplemental release 2014.

## Open Questions

- The identity of "the engineer" — multiple Utah engineers active in 1968–1972 plausibly fit the description.
- The nature of the project, which the file does not name.
