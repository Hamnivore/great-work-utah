## Summary

Recursion Pharmaceuticals is a Salt Lake City–based biotech that uses computer vision and machine learning on millions of cell images to discover drugs. Founded in 2013 by Chris Gibson, Dean Li, and Blake Borgeson; spun out of the University of Utah.

## Why It Matters

Recursion is the first credible deep-tech company to come out of Utah at scale. It treats biology as a search problem, runs that search at industrial scale, and partners with Bayer, Roche, Genentech, and Sanofi for shots at major therapeutic areas. The platform is the product; the drugs are the proof.

## What They Are Building

A "phenomics" pipeline: stain cells, image them at scale, label the resulting morphology with deep learning, and use the resulting embedding as the basis for finding drugs. By 2024 the company had over 23 petabytes of biological imaging data and was running multiple clinical trials sourced from the platform.

## Utah Context

Recursion's existence — and its 2021 IPO — answered a question that had been open for two decades: could Utah produce a deep-tech company at NASDAQ scale, not just a SaaS one? The answer is yes. The downstream effect on Utah's bio ecosystem (talent staying, investors paying attention, university spinouts feeling like a real path) is the larger story.

## What They Need Now

Talent in the unusual gap between ML engineers and bench biologists. Capital partnerships that can absorb the long cycles of drug development without spooking public-market investors. A second hit clinical program to back up the platform thesis.

## Who Could Help

- Clinical operators experienced in rare-disease trials.
- Capital partners patient enough for biotech timelines.
