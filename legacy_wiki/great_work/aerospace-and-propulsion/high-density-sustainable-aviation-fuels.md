# High-Density Sustainable Aviation Fuels

**Speculative tier:** P-B - strategically important if the fuel qualifies, scales, and actually beats incumbent fuel pathways on cost and performance
**Domain:** Aerospace & Propulsion
**Type:** Sustainable aviation fuel / advanced aerospace propellants / biofuel process
**Era:** 2023-present
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT

## What it is

High-density sustainable aviation fuel is the attempt to make drop-in aviation and aerospace fuels from biomass residues while preserving or improving vehicle performance. CleanJoule, headquartered in Salt Lake City, is developing CycloSAF, a fuel pathway based on agricultural and forestry residue and other biomass feedstocks.

The company frames the technical target as more than low-carbon compliance. It argues that the fuel can have higher energy density than typical Jet A, reduce soot, use flexible feedstocks, and avoid the blend limits that constrain some existing SAF pathways.

In 2026, CycloKinetics launched as a dedicated aerospace and defense propellant company around the same broader Utah advanced-fuels family. Its public product line includes CycloJP for aircraft fuels, CycloRP for RP-1/RP-2-class liquid rocket fuels, and CK-10 for JP-10-class missile fuel. The relationship is best treated as one entry for now: CleanJoule appears to be the civil aviation face, while CycloKinetics is the defense and space face of high-energy-density, drop-in hydrocarbon propellants.

## Why this might become great work

Long-haul aviation is hard to electrify. Batteries are too heavy for many large-aircraft missions, and hydrogen aviation faces infrastructure and aircraft-design barriers. Drop-in liquid fuels are therefore one of the most plausible routes for deep aviation decarbonization.

If a Utah-developed pathway can produce cost-competitive, high-performance, high-substitution SAF from abundant residues, it would matter globally. Aviation needs enormous fuel volumes; small percentage improvements in energy density, feedstock yield, or blend compatibility could have large system effects.

The defense and space version of the same thesis is performance rather than carbon. Higher-energy-density fuels can extend aircraft endurance, reduce tanker dependence, increase missile range, or improve launch payload without changing vehicle geometry. Those claims are strategically large, but they need independent test data because the strongest numbers are still company-reported.

## Signals so far

- CleanJoule is headquartered in Salt Lake City
- In 2023, the company announced a $50 million investment led by Indigo Partners, with participation from GenZero, Cleanhill Partners, Frontier Airlines, Wizz Air, and Volaris
- The three airline investors signed binding agreements to purchase up to 90 million gallons of SAF
- CleanJoule says CycloSAF is in the ASTM International qualification process
- The company describes a two-step process using flexible biomass feedstocks and a proprietary biocatalyst
- CleanJoule commissioned its CJ1 industrial manufacturing facility in Salt Lake City in 2025 for CycloSAF and SpaceSAF production
- CleanJoule reported that CycloSAF delivers roughly 10 percent greater energy density than Jet A, while SpaceSAF delivers roughly 4 percent greater energy density than super-refined kerosene rocket fuels
- CycloKinetics launched publicly in May 2026 as a dedicated defense and space propellant company headquartered in Salt Lake City
- CycloKinetics says its Salt Lake City industrial manufacturing facility became operational in 2025 and that deliveries under defense contracts began in Q2 2025
- CycloKinetics describes military testing across more than ten applications, including ground and flight tests on fielded systems
- Flying reported Mukund Karanjikar's explanation that CleanJoule and CycloKinetics products are distinct but share feedstock, facility, and technical synergies; CleanJoule now serves civil aviation while CycloKinetics targets aerospace and defense customers

## What would upgrade this

- ASTM qualification and commercial airline use
- Independent lifecycle carbon analysis and engine-performance validation
- A commercial-scale plant producing meaningful fuel volumes
- Proof that feedstock logistics, hydrogen use, yield, and cost work outside pilot scale
- Named platform tests, DoD contract records, independent engine/rocket tests, or published fuel-characterization data for CycloJP, CycloRP, and CK-10
- A launch, flight, or fleet deployment where the fuel's range, endurance, payload, soot, cold-weather, or maintenance claims are measured by someone other than the company

## Caveats

- SAF pathways often look strong before feedstock logistics and refinery economics arrive
- Airline offtake and investment are signals, not proof of scalable production
- Higher energy density claims need independent engine and certification validation
- Biomass availability is real but not unlimited; residue removal can have soil, land-use, and competing-demand constraints
- Defense and missile-fuel claims are harder to audit because many customer tests and contracts may remain confidential
- If CycloKinetics' strongest performance claims depend on special platform contexts, the world-scale impact could be narrower than the launch rhetoric suggests
