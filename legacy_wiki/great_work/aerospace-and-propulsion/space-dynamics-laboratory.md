# Space Dynamics Laboratory — Utah State University

**Tier:** A — 420+ space missions; built sensor payloads on landmark NASA science spacecraft; one of the most prolific satellite and space instrumentation labs in U.S. history
**Domain:** Aerospace & Propulsion
**Type:** University research lab / UARC
**Era:** 1959–present
**Location:** North Logan, UT (Utah State University)

## What it is

The Space Dynamics Laboratory (SDL) is a University-Affiliated Research Center (UARC) operated by Utah State University. Founded in 1959, SDL designs and builds electro-optical sensors, small satellites, and real-time data systems for defense and civilian space missions. It has completed over 420 space missions and deployed over 500 independent hardware and software systems into orbit.

SDL's particular strength is space-based remote sensing — infrared and electro-optical instruments that look down at Earth (for intelligence, weather, and Earth science) and out at the universe (for astronomy and astrophysics). With nearly 1,400 employees, it is the largest employer in Cache Valley and among the largest space research organizations in the intermountain West.

Notable missions and instruments:
- **WISE / NEOWISE** — SDL built and tested the Wide-field Infrared Survey Explorer telescope payload for NASA; launched 2009; mapped the entire sky in infrared; discovered the NEOWISE comet in 2020 and has since tracked thousands of near-Earth asteroids
- **NEO Surveyor** — SDL is integrating and testing NASA's first purpose-built infrared space telescope for detecting potentially hazardous near-Earth asteroids and comets
- **AWE (Atmospheric Waves Experiment)** — SDL built the ISS-mounted instrument for a USU-led NASA mission studying atmospheric gravity waves through airglow; first public data release covered 3,000 orbits in 2025
- **MSX (Midcourse Space Experiment)** — ballistic missile defense sensing; defined the infrared background of space for missile warning systems
- **Multiple DoD space surveillance and missile warning payloads** — SDL sensors are on operational defense satellites whose specific capabilities are classified
- **Small satellite constellation work** — SDL has become a leading builder of small satellites (6U–12U cubesats and ESPA-class spacecraft) for both government and research missions

## Why it mattered

SDL built the research capability to do precision space sensing at a regional university in a state without a major aerospace hub. Over six decades it has trained thousands of engineers and scientists, produced operational hardware that is on orbit today, and established Utah as a credible participant in national space and defense programs. The WISE telescope alone produced a catalog of over 500 million objects that transformed astrophysics research.

## Key people

- **Will Clayton** — SDL's founding director; established the lab's space sensing focus
- **Jan Sojka** — long-serving director; built up the civilian science program alongside the defense work

## Caveats

- SDL is primarily a defense contractor; most of its significant work is classified and cannot be publicly assessed
