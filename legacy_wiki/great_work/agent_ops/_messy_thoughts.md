## Fresh Leads

### Safeguarded Pyrochemical Processing of Used Nuclear Fuel / University of Utah

- **Lead:** University of Utah ARPA-E CURIE project on pyrochemical dissolution of light-water-reactor used nuclear fuel with actinide recovery for advanced reactors.
- **Work focus:** Dissolve oxide used nuclear fuel in molten LiCl-KCl salt and electrochemically recover uranium/transuranic metal in a process designed around safeguards and fewer handling steps.
- **Why it might matter:** Advanced reactors need fuel supply chains and credible waste strategies. A compact, safeguarded pyrochemical pathway could help close the fuel cycle without pure plutonium streams or sprawling conventional reprocessing infrastructure.
- **Utah tie:** University of Utah, Salt Lake City; ARPA-E lists a roughly $1.45M project.
- **Why this is messy / not yet a wiki entry:** This is still a funded research plan, not a demonstrated process. The "first" claim is conditional on success, and nuclear reprocessing carries policy, proliferation, waste, and licensing burdens.
- **Signals so far:** ARPA-E CURIE project description; explicit input-accountability/safeguards framing; molten-salt/electrochemical process concept aligned with advanced reactor fuel-cycle needs.
- **Possible tier:** P-C now; P-B if bench-scale process data are published with safeguards/accountancy performance.
- **Upgrade evidence:** Demonstrated oxide dissolution and U/TRU recovery, material-accountancy data, waste-form analysis, proliferation-resistance review, independent lab replication, and a reactor-fuel qualification pathway.
- **Sources / search terms:** `ARPA-E CURIE University of Utah pyrochemical dissolution used nuclear fuel`, `LiCl KCl U/TRU recovery University of Utah`, `University of Utah CURIE used nuclear fuel`.
- **Status:** promoted to `industry-and-infrastructure/safeguarded-pyrochemical-used-fuel-recycling.md`

### Native Long-Read Epigenomic Sequencing / Wasatch BioLabs

- **Lead:** Wasatch BioLabs, a Utah long-read sequencing service provider, is pushing direct whole/targeted methylome sequencing using native Oxford Nanopore reads.
- **Work focus:** PCR- and bisulfite-free genomic/epigenomic assays that preserve methylation, structural variation, phasing, repeat expansions, and other long-read context in one workflow.
- **Why it might matter:** If targeted native-read methylation becomes routine, it could make liquid biopsy, rare-disease, pharmacogenomic, prenatal, and translational biomarker work less lossy than short-read or bisulfite-heavy approaches.
- **Utah tie:** Salt Lake City / Pleasant Grove / Heber City operations; Utah company positioning itself as a large U.S. long-read sequencing specialist.
- **Why this is messy / not yet a wiki entry:** The signal is promising but still reads like service-provider capability plus early benchmarking. Need independent users, publications, or clinical/research programs showing that the Utah workflow changes outcomes or discovery quality.
- **Signals so far:** Agilent co-marketing agreement for dTMS; Oxford Nanopore certified provider listing; company-reported dTMS enrichment/accuracy benchmarks; 2025 expansion into pharmacogenomics, telomere sequencing, and mRNA vaccine QC assays.
- **Possible tier:** P-C now; P-B if independent adoption and validation appear.
- **Upgrade evidence:** Peer-reviewed method paper, external benchmark against bisulfite/short-read/whole-genome baselines, named biopharma or health-system adoption, CLIA/LDT clinical assay translation, or large cohort studies powered by the workflow.
- **Sources / search terms:** `Wasatch BioLabs dTMS Agilent`, `Wasatch BioLabs direct targeted methylation sequencing`, `Wasatch BioLabs Oxford Nanopore certified provider`.
- **Status:** promoted to `medicine-and-biology/native-long-read-epigenomic-sequencing.md`

### Voice Biomarkers for Multiple Sclerosis / Canary Speech + Intermountain

- **Lead:** Canary Speech and Intermountain Ventures launched an IRB-approved study to identify multiple sclerosis using vocal biomarkers.
- **Work focus:** AI analysis of subtle speech patterns as a screening or monitoring signal for MS and other cognitive/behavioral/neurologic conditions.
- **Why it might matter:** Voice is cheap, frequent, and remote. A robust vocal biomarker could help screen, monitor progression, or flag relapse without expensive imaging every time.
- **Utah tie:** Provo company; study with Intermountain Ventures / Intermountain Health.
- **Why this is messy / not yet a wiki entry:** Vocal biomarkers are noisy and easy to overclaim. The current signal is an announced study, not validated diagnostic performance.
- **Signals so far:** 2026 Utah Business announcement; IRB-approved MS study; Canary Ambient product positioning in healthcare/contact-center workflows.
- **Possible tier:** P-C if trial details are verified; P-B only with published performance and clinical workflow adoption.
- **Upgrade evidence:** Trial registration, peer-reviewed diagnostic/monitoring performance, independent validation across demographics/devices/noise conditions, and clinician workflow adoption.
- **Sources / search terms:** `Canary Speech Intermountain multiple sclerosis vocal biomarkers`, `Canary Speech IRB MS study`, `Canary Ambient clinical decision support`.
- **Status:** promoted to broader `medicine-and-biology/ai-voice-biomarker-health-screening.md`
