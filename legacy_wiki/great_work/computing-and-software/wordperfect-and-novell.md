# WordPerfect & Novell

**Tier:** A — both were #1 in their markets globally; defined an era of computing from Utah
**Domain:** Computing & Software
**Type:** Companies
**Era:** WordPerfect: 1979–1996; Novell: 1983–2000s
**Location:** Orem, UT (WordPerfect); Provo, UT (Novell)

## What it was

Two of the dominant personal computing software companies of the 1980s and early 1990s were headquartered within 10 miles of each other in Utah County.

**WordPerfect** was developed by Satellite Software International (later renamed WordPerfect Corporation) in Orem. Founded by Alan Ashton and Bruce Bastian, it became the dominant word processor on DOS and mainframes — at its peak in the late 1980s, WordPerfect 5.1 had a majority market share among PC word processors globally. Its "Reveal Codes" feature and keystroke-driven interface defined professional document creation before the mouse era.

**Novell NetWare** was the dominant network operating system of the 1980s and early 1990s, enabling the local-area networking that made office computing practical. At its peak, NetWare ran on roughly 70% of installed office networks worldwide. Novell also acquired UnixWare and UNIX System Laboratories (from AT&T) in 1993, briefly owning the UNIX trademark.

## Why it mattered

Before WordPerfect, word processing on PCs was primitive. WordPerfect demonstrated that serious professional software could be built in Utah — outside of Silicon Valley — and supported. It established the template for Utah's software industry.

Before Novell, PCs were isolated machines. NetWare made them cooperative: shared file servers, shared printers, centralized IT administration. Novell's networking infrastructure directly enabled the growth of office computing in the pre-internet era.

## Lasting impact

- WordPerfect's legal vertical (Corel WordPerfect) still commands loyalty in U.S. law firms; courts accept WordPerfect format; Corel still ships it
- Novell's Directory Services (NDS) was the predecessor concept to Microsoft Active Directory, which controls identity management in most enterprise networks today
- Both companies built a generation of Utah software engineers and executives who seeded the later "Silicon Slopes" startup ecosystem
- Alan Ashton's wealth funded the Thanksgiving Point complex in Lehi, a major science/culture campus

## Key people

- **Alan Ashton** — WordPerfect co-founder; BYU computer science professor who started the project as a contract for the city of Orem
- **Bruce Bastian** — WordPerfect co-founder; graduate student who built the first version
- **Ray Noorda** — Novell CEO 1983–1994; turned a failing diskless workstation company into the networking infrastructure giant
- **Drew Major** — lead developer of NetWare; the core networking architecture is largely his work

## Caveats

- Both companies lost their dominant positions in the mid-1990s to Microsoft — WordPerfect to Word, NetWare to Windows NT Server. The transition was abrupt; neither recovered their former market positions
- Novell's acquisition of UNIX in 1993 was a distraction that consumed resources and management attention during the critical Windows NT transition
- WordPerfect was sold to Novell in 1994, then to Corel in 1996; the Utah chapter of the story ends at the acquisition
