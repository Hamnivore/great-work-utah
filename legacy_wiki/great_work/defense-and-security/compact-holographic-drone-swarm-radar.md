# Compact Holographic Drone-Swarm Radar

**Speculative tier:** P-B - credible defense-tech signal with public product details, but still needs independent operational validation against real swarm threats
**Domain:** Defense & Security
**Type:** Counter-UAS radar / compact surveillance sensor
**Era:** 2026-present
**Location:** Orem, UT

## What it is

Spotter Global's GAX500-3D is a compact counter-drone radar built around what the company calls Compact Holographic 3D radar. Instead of scanning a narrow beam across the sky, the system is designed to observe a full volume continuously and update target positions at 18 Hz, or every 56 milliseconds.

The Utah-specific work is design, manufacturing, assembly, and software coordination from Spotter Global's Orem headquarters. The company has long focused on small, low-power surveillance radar for military force protection and critical infrastructure.

## Why this might become great work

Drone attacks are becoming cheaper, faster, lower-flying, and more numerous. Detection is often the bottleneck: interceptors, jammers, or directed-energy systems need fast, precise tracks before they can do anything useful. A radar that is light enough for mobile units, low-power enough for field deployment, and fast enough for small swarms could become a core counter-UAS layer.

The important idea is not a single sensor SKU. It is continuous, low-SWaP, three-dimensional airspace awareness for environments where traditional radars are too heavy, too slow, or too power-hungry.

## Signals so far

- Spotter announced the GAX500-3D in March 2026 with claimed 18 Hz full-volume updates
- The company reports tracking up to 50 simultaneous targets and detecting 3 kg drones at up to 500 meters
- Public product details emphasize less than 4 kg system weight, less than 35 W power consumption, and 90 by 90 degree coverage per panel
- Spotter says its compact radars, Remote Drone ID, NetworkedIO C2, and integrated management software are designed and manufactured from Orem
- The product received a Security Industry Association 2026 New Product Award in the countermeasures to drone and robot threats category

## What would upgrade this

- Independent test data against fast FPV drones, coordinated swarms, low-RCS drones, and cluttered urban backgrounds
- Integration into deployed counter-UAS systems with real incident performance
- Procurement by defense, critical infrastructure, or major-event security customers
- Comparative field trials showing better warning time or lower false-alert burden than competing radar/optical/acoustic systems
- Evidence that multiple-panel deployments maintain performance under jamming, weather, terrain masking, and dense RF environments

## Caveats

- Most performance claims are company-reported
- A 500 m range may be powerful for mobile/local defense but is not enough by itself for wide-area air defense
- Detection does not solve classification, authority-to-engage, or mitigation
- Defense adoption can be shaped by procurement channels and integration politics as much as raw technical merit
