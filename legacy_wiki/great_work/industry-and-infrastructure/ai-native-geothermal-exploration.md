# AI-Native Geothermal Exploration

**Speculative tier:** P-A - could become a major clean-energy discovery method if the field results repeat and convert into operating power plants
**Domain:** Industry & Infrastructure
**Type:** Geothermal exploration / computational geoscience
**Era:** 2021-present
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT; western U.S. field sites

## What it is

AI-native geothermal exploration is the attempt to make geothermal prospecting less like wildcat drilling and more like a repeatable, data-driven development process. Zanskar is the clearest Utah example: a Salt Lake City company combining machine learning, geophysics, field geology, drilling data, and project development to find hidden geothermal systems across the western U.S.

The important claim is not just "AI for energy." It is that natural geothermal resources may be much larger than the visible hot-spring map suggests, if a developer can find blind systems with no obvious surface expression and drill fewer dry holes.

## Why this might become great work

Geothermal has a beautiful energy profile: firm, clean, compact, and available day and night. Its historical bottleneck has been discovery risk. If Utah-built exploration software and field methods can lower that risk, geothermal could become a larger part of the grid without waiting for every project to use engineered reservoirs.

The potentially world-scale version of this work is a repeatable prospecting machine for hidden heat. That would matter for data centers, industrial loads, utilities that need clean firm power, and regions where solar and wind need a dispatchable companion.

## Signals so far

- Zanskar operates from Salt Lake City and describes itself as an AI-native geothermal company
- The company reports turning around the Lightning Dock geothermal project in New Mexico
- In 2025, Zanskar announced Big Blind, a blind geothermal discovery in Nevada with no surface manifestations or prior well data
- The company also announced multiple recent discoveries, including Pumpernickel and Big Blind in Nevada
- In 2026, Zanskar raised a $115 million Series C and then a $40 million development-capital facility structured to scale to $100 million
- A 2026 GEODE agreement with California Community Power gives Zanskar a utility-linked path to prospect for undiscovered geothermal resources in California

## What would upgrade this

- Multiple AI-sited geothermal discoveries reach commercial operation
- Independent confirmation that the method materially reduces dry-hole rates or exploration cost
- Utilities or project financiers treat AI-discovered greenfield geothermal as a financeable asset class
- Replication outside Zanskar's first western U.S. portfolio

## Caveats

- Discovery announcements are not the same as producing power
- Geothermal projects still face permitting, transmission, drilling, reservoir, and financing risk
- Much of the performance evidence is company-reported
- The strongest historical comparison may be oil-and-gas exploration, which means the method could become important without being as clean or simple as the story sounds
