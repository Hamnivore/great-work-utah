# Commercial-Scale Enhanced Geothermal at Cape Station

**Speculative tier:** P-A - one of the strongest Utah-based candidates for future S-tier clean-energy infrastructure; still needs first power and sustained commercial performance
**Domain:** Industry & Infrastructure
**Type:** Enhanced geothermal deployment / clean firm power infrastructure
**Era:** 2023-present
**Location:** Beaver County, UT

## What it is

Cape Station is a utility-scale enhanced geothermal system (EGS) development in Beaver County, near Utah FORGE. The specific work is turning oil-and-gas-style horizontal drilling, reservoir stimulation, fiber-optic sensing, and modular surface power generation into a bankable geothermal power plant rather than a one-off research demonstration.

The project is being developed by Fervo Energy, which is based in Houston, but the work belongs in this wiki because the flagship commercial-scale field development, construction, wells, financing milestone, and planned power delivery are in Utah.

## Why this might become great work

Clean electricity systems need firm power: generation that can run when the wind is not blowing and the sun is down. If Cape Station proves that EGS can be financed, built, and operated at 100-500 MW scale, it would move geothermal from promising demonstration into infrastructure category.

The Utah-specific importance is the pairing of DOE's Utah FORGE research field lab with a nearby private commercial development. That makes Beaver County one of the world's most important tests of whether next-generation geothermal can leave the lab and scale.

## Signals so far

- Cape Station is planned as a 500 MW next-generation geothermal development in southwest Utah
- Phase I is expected to begin delivering power in 2026 and reach roughly 100 MW of capacity by early 2027
- In March 2026, Fervo closed a $421 million non-recourse project financing package for Phase I
- Power purchase agreements include Shell Energy and Southern California Edison/other California buyers
- Baker Hughes and Turboden have announced equipment awards for Cape Station power generation
- The Sugarloaf appraisal well reached a projected bottomhole temperature around 500 F and set internal drilling records for Fervo

## What would upgrade this

- First power delivered to the grid
- 100 MW operating capacity reached with high uptime and predictable decline curves
- Expansion to 500 MW without major induced-seismicity, water, permitting, or reservoir-performance problems
- Financing and engineering model copied by other EGS projects
- Evidence that costs fall enough for geothermal to compete as clean firm power

## Caveats

- As of this entry, the project is not yet a proven operating power plant
- EGS reservoir performance, induced seismicity, drilling cost, water management, and long-term thermal decline remain the hard tests
- Attribution should stay precise: Fervo is not Utah-based, but the commercial field development is in Utah and builds on the local geothermal corridor
