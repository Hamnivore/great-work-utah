# Direct Lithium Extraction from Great Salt Lake Brines

**Speculative tier:** P-B - technically promising and directly tied to a world-scale environmental constraint, but commercial and ecological proof is still ahead
**Domain:** Industry & Infrastructure
**Type:** Critical-minerals extraction / environmental technology
**Era:** 2024-present
**Location:** Great Salt Lake, UT

## What it is

Direct lithium extraction (DLE) from Great Salt Lake brines is the attempt to recover lithium without relying on conventional evaporation ponds that consume water and expand land disturbance. The work matters because the lake is both a potential critical-minerals resource and an ecosystem under severe hydrological stress.

The most visible recent field work is Lilac Solutions' ion-exchange pilot on the lake. The company is not Utah-based, but the work belongs in this wiki because the pilot, regulatory problem, brine chemistry, environmental stakes, and possible commercial facility are all specific to Great Salt Lake.

## Why this might become great work

If Utah can demonstrate commercially meaningful lithium production without lowering lake levels or damaging lake chemistry and biota, it would be a globally important model: critical-mineral extraction under ecological constraint. That is a much harder and more interesting problem than "mine more lithium."

Great Salt Lake brine is difficult: low lithium concentration compared with major South American salars, high magnesium-to-lithium ratio, sensitive discharge requirements, and intense public scrutiny. A working process here would say something real about DLE's ability to unlock unconventional resources.

## Signals so far

- A seven-month Great Salt Lake pilot processed more than one million gallons of brine
- Reported average lithium recovery was 87% from roughly 69 mg/L lithium brine
- Reported overall impurity rejection was 99.97%
- The process returns lithium-depleted brine rather than depending on evaporation ponds
- The first proposed commercial phase targets 5,000 tonnes per year of lithium carbonate equivalent, with a possible expansion to 20,000 tonnes per year
- Utah's 2024 mineral-extraction law pushed the regulatory frame toward non-consumptive extraction methods

## What would upgrade this

- Commercial production of battery-grade lithium carbonate from Great Salt Lake brine
- Independent ecological monitoring showing no material harm to lake chemistry, biota, or water levels
- Binding offtake and project finance reaching construction
- A repeatable regulatory model that other saline-lake jurisdictions copy

## Caveats

- The most complete performance numbers are company-reported
- "Non-consumptive" still needs careful verification across full operations, reagents, waste streams, and discharge chemistry
- Lithium markets are volatile; a good technical process can still fail commercially
- The lake's larger crisis is water depletion from upstream diversion; DLE does not solve that by itself
