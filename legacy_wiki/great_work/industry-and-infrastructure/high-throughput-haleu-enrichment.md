# High-Throughput HALEU Enrichment

**Speculative tier:** P-B - nationally important if the production claims hold, but still awaiting commercial fuel delivery and regulatory proof
**Domain:** Industry & Infrastructure
**Type:** Nuclear fuel supply chain / isotope separation
**Era:** 2025-present
**Location:** West Valley City, UT

## What it is

High-throughput HALEU enrichment is the attempt to produce high-assay low-enriched uranium at commercial scale for advanced nuclear reactors. HALEU is uranium enriched above ordinary light-water-reactor fuel levels but below weapons-grade material; many small modular and advanced reactor designs depend on it.

The Utah case is Nusano's West Valley City program. Nusano is already in the wiki for multiplexed medical radioisotope production. This is a separate speculative thread: using the company's mass-separation and physics platform to attack the nuclear fuel bottleneck rather than the medical isotope bottleneck.

## Why this might become great work

Advanced reactors do not matter if they cannot get fuel. U.S. HALEU supply is thin, strategically sensitive, and tangled in legacy enrichment infrastructure. If a Utah facility can produce HALEU domestically at meaningful scale, it could unlock reactor demonstrations that otherwise stall for lack of fuel.

The great-work version is supply-chain infrastructure: boring, regulated, repeatable, and trusted enough that advanced reactor developers can plan around it. It would also make Utah part of the physical stack behind AI-era power demand, not just the software or data-center layer.

## Signals so far

- Nusano announced the HALEU program in June 2025 from West Valley City, Utah
- The company says the process avoids uranium hexafluoride, a bottleneck and hazard in conventional fuel cycles
- Nusano claims a single system could produce more than 50 metric tons of HALEU annually, with a target of about 350 metric tons annually by 2029
- The company says initial commercial samples are planned for Q4 2026 and large-scale production for Q1 2027
- The HALEU work is tied to Nusano's broader mass-separation platform, which also supports radioisotopes, stable isotope enrichment, and critical-mineral refining
- Utah officials have connected the work to Operation Gigawatt and possible future use at the Utah San Rafael Energy Lab

## What would upgrade this

- Delivery of commercial-grade HALEU samples that meet reactor-developer and regulatory specifications
- Independent confirmation of enrichment performance, throughput, energy use, cost, safeguards, and environmental claims
- A licensed production pathway with nuclear-material accounting and security requirements satisfied
- Named advanced-reactor customers receiving fuel under durable supply agreements

## Caveats

- The largest claims are company-reported and forward-looking
- Nuclear fuel production is regulated, security-sensitive, and politically exposed
- HALEU supply is a real bottleneck, but solving it requires safeguards and trust as much as technical throughput
- Nusano's platform could be important even if the exact 2027-2029 scale targets slip
