# AI-Assisted Digital Pathology Workflows

**Speculative tier:** P-B - meaningful clinical workflow signal with real partners, but broad medical impact depends on regulated adoption and diagnostic performance
**Domain:** Medicine & Biology
**Type:** Digital pathology platform / AI-assisted diagnostics
**Era:** 2013-present
**Location:** Orem, UT

## What it is

Techcyte Fusion is an AI-enabled digital pathology platform that tries to unify anatomic and clinical pathology workflows: slide image management, cloud viewing, worklists, AI analysis, LIS/EHR integration, reporting, and partner algorithms in one browser-based system. The work is not simply "AI diagnoses disease"; it is building the practical workflow layer that lets labs digitize microscopy-heavy work and use AI as an assistive diagnostic tool.

The Utah-specific work is Techcyte's Orem headquarters and long-running platform development across clinical pathology, veterinary diagnostics, environmental testing, and anatomic pathology.

## Why this might become great work

Pathology is a bottleneck in healthcare. Labs face staffing shortages, rising test volumes, inconsistent manual review, and fragmented software. If AI-assisted digital pathology becomes a normal operating layer for labs, it could shorten turnaround time, standardize review, support remote expertise, and let scarce pathologists and technologists focus on the cases that need human judgment most.

Techcyte is interesting because it is not only chasing one flashy foundation model. It is trying to make digital pathology operational across many specimen types and lab workflows, with AI tools embedded where they can be reviewed and reported.

## Signals so far

- Techcyte was founded in Orem in 2013 and has commercial roots in AI image analysis for cellular and liquid-based microscopy
- The company has collaborated with ARUP Laboratories, Zoetis, BD, Mayo Clinic Platform, and multiple AI pathology partners
- Mayo Clinic Platform and Techcyte announced a strategic collaboration in 2024 to build an open, end-to-end AI-driven digital pathology platform
- In 2025, Techcyte launched Fusion as an open, standards-based SaaS platform intended to unify anatomic and clinical pathology workflows
- Techcyte reports CE-IVD / CE-IVDR status for parts of its platform in Europe while U.S. uses remain research-use-only or application-specific
- In April 2026, Techcyte announced a $15 million capital raise led by Van Tuyl Companies with participation from Zoetis and Mayo Clinic

## What would upgrade this

- FDA-cleared or otherwise regulator-authorized AI-assisted workflows in high-volume human diagnostics
- Published evidence of improved turnaround time, accuracy, reproducibility, or labor efficiency in real clinical labs
- Durable adoption across large health systems or reference labs, not only pilots
- Strong performance on edge cases, rare findings, scanner variability, and diverse patient populations
- Clear clinical governance showing how AI findings are reviewed, corrected, audited, and improved

## Caveats

- Digital pathology adoption is constrained by regulation, reimbursement, scanner cost, data integration, and pathologist trust
- Many claims are platform and partner claims, not independent clinical outcome studies
- "AI-assisted" can mean very different levels of autonomy; Techcyte's current strength appears to be workflow augmentation rather than unsupervised diagnosis
- Competition from PathAI, Paige, Proscia, Aiforia, Deep Bio, and large health-system platforms is intense
