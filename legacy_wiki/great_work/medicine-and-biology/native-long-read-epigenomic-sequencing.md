# Native Long-Read Epigenomic Sequencing

**Speculative tier:** P-C - technically interesting Utah genomics infrastructure, but still mostly a service-platform signal rather than proven field-changing work
**Domain:** Medicine & Biology
**Type:** Genomics service platform / epigenomic assay infrastructure
**Era:** 2022-present
**Location:** Salt Lake City / Pleasant Grove / Heber City, UT

## What it is

Native long-read epigenomic sequencing is the attempt to read genetic variation and DNA methylation from the same long DNA molecules without bisulfite conversion or PCR amplification. Wasatch BioLabs, a Utah long-read sequencing provider, offers direct whole methylome sequencing, direct targeted methylation sequencing, cell-free DNA methylation workflows, direct RNA sequencing, pharmacogenomics panels, telomere sequencing, and related Oxford Nanopore-based services.

The specific Utah signal is not "long-read sequencing exists." It is a service-lab and assay-development push around native-read methylation workflows: preserving methylation and long-range haplotype context while using targeted enrichment or whole-methylome reads to make epigenomic studies more practical for translational research.

## Why this might become great work

Epigenomic biomarkers are attractive for cancer, neurodegeneration, reproductive biology, aging, pharmacogenomics, and rare disease, but older methylation methods force tradeoffs. Bisulfite conversion damages DNA and erases other context; short reads can miss structural variants, phasing, repeats, and molecule-level methylation patterns.

If native long-read methylome workflows become routine in clinical research or diagnostics, they could make biomarker discovery less lossy and make targeted follow-up assays easier to translate. A Utah lab that packages these methods into repeatable, regulated, high-throughput workflows could become useful scientific infrastructure even if the underlying sequencing chemistry is global.

## Signals so far

- Wasatch BioLabs operates in Utah and describes itself as focused on advanced genomics, transcriptomics, and epigenetics
- Oxford Nanopore lists Wasatch BioLabs as a certified sequencing service provider for DNA, RNA, and PromethION workflows
- Wasatch BioLabs says its direct whole methylome sequencing profiles more than 27 million CpG sites without bisulfite conversion or PCR amplification
- The company describes direct targeted methylation sequencing that combines modified Agilent SureSelect or Avida enrichment with native Oxford Nanopore sequencing
- In December 2025, Wasatch BioLabs announced a co-marketing agreement with Agilent to support adoption of its direct targeted methylation sequencing platform for research-use-only and clinical research studies
- In October 2025, the company announced additional Oxford Nanopore-developed research-use-only assays for pharmacogenomics, telomere sequencing, and mRNA vaccine quality-control workflows
- Oxford Nanopore's provider listing gives a Heber City, Utah address and lists custom targeted methylation sequencing, whole genome sequencing, direct RNA sequencing, cDNA sequencing, and PCR amplicon sequencing as offered techniques

## What would upgrade this

- Peer-reviewed method papers or external benchmarks showing reproducible gains over bisulfite, array, short-read, or standard long-read baselines
- Named academic, biopharma, or health-system studies where the Utah workflow enables discoveries that would likely have been missed otherwise
- Clinical validation of a native-read methylation assay with clear sensitivity, specificity, reproducibility, turnaround, and cost data
- CLIA/LDT or regulator-facing diagnostic deployments where long-read native methylation is necessary rather than merely nice to have
- Open or reusable analysis tooling that makes the method more broadly accessible outside Wasatch BioLabs' service business

## Caveats

- Oxford Nanopore, Agilent, and many non-Utah labs are central contributors; the Utah claim is about workflow packaging, service infrastructure, and possible assay development
- The strongest performance claims are currently company-reported or announcement-based
- Service-provider growth is not the same as field-changing scientific impact
- Clinical methylation assays face hard validation, reimbursement, privacy, and regulatory barriers
- If short-read, array, or lower-cost targeted methods are good enough for most clinical tasks, this may remain a useful niche rather than great work
