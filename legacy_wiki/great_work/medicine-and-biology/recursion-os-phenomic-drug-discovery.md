# Recursion OS - Phenomic Drug Discovery at Scale

**Speculative tier:** P-A - one of the strongest Utah candidates for future A/S-tier work, but the platform still has to prove it can reliably produce approved medicines
**Domain:** Medicine & Biology
**Type:** AI-enabled drug discovery platform / biological dataset
**Era:** 2013-present
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT

## What it is

The Recursion OS is an industrialized drug-discovery platform built around high-throughput cellular experiments, automated microscopy, phenomic image analysis, chemical biology, patient data, and large-scale AI models. The core work is not "a biotech company exists in Utah"; it is the attempt to turn biology into a searchable, learnable map by repeatedly perturbing cells, imaging the results, and training models on the resulting biological signatures.

The recent speculative signal is the platform's scale. Recursion built BioHive-2, an NVIDIA DGX H100 supercomputer in Salt Lake City, and has used its data and compute to train phenomic foundation models such as the Phenom family. After combining with Exscientia in 2024, the platform added stronger chemistry design and synthesis loops, making the work closer to an end-to-end drug-discovery operating system.

## Why this might become great work

If this approach works, it could change the economics of drug discovery: fewer purely intuition-driven screens, faster target discovery, better compound prioritization, and more shots on goal for diseases where conventional discovery has been slow or biased toward already-understood biology.

The Utah-specific point is that a meaningful part of this platform - the wet-lab automation, phenomics data, headquarters compute, and operating culture - was built in Salt Lake City rather than imported after the fact.

## Signals so far

- BioHive-2 ranked #35 on the TOP500 list in May 2024 and was described by Recursion and NVIDIA as the largest supercomputer in the pharmaceutical industry at the time
- The platform has accumulated tens of petabytes of biological, chemical, and patient-centric data
- Recursion has major partnerships with large pharmaceutical companies, including Roche/Genentech and Bayer
- In 2025, MIT and Recursion released Boltz-2, an open-source biomolecular model combining structure and binding-affinity prediction
- Recursion reported more than $500 million in upfront and milestone payments across partnerships by late 2025

## What would upgrade this

- Multiple internally discovered or platform-discovered drugs reaching approval
- Clear evidence that the platform reduces discovery time, failure rate, or cost versus comparable traditional programs
- Independent adoption of Recursion-trained models or datasets as field infrastructure
- A strong clinical readout where the biological hypothesis was discovered primarily through the platform

## Caveats

- AI drug discovery is still full of beautiful demos that do not survive clinical reality
- Many claims are company-reported, and the strongest proof will be clinical, not computational
- The Exscientia combination makes attribution less clean: future success may come from a multi-site platform, not only Utah
