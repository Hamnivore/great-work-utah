## Overview

This guide shows how the wiki can turn light intake into explainable match briefs. The goal is not to prove that these exact introductions should happen. The goal is to show what a trustworthy recommendation artifact looks like: what each side appears to need, why the match has signal, what evidence supports it, and what remains uncertain.

The examples below use synthetic talent profiles where private person data would normally be required. Real organization context is linked to public fact pages and source notes.

## Demo Criteria

The Nucleus brief asks for matching that accounts for skills, domain expertise, stage preference, availability, risk tolerance, mission alignment, startup sector, origin, commercialization stage, immediate needs, and funding status.

For the demo, a good match should make a specific argument:

- the person's background maps to a visible need
- the stage and risk profile are plausible
- the Utah context is not generic
- the explanation includes caveats instead of pretending certainty
- the next step is lightweight enough to fit a real connections workflow

## Why This Is More Trustworthy Than a Job Board

A job board tends to answer: "Who posted a role?" This guide answers: "Why might this person be unusually useful here, and what would we need to verify before making an intro?"

That difference matters for Nucleus because many valuable matches are not open roles. They are advisors, operators, fractional executives, student interns, commercialization partners, and service providers whose fit depends on context.
