## Recommendation

A synthetic defense commercialization executive with counter-UAS, critical-infrastructure, and government-procurement experience is a credible match for [Fortem Technologies](../ventures/fortem-technologies.md).

This is not a claim that Fortem is seeking this person or that the person exists. It is a demo match showing how the wiki should explain a high-signal executive-to-deep-tech recommendation.

## What Each Side Appears To Need Or Offer

The synthetic executive offers defense sales judgment, customer discovery across public-sector buyers, program capture experience, and the ability to translate a technical counter-UAS product into buyer-specific adoption paths.

Fortem appears relevant because its public product surface spans radar, autonomous interceptors, SkyDome, defense, energy, airports, public venues, prisons, and law enforcement. That implies a commercialization challenge across several buyer types, each with different rules and urgency.

## Why The Match Makes Sense

The match is strong because the hard part is not simply "sell drones." Counter-UAS lives at the intersection of airspace law, defense procurement, infrastructure security, product liability, and manufacturing readiness. A general SaaS go-to-market executive would probably miss too much context.

A good executive match here would understand how to qualify buyers, avoid overbroad claims, navigate procurement cycles, and help the company choose where a small number of high-trust deployments could create the most signal.

## Risks And Unknowns

- Fortem's actual executive needs are not known from public evidence.
- Counter-UAS policy is still evolving, especially for civilian infrastructure.
- A commercialization executive without technical humility could create more noise than value.
- Government-contracting experience transfers only if the person understands the specific buyer and deployment environment.

## Suggested Next Step

Ask for a 20-minute scoped conversation around one question: "Which buyer segment has the clearest urgent need and the fewest policy blockers: defense, energy infrastructure, airports, or public venues?"

If the executive can frame that tradeoff intelligently, the match becomes more credible.
