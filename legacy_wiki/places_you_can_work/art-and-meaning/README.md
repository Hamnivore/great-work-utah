# Art & Meaning

New creative mediums, platforms for meaning-making at scale, and the institutions that shape what stories get told. Includes immersive storytelling (VR/AR/XR), new filmmaking forms, generative creative tools, and nonprofit or artist-owned infrastructure that shapes culture.

This domain sits uneasily next to the others — its impact is on human experience rather than physical capability — but it belongs. A civilization that solves energy, transportation, and health but tells itself impoverished stories has still failed at something. Art is not optional.

**A note on Utah:** Utah's art-and-meaning footprint is anchored by one genuinely major institution — the **Sundance Institute** — and a long tail of VR/AR production studios clustered around it. The Sundance Film Festival will hold its final Park City edition in January 2026 and move to Boulder, Colorado in 2027. The Sundance Institute itself, however, is keeping its headquarters in Park City and its artist labs at Sundance Resort. The nonprofit stays Utah-based; only the festival relocates.

## Ecosystem context

- **Sundance Film Festival** — The public-facing half of the Institute. Moving to Boulder in 2027 after 40+ years in Park City. Separate from the Institute's year-round labs and artist programs, which remain in Utah.
- **Sundance New Frontier Lab** — The Institute's program dedicated to VR, AR, and emerging-media storytelling. One of the few institutions that has taken immersive media seriously as an art form for over a decade.
- **Utah VR/AR production studios** — Frame Sixty, others clustered in Salt Lake City. Commercial VR work, not frontier storytelling research.
- **Sundance's move to Boulder** — Utah lost the festival over a mix of politics, logistics, and money; Colorado offered $34M in tax incentives. The Institute's choice to keep its labs and HQ in Utah suggests the labs are the more mission-central part of the organization.
