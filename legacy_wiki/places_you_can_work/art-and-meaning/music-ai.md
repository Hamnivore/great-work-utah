# Music AI (Moises)

**Tier:** B — 50M users, Apple App of Year, genuine creative tool; world-changing angle is indirect
**Domain:** Art & meaning — AI-powered music creation and audio tools
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Series A ($40M, January 2025; Connect Ventures / CAA+NEA, Kickstart, Samsung Next, Pelion)
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT (founders from Brazil; US HQ in Utah)
**Website:** [music.ai](https://music.ai)

## Mission

Build AI tools that empower musicians, producers, and creators — starting with source separation (isolating vocals, instruments, stems) and expanding into licensed voice cloning, music generation, and collaboration tools.

## Why it matters

Music creation has historically required expensive studios, engineers, and equipment. Moises (the flagship product) put professional-grade source separation in the hands of 50 million registered users — making remixing, learning songs by ear, practicing with backing tracks, and building new compositions accessible to anyone with a smartphone. The Apple iPad App of the Year award (2024) reflects genuine user love. The licensed voice cloning and music AI platform are the more expansive bet: building infrastructure that lets artists monetize their voice and style in the AI era, rather than having it scraped and replicated without consent. That's a real ethical question that determines whether AI empowers or displaces musicians.

## The hard problem

Source separation (separating mixed audio into individual stems) is a hard signal processing problem that required deep learning to solve well. The harder longer-term problem is music generation and voice synthesis that's musically compelling (not just technically accurate), and doing it in a way that respects and compensates original artists — a commercial, legal, and technical challenge simultaneously.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct deployment at scale: 50M users is a meaningful platform. The ethical AI framing (artists as investors, licensed voice cloning) could establish a model for the music industry that doesn't collapse music creation economics — which matters for whether music-making remains a viable career.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

ML engineers (audio, signal processing, generative models). Product engineers. People at the intersection of music and technology. Artists-in-residence or creative technologists. The Brazilian founding team culture + Utah HQ creates an international company atmosphere.

## Ownership

Private. Founders: Geraldo Ramos (CEO), Eddie Hsu, Jardson Almeida. Connect Ventures (CAA + NEA joint venture) led Series A. Notable individual investors: Steve Aoki, 3LAU, Freddy Wexler, Alexander23 (artists investing as stakeholders in their own tool).

## Caveats

- The core source separation technology is increasingly commoditized (open-source models are catching up)
- 50M registered users ≠ 50M paying users; conversion and monetization of a freemium music tool is hard
- The licensed voice cloning market is legally uncertain and evolving rapidly
- Music AI is a crowded space (Suno, Udio, Adobe Podcast, and many others)
- World-changing angle is indirect — tools for creators, not infrastructure for civilization

## Why this is not ⭐

The scale is real (50M users, Apple App of Year) but the world-changing counterfactual is weak: if Music AI didn't exist, other companies would fill the space. A star-tier entry would need a more defensible position — either unique data/relationships with the music industry, or a patent portfolio that makes them the unavoidable infrastructure layer.
