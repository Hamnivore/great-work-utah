# Biological Engineering

Programming living systems as a platform — synthetic biology, gene editing, fermentation, biomaterials, and engineered organisms as a substrate for solving problems.

**A note on Utah:** Gene editing and synthetic biology companies are concentrated in Boston, Bay Area, and San Diego. Utah has good university research programs (U of U, USU) and a growing spinout ecosystem through Altitude Lab, but most biological engineering work is still in university labs rather than commercial companies. The one standout is CleanJoule, which uses fermentation to produce aviation and rocket fuels that outperform petroleum. USU's Randy Lewis spider silk lab (Araknitek spinout) is the other flagged effort — the science is world-class but commercialization has been slow across the entire field.
