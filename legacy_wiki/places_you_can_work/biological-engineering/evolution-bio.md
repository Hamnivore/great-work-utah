# Evolution Bio

**Tier:** B — platform technology with real pharma partnerships, directed evolution is genuinely powerful
**Domain:** Biological engineering — directed protein evolution (VEGAS platform)
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Early (Nucleus Fund + Altitude Lab investment; Eli Lilly + Kallyope partnerships)
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT (U of U Biochemistry spinout)
**Website:** Unknown

## Mission

Engineer better proteins — especially GPCRs — for drug discovery by making directed evolution fast, practical, and mammalian-cell-compatible using the VEGAS platform.

## Why it matters

GPCRs are the single most important target class in drug discovery — roughly one-third of all approved drugs hit GPCRs, yet they are notoriously difficult to engineer and screen. VEGAS (Viral Evolution of Genetically Actuated Sequences) performs directed evolution of GPCRs, transcription factors, and nanobodies directly in mammalian cells, and does it in under a week per round. That speed matters: existing directed evolution approaches require bacterial or yeast systems, which don't recapitulate human cell pharmacology. If VEGAS works as described, it makes GPCR drug optimization dramatically faster and more accurate — tools that the pharma industry will pay for. Eli Lilly's partnership is a strong signal.

## The hard problem

Directed evolution in mammalian cells is genuinely harder than in bacteria: cell growth is slower, transfection efficiency is lower, and selection pressure is harder to implement. VEGAS overcomes this by using viral replication as the selection pressure — only viruses that encode proteins with the desired function replicate efficiently. The key open question is how broadly this approach transfers across GPCR subtypes and other target classes.

## Mechanism of impact

Platform tool: pharma and biotech companies license VEGAS or contract with Evolution Bio for target-specific protein engineering. This is scientific infrastructure with a commercial model, not a drug development company in the traditional sense.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Biochemists, molecular biologists, protein engineers. Anyone interested in combining synthetic biology with drug discovery. Early-stage, so broad responsibility and direct impact on the core technology.

## Ownership

U of U spinout. Nucleus Fund + Altitude Lab investment (undisclosed amount). Founder: Dr. Justin English (U of U Biochemistry). 4 issued/pending patents.

## Caveats

- No disclosed financials beyond early institutional investment
- Platform tools are common in biotech and tend to be valued at lower multiples than drug programs unless they generate multiple validated assets
- Competition from directed evolution platforms elsewhere (Dyno Therapeutics, AbCellera, Evotec)
- Eli Lilly and Kallyope partnerships are encouraging but partnership ≠ product success

## Why this is not ⭐

The VEGAS technology is real and the pharma interest is validated, but it's still very early commercially. A star-tier entry would need either (a) a major drug candidate validated using VEGAS in clinical trials, or (b) demonstrated platform revenue at scale.
