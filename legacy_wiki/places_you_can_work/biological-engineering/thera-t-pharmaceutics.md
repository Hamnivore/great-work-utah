# Thera-T Pharmaceutics

**Tier:** C — plausible multi-antigen approach to blood cancer resistance, very early
**Domain:** Biological engineering — multi-antigen T-cell immunotherapy
**Also relevant to:** [Health & longevity](../health-and-longevity/)
**Type:** Company (early-stage)
**Stage:** Preclinical (U2TAH-incubated; ~$400K funding; animal studies ongoing)
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT (U of U College of Pharmacy spinout)
**Website:** [thera-tpharmaceutics.com](https://thera-tpharmaceutics.com)

## Mission

Treat B-cell leukemias, lymphomas, and multiple myeloma with MATCH (Multi-Antigen T-Cell Hybridizers) — self-assembling two-component constructs that direct T cells to multiple cancer antigens simultaneously, reducing resistance and immune overstimulation.

## Why it matters

Blood cancer immunotherapy (especially CAR-T and bispecific antibodies) has real clinical successes, but a major failure mode is antigen escape — cancer cells that downregulate the single target the therapy is aimed at and escape killing. MATCH targets multiple antigens simultaneously, making escape harder. The two-component, self-assembling design (no viral manufacturing required) could also dramatically reduce the cost and complexity of production relative to current CAR-T manufacturing. U of U's 2025 Breakthrough Technology of the Year award signals institutional confidence.

## The hard problem

Multi-antigen targeting sounds simple but requires that all targeting domains remain functionally active and non-interfering when self-assembled. The AI-guided dosing model (personalizing based on tumor burden, antigen expression, T-cell counts) adds an interesting layer but also requires validated clinical data to tune. No multi-specific T-cell engager of this design has been clinically validated yet.

## Ownership

U2TAH (Huntsman Cancer Institute therapeutics accelerator) incubated. Founder: Tommy Gambles (postdoc, U of U College of Pharmacy), building on Dr. Jindřich Kopeček's polymer immunotherapy lab.

## Caveats

- Preclinical only — animal studies ongoing, no IND filed
- $400K in funding is lab-scale only; clinical development requires orders of magnitude more capital
- Blood cancer immunotherapy is a crowded space (Kymriah, Yescarta, bispecifics are already approved)
- MATCH's self-assembling design is novel but unvalidated at clinical scale
