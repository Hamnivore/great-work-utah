# Defense & Security

Protecting civilization against kinetic, cyber, and infrastructure threats. Includes defense hardware, counter-weapon systems, military aerospace, cybersecurity, and national security research.

**A note on Utah:** Utah has a deep defense footprint but most of it is prime-contractor territory — Hill AFB's Ogden Air Logistics Complex (the F-35 depot), Northrop Grumman at Promontory (Sentinel ICBM and Artemis SLS solid rocket motors), and the supply chain around them. Those are civilizationally important programs, but they don't fit this wiki's model: individual leverage is limited, mission autonomy is institutional, and a reader choosing where to spend their working hours there is choosing a large company career path.

The frontier signal in Utah defense is concentrated in a smaller cluster of companies working on counter-drone systems, attritable drones, autonomous airspace defense, and the aerospace/defense ecosystem org **47G**. Counter-UAS and drone-enabled SOF operations have become two of the fastest-moving problem areas in defense since 2022, and Utah is unexpectedly central to both.

See also: [Space](../space/) for Space Dynamics Lab (UARC for Space Force and Missile Defense Agency) and [Transportation](../transportation/) for Electric Power Systems (electric aviation propulsion with defense applications).

## Ecosystem context

- **47G** — Aerospace & defense ecosystem organization. 200+ member companies and universities, positioned to lobby and coordinate Utah's defense sector. Strong signal for where member-company activity clusters. [47g.org](https://www.47g.org/)
- **Northrop Grumman (Promontory facility)** — Covered under [space/⭐northrop-grumman-promontory.md](../space/⭐northrop-grumman-promontory.md). Sentinel ICBM and SLS/Artemis solid rocket motors.
- **BAE Systems, Boeing, Lockheed Martin** — Various Utah facilities tied to Hill AFB supply chain.
- **Karman Space & Defense (West Valley City, UT)** — Building a $28.5M factory to quadruple production of UAS launcher systems and nozzle components for loitering munitions and counter-UAS programs. 100 new jobs expected. Deploying "Karman Operating System" — AI-integrated manufacturing automation. Opening Q4 2026. A signal that Utah is becoming a manufacturing node for precision defense hardware, not just systems integration.
