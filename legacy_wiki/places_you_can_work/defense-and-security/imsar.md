# IMSAR

**Tier:** A — 20-year track record, 30+ country deployments, $99M Army contract; quiet but load-bearing for drone-enabled ISR
**Domain:** Defense & security — Synthetic aperture radar / airborne sensing
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Mature private company; operating since 2004; no public funding rounds
**HQ:** Springville, UT
**Founded:** 2004
**Website:** [imsar.com](https://www.imsar.com)

## Mission

Make synthetic aperture radar small, light, cheap, and reliable enough to fly on any drone — from a Group 1 hand-launched UAS to a manned aircraft — giving tactical platforms all-weather, day/night, through-smoke imaging that was previously only available on large strategic aircraft.

## Why it matters

Synthetic aperture radar (SAR) is the sensor that sees through clouds, smoke, darkness, and camouflage. For decades it was confined to large aircraft because the hardware was massive and expensive. IMSAR's low-SWaP (Size, Weight, and Power) NSP radar line changed that — putting SAR capability on drones small enough for a soldier to carry. The Army's $99M contract for small-aperture radar on small UAS validated the technology at program scale. Thirty-plus countries now fly IMSAR systems; 100+ unique platform integrations have been fielded.

The connection to the rest of this wiki is direct: IMSAR's detect-and-avoid radar technology was the foundation that Fortem Technologies (this wiki's S-tier counter-drone company) acquired and built their airspace defense system on. IMSAR is the sensor layer that multiple downstream platforms depend on.

## Product line

NSP radar systems scaled to UAS group:

- **NSP-2**: Group 1–2 UAS (hand-launched, <25 lbs)
- **NSP-3**: Group 2–3 UAS
- **NSP-5**: Group 3–4 UAS
- **NSP-7 / NSP-8**: Group 4–5 UAS and manned aircraft; imaging to 55 km at 1m resolution, all-weather

All systems provide multi-mode operation: SAR imaging (high-resolution still), change detection (spot what moved since last pass), and moving target indicator (MTI, track things in motion). These modes in a single low-SWaP unit are the core value proposition.

## Customers

Lockheed Martin, Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory, and drone manufacturers across 30+ countries. The breadth indicates that IMSAR is functioning as infrastructure for the broader drone ISR ecosystem — the sensor that integrators build around.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

RF engineers, radar signal processing specialists, embedded systems engineers, and aerospace engineers. The company is private and has operated for 20 years without needing VC — indicative of a profitable, contract-driven business rather than a growth-at-all-costs play. Deep technical work, long-horizon programs.

## Caveats / red flags

- No public financials; company size and trajectory are opaque
- 20-year-old company in a space now attracting many well-funded competitors (Capella Space, Umbra, SAAB, Leonardo DRS, others)
- Low-SWaP SAR is increasingly commoditizing as component costs fall; the moat is manufacturing quality and program relationships, not IP alone
