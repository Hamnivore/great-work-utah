# L3Harris Salt Lake City

**Tier:** B — real with significant caveats; outclassed, mid-tier, or commoditizing
**Domain:** Defense & security — Military communications, electronic warfare, ISR payloads
**Also relevant to:** [Space](../space/)
**Type:** Company (public, NYSE: LHX — Salt Lake City facility)
**Stage:** Established, major defense prime
**HQ:** Melbourne, FL (corporate HQ); Salt Lake City Communication Systems site is one of the largest
**Founded:** L3Harris formed 2019 (merger of L3 Technologies and Harris Corporation)
**Website:** [l3harris.com](https://www.l3harris.com)

## Mission

Provide resilient communications, electronic warfare, and ISR (intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance) systems to the US military and allied partners, spanning tactical radios, space-based payloads, and contested-environment networking.

## Why it matters

Modern warfare is a communications problem as much as a kinetic one. Ukraine demonstrated that whoever controls the EM spectrum — radios that work under jamming, tactical communications that survive EW attack, ISR links that withstand interception — shapes the ground truth of the battle. L3Harris is one of the major US companies building that layer of the stack. Their Salt Lake City Communications Systems site is one of the larger defense comms facilities in the US, with significant work on Falcon IV tactical radios, SATCOM terminals, and various classified ISR payloads.

This is supply-chain work for the US and allied militaries. The specific programs change, but the underlying capability — secure, resilient, spectrum-agile communications — is what a modern military runs on. If you think the credibility of US defense depends substantially on communications and ISR, L3Harris is one of a small set of companies doing the actual engineering.

Salt Lake City specifically is a large site: thousands of engineers, multiple buildings, visible hiring in RF/comms, software, systems engineering. It is one of Utah's largest defense employers after Hill AFB.

## The hard problem

Designing radios and communication links that work in contested RF environments — jamming, spoofing, direction-finding exploitation — while also being interoperable with legacy military hardware, light enough to be man-portable, and low-power enough to field at scale. The trend lines include waveform agility, cognitive radio, mesh networking, and integration with satellite and high-altitude relays. EW is a cat-and-mouse game where each side's advantage decays; staying ahead requires continuous investment.

## Mechanism of impact

Supply chain to US and allied militaries. Every tactical radio in the field, every SATCOM link, every Ukraine-fielded comms relay — some meaningful portion of that is L3Harris hardware and firmware. Second-order: allied interoperability (NATO, Five Eyes, etc.) depends on compatible comms, which shapes coalition capability.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

RF engineers, firmware engineers, systems engineers, cybersecurity specialists, program managers — all at scale and most requiring clearances. Large-company culture; individual leverage is moderate but the platforms being worked on are real and deployed. Good fit for engineers who want to work on production defense comms with a relocation radius that includes Salt Lake City rather than a Beltway commute.

## Ownership

Public, NYSE: LHX. Formed by 2019 merger of L3 Technologies and Harris Corporation. Standard institutional ownership; no single dominant holder. Corporate direction set at Melbourne FL HQ; Salt Lake City is a major operating site but not corporate decision-making center.

## Why this is not ⭐

Large public defense prime. Individual engineer leverage is limited by company scale; mission autonomy is corporate-institutional. The Salt Lake City site does genuinely important work, but the wiki's frontier-focus rewards category-defining companies and high-leverage environments. L3Harris is a solid, important place to work on defense comms — just not the indie-frontier story this wiki foregrounds.

## Caveats

- Clearance requirement for most interesting work
- Public defense prime — individual code rarely visible outside classified programs
- Corporate strategy direction set outside Utah
- Defense budget / program cycle volatility
