# Teal Drones

**Tier:** B — Army Program of Record for SRR validates the platform; public-company subsidiary limits individual leverage; crowded market
**Domain:** Defense & security — Small unmanned aircraft systems / Army ISR
**Also relevant to:** [Transportation](../transportation/)
**Type:** Company (subsidiary of Red Cat Holdings, NASDAQ: RCAT)
**Stage:** Operating; Army Program of Record; scaling toward 1,000 units/month
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT (Red Cat Holdings HQ: Puerto Rico)
**Founded:** 2015
**Website:** [tealdrones.com](https://tealdrones.com)

## Mission

Build American-made small drones that give every Army platoon a deployable ISR capability — reconnaissance at the squad level without dependence on foreign hardware supply chains.

## Why it matters

The U.S. Army selected Black Widow as its Program of Record for Short Range Reconnaissance (SRR). Program of Record means it's the official Army-wide platform — not a pilot, not a procurement experiment. Every platoon in the Army that gets an SRR sUAS gets a Black Widow. The scale implied is significant.

The supply chain angle matters too: Black Widow is manufactured in Salt Lake City, on the DoD Blue sUAS list (vetted for cyber and supply chain security), and uses AES-256 encrypted communications — explicitly designed to compete against DJI and other Chinese-manufactured drones that the DoD is actively trying to exit from its supply chain.

## Product

**Black Widow** — Group 1 quadcopter for ISR:
- Short-range reconnaissance (SRR) mission profile
- AES-256 encrypted communications
- Modular design with field-swappable batteries compatible across the Teal family
- Integrates with Tomahawk Robotics' Kinesis C2 interface
- Blue sUAS DoD-certified

**Fang** — FPV aircraft variant for closer-range or dynamic missions

## Business context

Teal Drones was founded in 2015 as a consumer/enterprise drone company and pivoted hard into defense. Red Cat Holdings (NASDAQ: RCAT) acquired Teal to anchor its defense drone portfolio alongside FlightWave Aerospace. Utah Governor has visited the SLC facility to signal state support. Manufacturing scaling target: 1,000 units/month.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Mechanical and electrical engineers focused on small UAS systems, defense acquisition professionals, and embedded software engineers with experience in encrypted communications and DoD program management. Manufacturing operations roles as production scales.

## Caveats / red flags

- Public company subsidiary (Red Cat Holdings, NASDAQ: RCAT) — individual contributor leverage is limited compared to an early-stage startup
- The Group 1 sUAS market is crowded: BRINC, Skydio (though Skydio has struggled on military contracts), Joby, Joby for defense — a Program of Record win can be reversed in subsequent tranches
- Red Cat Holdings' overall financial health matters — if the parent has cash flow issues, Teal's growth capital could be constrained
- SRR Tranche 2 competition is ongoing; Teal/Black Widow was finalist but outcome unclear as of early 2026
