# Wasatch Ionics

**Location:** Utah (exact city unknown)
**Website:** Unknown

## Mission

Develop lithium-oxygen (Li-O2) reserve batteries with integrated chemical oxygen generation for U.S. Army applications requiring long shelf life (30-day sleep mode) and high-power pulses on demand.

## Why it matters

The Army's Close Terrain-Shaping Obstacles (CTSO) require a power source that sleeps for weeks, then delivers a high-current pulse to activate the obstacle system. Current Li-thionyl chloride reserve batteries are the standard, but Li-O2 chemistry promises ~10x energy density improvement at equivalent weight. If the chemistry matures, it's relevant not just to obstacle systems but to a wide class of defense electronics needing long standby + burst power.

## The hard problem

Li-O2 batteries have been theoretically compelling for years but practically difficult: oxygen management, dendrite formation during discharge, and electrolyte degradation under repeated charge-discharge are all unsolved at scale. Wasatch Ionics is integrating chemical oxygen generation on-board, which adds complexity but removes the open-cathode requirement — a genuine engineering bet.
