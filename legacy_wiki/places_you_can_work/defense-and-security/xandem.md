# Xandem

**Tier:** C — serious underlying technology, unclear trajectory
**Domain:** Defense & security — RF tomographic through-wall motion detection
**Type:** Company (private, bootstrapped)
**Stage:** Commercial (consumer pivot underway as of 2024)
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT (U of U ECE spinout)
**Website:** [xandem.com](https://www.xandem.com)

## Mission

Detect human presence and motion through walls using RF tomographic sensing — a mesh of small wireless transceivers that measure how people disrupt radio waves between nodes.

## Why it matters

Through-wall sensing without active radar or cameras has significant ISR (intelligence/surveillance/reconnaissance) and search-and-rescue applications. Knowing if a building is occupied before entry is a life-safety problem for law enforcement and military. The underlying physics (RF tomographic imaging) is sound and DHS/NSF have granted funding, signaling government interest. If the technology works at operational reliability, it's a genuinely useful defensive and rescue tool.

## The hard problem

RF tomographic imaging is computationally demanding and sensitive to environmental multipath. Distinguishing humans from pets, HVAC systems, or structural movement is a signal processing challenge. The sensor mesh requires fixed node placement, which limits tactical deployment. The team has the academic pedigree (Joey Wilson, Neal Patwari from U of U ECE) but hasn't demonstrated military-grade reliability.

## Ownership

Bootstrapped. $150K NSF SBIR + small angel investment from Ryan Smith. Founder-controlled.

## Caveats

- As of 2024, the company is pivoting toward consumer home security (Xandem Home, Indiegogo crowdfund) — this is a bad sign for the serious defense/ISR potential
- Bootstrapped limits R&D investment needed to take the technology to mil-spec
- Through-wall RF has attracted well-funded competitors; if the government wanted this at scale, they'd likely fund a larger player
- Worth revisiting if the company reverses course and pursues defense contracts seriously
