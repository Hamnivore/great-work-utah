# Education

How humans learn, teach, and accumulate knowledge. Includes AI tutoring at human-teacher quality, alternative school models, cognitive science applied to learning, and infrastructure for credentialing and lifelong education. Distinct from [scientific infrastructure](../scientific-infrastructure/) (research tools) and [tools for thought](../tools-for-thought/) (interfaces that augment individual reasoning).

**A note on Utah:** Utah has a large edtech *industry* — Instructure (Canvas LMS, public), Pluralsight (tech upskilling, went private in 2021), Nearpod, MasteryConnect, and dozens of SaaS learning tools in the Silicon Slopes corridor. But this is a mature enterprise-software ecosystem, not frontier research on how humans learn. The "second wave of edtech" in Utah is mostly AI features bolted onto existing platforms (Mindsmith, others).

The frontier bets on education that would clear this wiki's bar — AI tutoring that reliably outperforms human teachers, serious alternative school models, cognitive-science-backed curriculum redesign, or reliable credentialing outside of traditional universities — are concentrated elsewhere: Khan Academy (Bay Area), Alpha School (Austin), Math Academy, Quantic/Pedago, and the growing cluster of post-AI tutoring research groups.

Two Utah exceptions: **Sandbox** — a credit-bearing startup-founding practicum that originated at BYU and is now expanding across USU, Utah Tech, UVU, and the University of Utah, experimenting with restructuring undergraduate senior years. And **BYU-Pathway Worldwide** — 80,000+ students in 180+ countries, run by the LDS Church, combining low-cost accredited online degrees with an employment placement engine. BYU-Pathway's impact angle is access and scale, not pedagogy innovation.

## Ecosystem context

- **University of Utah College of Education & BYU McKay School of Education** — Strong teacher-training programs, some learning-sciences research, but not a noted source of frontier-edtech spinouts.
- **UCET (Utah Coalition for Educational Technology)** — Teacher-facing professional network; useful signal for what tools are actually landing in K-12 classrooms.
- **Utah AI Policy Act (HB 452, updated 2024)** — Regulation of generative AI in education and mental health. Worth knowing as a constraint on AI-tutoring products serving Utah schools.
