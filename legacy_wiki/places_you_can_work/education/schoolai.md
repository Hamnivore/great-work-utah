# SchoolAI

**Tier:** B — 1M classrooms, real deployment, but AI tutoring moat is unclear
**Domain:** Education — AI personalized learning and teacher support platform
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Series A ($25M, April 2025; Insight Partners, NextView Ventures, Peterson Ventures; $32M total)
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT
**Website:** [schoolai.com](https://schoolai.com)

## Mission

Help teachers personalize learning for every student using AI — combining an AI assistant that reduces teacher administrative burden with AI-powered "Spaces" (personalized tutors, games, and lessons that adapt to each student).

## Why it matters

Personalized education has been the unfulfilled promise of EdTech for 30 years. The teacher-to-student ratio problem is real: one teacher cannot give 30 students individualized attention simultaneously. SchoolAI's bet is that AI can serve as a force multiplier — handling the per-student adaptation while giving teachers better data on where each student is struggling. 1M classrooms across 80+ countries with 80% of Utah schools engaged suggests the product has cleared the school district adoption barrier (which is notoriously high). The Trump administration's 2025 AI-in-education executive order may accelerate government tailwinds.

## The hard problem

Making AI tutoring that is genuinely pedagogically effective (not just responsive) requires understanding how learning actually works — not just answering questions but structuring knowledge acquisition, detecting misconceptions, and adapting to individual student learning styles. The safety requirements for AI deployed to minors add additional constraints. SchoolAI has an architecture that's designed for managed, safe AI deployment in schools, which is a real differentiator from generic AI tools.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct deployment: 1M classrooms is meaningful scale. If the AI tutoring genuinely improves learning outcomes (not just engagement), the effect on educational equity could be significant — students in under-resourced schools accessing quality personalized instruction for the first time.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

ML engineers, curriculum designers, education researchers, product engineers. People who care about educational equity and believe AI can genuinely help (not just distract). The company is in rapid growth mode — broad responsibilities, fast iteration cycles.

## Ownership

Private. Founder: Caleb Hicks. Insight Partners-led Series A. Peterson Ventures (Utah VC) as continuing investor.

## Caveats

- AI tutoring is a crowded space (Khan Academy, Khanmigo, Duolingo, Google Classroom AI, etc.)
- School district procurement is slow and cyclical — growth can stall at budget constraint points
- Outcome data (does it actually improve learning?) is rarely published; engagement ≠ learning
- 1M classrooms is reach, not depth — how embedded the tool is in daily instruction matters more than the headline number
- Founded 2023, very young for the institutional scale claimed

## Why this is not ⭐

Real deployment but not yet a defensible category leader. The AI tutoring space is bifurcating between consumer (Duolingo-style) and institutional (school district sales), and SchoolAI is playing the harder institutional game. If they can prove measurable learning outcome improvement, the tier should move up.
