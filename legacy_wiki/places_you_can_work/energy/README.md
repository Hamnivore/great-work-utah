## Ecosystem context

- **Utah FORGE** (University of Utah, Milford UT) — DOE-funded field lab for enhanced geothermal R&D. Not a company, but the reason Utah is the center of EGS development. [utahforge.com](https://utahforge.com)
- **Utah San Rafael Energy Lab** (Emery County) — State-run nuclear test site, currently hosting Valar Atomics and planned for more advanced reactor companies.
- **Intermountain Power Project (Delta, UT)** — Coal-to-hydrogen conversion in progress. IPP is transitioning from coal generation to hydrogen-capable gas turbines. Large-scale industrial green-hydrogen infrastructure is genuinely emerging in Utah; worth tracking for when a clear company-shaped entry emerges.
- **Creekstone Energy (Delta, UT)** — Building the "AI Gigasite" in Millard County: a planned 10 GW hybrid-generation (natural gas + solar) data center campus targeting hyperscale AI compute demand. Series B led by Trident Ridge. Not a deep tech company per se, but a signal that Utah's energy infrastructure is being built out to support large-scale AI compute. First powered data centers targeted for 2026.
- **Great Salt Lake lithium** — Lilac Solutions (Oakland CA) completed a successful 7-month DLE pilot at GSL in 2024 (87% lithium recovery, 99.97% purity) and has a 10-year offtake agreement with Traxys; commercial production target 2027. International Battery Metals has a DLE plant near Salt Lake City. Utah HB 453 (2024) banned evaporation-based mineral extraction from GSL, making DLE the only legal path for new lithium ventures at the lake. No dominant Utah-headquartered company yet — this remains an open opportunity. See [other/great-salt-lake.md](../other/great-salt-lake.md) for context.
