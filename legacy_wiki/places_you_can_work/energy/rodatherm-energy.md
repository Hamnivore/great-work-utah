# Rodatherm Energy

**Tier:** A — novel closed-loop geothermal technology, largest first-raise in geothermal startup history, pilot underway in Utah
**Domain:** Energy — Enhanced geothermal systems (closed-loop)
**Also relevant to:** [Scientific infrastructure](../scientific-infrastructure/) (Utah FORGE ecosystem)
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Series A; $38M (oversubscribed); pilot underway
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT (also Calgary, Alberta)
**Founded:** ~2022
**Website:** [rodatherm.com](https://rodatherm.com)

## Mission

Unlock geothermal energy from hot sedimentary basins — the vast majority of the Earth's subsurface heat resource — using a sealed, closed-loop system that achieves 50% higher efficiency than conventional approaches.

## Why it matters

Conventional geothermal and enhanced geothermal systems (EGS) like Fervo's depend on fluid circulating through rock fractures — effective in hard crystalline rock but difficult in hot sedimentary basins where fracture networks are unreliable. Rodatherm's closed-loop system addresses this directly: a proprietary working fluid stays fully isolated in a sealed pressurized loop, never interacting with the reservoir. Heat transfers conductively and convectively from the rock into the fluid, which then drives a power turbine at the surface.

The numbers are significant: 50% higher efficiency than water-based binary-cycle geothermal systems, 5x less fluid, no freshwater consumption, and minimal surface footprint. The sealed design eliminates the primary operating-cost risk of conventional geothermal — fluid loss through leaking rock formations.

The $38M Series A was the largest first venture raise for any geothermal startup in history. Investors include TDK Ventures, Toyota Ventures, and TechEnergy Ventures — a mix of industrial corporations with strategic interest in geothermal scaling. Evok Innovations led.

## Technology

The system is optimized for hot sedimentary basins — geologic formations that cover large swaths of the Great Basin (Utah's geology), enabling heat extraction in places where conventional geothermal is impractical. The working fluid stays sealed: no water injected into the formation, no brine management, no induced seismicity from fluid pressure changes. The system can operate in populated areas where conventional geothermal's surface footprint and seismic risk are disqualifying.

Rodatherm's approach is distinct from Fervo Energy (horizontal EGS in hard rock at Utah FORGE / Beaver County) and Zanskar Geothermal (AI-driven exploration). All three are in the Utah geothermal cluster but attacking different parts of the problem.

## Utah context

The pilot system is being built in Utah's Great Basin. Rodatherm's target after the pilot is a 100 MW system at the same site using project financing. CEO Curtis Cook previously built Vesta Energy Corp from a single well to $1B+ asset value — oil and gas operational experience applied to geothermal.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Petroleum engineers, drilling engineers, and reservoir engineers comfortable with energy project development. Mechanical engineers for the power-generation hardware. Early-stage startup pace inside a field that runs on multi-year project timelines.

## Caveats / red flags

- The pilot has not yet validated the technology at commercial scale — the $38M is specifically for that validation
- Closed-loop geothermal has been attempted before (Eavor Technologies in Canada is the prominent comparable); whether Rodatherm's specific fluid choice and system design delivers the claimed efficiency advantage at commercial scale remains to be proven
- Hot sedimentary basins are geographically abundant but heat gradients vary widely — site selection risk is real
- The company has dual HQ in Calgary and Salt Lake City; long-term Utah commitment is not guaranteed
