# Zanskar Geothermal ⭐

**Tier:** S — category-defining; arguably the best place in the world to do this work
**Domain:** Energy — Geothermal Exploration
**Stage:** Series B ($115M raised)
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT
**Founded:** 2021
**Website:** [zanskar.com](https://zanskar.com)

## Mission

Make geothermal power the most affordable form of widely-deployable, 24/7 carbon-free generation on the planet.

## Why it matters

Geothermal has an almost unique property among clean energy sources: it is always on. No storage problem, no intermittency, no weather dependence. The catch has always been that finding viable sites is expensive, slow, and high-risk — most of the Earth's heat is in places that are hard to characterize. If Zanskar makes exploration repeatable and data-driven, it could unlock geothermal at terawatt scale across the western U.S. and beyond. In 2025, they became the first company in over 30 years to discover and confirm a *blind* geothermal system (one with no surface expression) as a commercial prospect — entirely using AI and computational geoscience.

## The hard problem

Subsurface geothermal resources are largely invisible from the surface. Traditional exploration is slow, expensive, and heavily reliant on geological luck. The unsolved problem is: how do you turn geothermal exploration into a repeatable, predictable process? Zanskar is attacking this with AI-driven interpretation of seismic, geophysical, and historical drilling data to map heat and permeability underground without drilling first.

## Mechanism of impact

An exploration tool that other developers (Fervo, Cyrq, Ormat, etc.) can use to de-risk and accelerate site development. If the tool works, the bottleneck for geothermal shifts from "can we find sites?" to "can we drill fast enough?" — a much more tractable problem.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Geoscientists and ML engineers who are comfortable with messy, high-dimensional physical data. People who want to work at the intersection of applied earth science and inference under uncertainty. The founders (CEO Carl Hoiland, CTO Joel Edwards) come from geoscience and data science respectively — the culture likely rewards technical depth over product polish.

## Ownership

VC-majority. $115M raised across multiple rounds from private investors. Founders Carl Hoiland and Joel Edwards likely retain meaningful equity at Series B, but outside investors hold substantial influence over exit timeline. Standard VC pressure to find a liquidity event within 7–10 years applies — watch for acquisition by a large energy company before the mission fully plays out.

## Caveats

- The AI claims are real but still early — one confirmed discovery does not prove the method generalizes
- Exploration tools create value only when other infrastructure (drilling, power purchase agreements, grid connection) catches up
- Revenue model depends on either licensing the tech or developing projects themselves; the former may have limited margin, the latter requires a lot of capital
