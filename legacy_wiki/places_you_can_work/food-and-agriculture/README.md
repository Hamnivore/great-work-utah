# Food & Agriculture

Feeding people sustainably — precision agriculture, alternative proteins, food production systems, soil and water, and the biology of what humans eat. Distinct from [biological engineering](../biological-engineering/) (which covers programming living systems generally, including fermentation for non-food uses).

**A note on Utah:** Utah State University is a land-grant institution with strong agricultural research (UAES, Western Dairy Center, Center for Human Nutrition Studies), but the commercial spinout pipeline in food and ag is quiet compared to California, Boston, and the Midwest. The one Utah-specific pressure that *should* be driving frontier ag work — the shrinking Great Salt Lake and the regional water crisis — hasn't yet produced a standout startup. But under the wiki's revised inclusion bar, several Utah food-and-ag operators are worth naming, even if they aren't category-defining.

## Ecosystem context

- **Utah Agricultural Experiment Station (UAES, USU)** — Land-grant research network; hundreds of active projects across Utah. Strong in range management, dairy, arid-land crops. [uaes.usu.edu](https://uaes.usu.edu)
- **Western Dairy Center (USU)** — Basic and applied dairy research with industry tech-transfer focus. Utah has significant dairy; this center connects research to production.
- **Center for Human Nutrition Studies (USU)** — Clinical nutrition research infrastructure; supports industry-funded nutrition studies.
- **HyPAR.farm** — Vertical farming operator in Utah; limited public info.
- **Grōv Technologies (Vineyard, UT)** — Automated indoor vertical farming system (Olympus Tower) for livestock fodder. Produces 5,000–6,000 lbs/day of sprouted wheatgrass per tower at <5% of field-irrigation water. Similar mission to Renaissance Ag but larger hardware (commercial-scale towers vs. shipping containers) and signed deals with River Ranch Dairy and Superior Farms (10 towers for 60,000 lbs/day). Major caveat: wholly owned subsidiary of Nu Skin Enterprises (NYSE: NUS) — mission autonomy is weak. No separate entry because the parent ownership and overlap with Renaissance Ag's mission make it hard to treat as an independent actor.
- **Great Salt Lake crisis** — Not a company, but the defining environmental pressure on Utah agriculture. ~70% of Utah's water goes to alfalfa and cattle feed. The Lake is drying. A startup that materially shifts the water-per-calorie ratio of Utah livestock feed would be genuinely world-changing *for this region*; the opportunity is visible and no one has fully cracked it. See [other/great-salt-lake.md](../other/great-salt-lake.md).
