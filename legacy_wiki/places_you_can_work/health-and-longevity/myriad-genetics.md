# Myriad Genetics

**Tier:** A — strong; not globally category-defining but excellent
**Domain:** Health & longevity — Hereditary cancer testing, MRD liquid biopsy, precision oncology
**Also relevant to:** [Biological engineering](../biological-engineering/) (NGS workflow development)
**Type:** Company (public, NASDAQ: MYGN)
**Stage:** Established (founded 1991, public since 1995, ~$800M revenue)
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT
**Founded:** 1991 — direct offshoot of Mark Skolnick's BRCA1 sequencing work
**Website:** [myriad.com](https://myriad.com)

## Mission

Translate insights from genetics into clinical diagnostics — identifying hereditary cancer risk, guiding cancer treatment selection, and detecting minimal residual disease (MRD) after treatment.

## Why it matters

Myriad is the company that commercialized **BRCA1/BRCA2 genetic testing** — arguably the single most impactful genetic diagnostic of the last 30 years. The genes themselves were identified using the Utah Population Database; Mark Skolnick and the Myriad team sequenced BRCA1 in August 1994. Every woman who has been tested for hereditary breast and ovarian cancer risk since then — and the thousands of cancers prevented by prophylactic decisions made on those results — traces back to work that started at Myriad.

The BRCA moat no longer exists (the Supreme Court's 2013 Myriad decision invalidated their gene patents), but Myriad pivoted. The current frontier is **Precise MRD** — their molecular residual disease test, which detects circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) at part-per-million levels to identify cancer recurrence before imaging can. A 2025 *Lancet Oncology* publication showed Precise MRD detecting ctDNA in 94% of tested patients at very low tumor burden in metastatic renal cell carcinoma. If MRD testing becomes standard-of-care for monitoring treatment response and recurrence — which many oncologists believe it will — Myriad is one of the small set of companies positioned to be at the center of it.

The September 2025 SOPHiA Genetics partnership extends Myriad's liquid biopsy companion diagnostics globally via SOPHiA's 800+ institution network. This is how a US diagnostics lab becomes globally relevant — through platform partnerships.

## The hard problem

Detecting circulating tumor DNA at <100 ppm in a blood sample, distinguishing it from germline variation and normal clonal hematopoiesis, and doing all of this reliably enough for clinical decision-making is at the frontier of oncology diagnostics. The analytical chemistry (ultra-deep sequencing, error correction, signal deconvolution) and the clinical interpretation (how low a ctDNA signal still means "treat the recurrence") are both active research problems.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct deployment in clinical care. Every Precise MRD result used to make a treatment decision is impact footprint. Second-order: Myriad's commercialization of BRCA testing established the entire hereditary cancer risk industry, which has prevented thousands of cancers. If MRD testing similarly becomes standard, the recurrence detection impact will dwarf that.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Molecular biologists, bioinformatics engineers, clinical lab scientists, and product managers who understand both the science and the regulatory pathway for laboratory developed tests. Public mid-cap culture; individual leverage is moderate. Good fit for someone who wants impactful work in precision oncology without moving to Boston or San Francisco.

## Ownership

Public (NASDAQ: MYGN). Historical founder/leadership continuity has weakened over the years; the company has had multiple CEO transitions and strategic pivots. Institutional ownership is standard; no dominant single holder.

## Why this is not ⭐

Myriad has been through a prolonged strategic transition — losing the BRCA patent moat, competing with better-funded MRD players (Natera's Signatera is the market leader), and several CEO changes. The BRCA legacy is S-tier; the current company is a real precision oncology business but not clearly the category leader in any single high-growth segment. If Precise MRD captures meaningful market share over the next 3–5 years, re-evaluate for a star.

## Caveats

- Public-market volatility; mid-cap diagnostics companies trade on quarterly test volume beats and misses
- Intense competition in MRD (Natera, Guardant, Inivata, Adaptive Biotech)
- Post-BRCA identity transition is not fully complete
- CEO and strategy turnover in recent years
- The 2013 Supreme Court decision invalidating gene patents materially weakened their original moat
