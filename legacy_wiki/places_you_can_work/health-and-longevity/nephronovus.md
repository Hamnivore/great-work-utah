## Mission

Develop therapeutics that reverse renal fibrosis — the scarring process that drives progression of chronic kidney disease.

## Why it matters

Chronic kidney disease affects ~800 million people globally; there are no approved drugs that reverse established fibrosis. Most existing therapies slow progression at best. If renal fibrosis could genuinely be reversed, it would meaningfully extend life and reduce dialysis burden worldwide.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Early co-founder or lab scientist in CKD biology. Two-person team at incubation stage.

## Why this is not ⭐

No disclosed funding, no published results, no disclosed mechanism. Altitude Lab residency provides some validation that someone with domain knowledge looked at this and thought it was worth incubating. The scientific premise is real; the execution risk is enormous at this stage.

## Caveats

- No public information on mechanism, pipeline, or fundraising
- Two-person team
- CKD is a crowded space with major pharma R&D and failed trials (pirfenidone, nintedanib analogs)
