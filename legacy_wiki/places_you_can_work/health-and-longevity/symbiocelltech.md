## Mission

Cure Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes with "Neo-Islets" — 3D clusters of insulin-expressing cells co-packaged with Mesenchymal Stem Cells (MSCs) that provide local immune isolation, eliminating the need for systemic anti-rejection drugs.

## Why it matters

Type 1 diabetes requires lifelong insulin management. Islet transplantation can restore glucose control but requires systemic immunosuppression (which has its own serious side effects) indefinitely. SymbioCellTech's bet: MSCs within the Neo-Islet cluster provide local immune privilege, removing the need for systemic drugs. If it works, you have a functional cure for 500M+ diabetics that doesn't require lifetime immunosuppression. That's a step change — not an improvement.

## The hard problem

MSC-mediated local immune privilege is real in some contexts but not yet demonstrated as durable protection for transplanted islets at clinical scale. Maintaining cluster viability after transplantation, achieving engraftment without vascularization challenges, and validating that MSC-protection doesn't wane over time are all open questions.

## Ownership

## Caveats

- Preclinical as of 2025 — no IND submitted
- The "no immunosuppression" claim is the key differentiator and the key unproven claim
- Islet transplantation has a long history of promising preclinical results that fail in human trials
- Worth revisiting once IND is submitted and first-in-human trial results are available
