# Techcyte

**Tier:** B — real product, real clinical scale, but outclassed nationally by better-funded AI pathology platforms
**Domain:** Health & longevity — AI-driven digital diagnostics / anatomic and clinical pathology
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Late growth; ~$15M revenue, 124 employees; $15M raise (2026)
**HQ:** Orem, UT
**Founded:** 2013 (U of U spinout via Dr. Mohamed Salama; founders Ralph Yarro and Rick Smith)
**Website:** [techcyte.com](https://techcyte.com)

## Mission

Make pathology faster, more consistent, and accessible worldwide by replacing manual microscope review with AI analysis of digitized slides — across human clinical labs, veterinary diagnostics, and environmental testing.

## Why it matters

Pathology is the diagnostic bottleneck of modern medicine. A cancer diagnosis, an infection diagnosis, a hematology workup — nearly all require a trained human to look through a microscope. There are too few pathologists, they are unevenly distributed geographically, and human review introduces variability. A platform that automates the first pass — flagging slides, pre-classifying cells, integrating results into lab workflows — directly reduces diagnostic delay and frees pathologists for harder cases. At 9 million digital scans analyzed per year, Techcyte is not a demonstration project.

## The hard problem

Pathology is notoriously heterogeneous: staining variation, scanner differences, tissue preparation differences, and the sheer diversity of what pathologists are looking for (cancer grading, cell counts, parasite detection, food safety) make it hard to build a single platform that works across all use cases without retraining for every new lab and protocol. Techcyte's Fusion platform — announced March 2025 — is the first to unify anatomic pathology (tissue sections) and clinical pathology (blood, urine, cytology) in a single SaaS environment. Getting those two historically separate workflows to share infrastructure, AI models, and data is a real integration challenge.

## Mechanism of impact

Platform deployed at clinical labs, veterinary labs, and food-safety testing facilities. Techcyte doesn't generate diagnoses autonomously — it supports and accelerates human pathologists. Impact lands through: (1) reducing time-to-diagnosis, (2) enabling pathology services in labs too small to staff a full-time pathologist, and (3) standardizing analysis across sites that currently vary by individual reviewer. The Mayo Clinic partnership (2025 capital raise includes access to Mayo's Safe Harbor dataset of 17M de-identified slides) gives Techcyte the training data to compete with the large foundation-model labs.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

ML engineers with computer vision backgrounds who want to work on real medical data at real clinical scale. Pathology domain experts (MD or PhD) who want to be on the tech side. Biomedical informatics engineers. Not a basic-research environment — the product is shipping and used in clinical labs. Utah-based, not remote-first.

## Ownership

Private. Investors: Van Tuyl Companies (lead, 2026), Zoetis Inc. (strategic, since 2020), Mayo Clinic (strategic, access to 17M slide dataset). U of U TLO spinout. No VC majority — industrial/clinical strategics lead the cap table, which reduces exit pressure and supports a longer product roadmap.

## Why this is not ⭐

PathAI (Boston), Paige.ai (NYC), and Proscia (Philly) are better-funded, more AI-native, and more aggressively pursuing regulatory clearance for autonomous diagnostic tools. Techcyte's strength is breadth (multi-modality, multi-species, food safety) and its strategic position with Zoetis and Mayo Clinic, not frontier research or the largest training dataset. It is a real company at real scale, not a bet on a new paradigm.

## Caveats / red flags

- Revenue (~$15M) is modest for the stage and team size; path to profitability is the stated goal of the 2026 raise — not top-line growth
- The AI pathology space is consolidating rapidly; better-funded national players may outcompete on model capability
- Limited venture backing; Mayo and Zoetis are strategics who may prefer acquisition over independent scaling
- Regulatory: FDA clearance for clinical-use AI diagnostic tools is a gating challenge; unclear how much of Techcyte's revenue is in cleared vs. research-use-only deployments
