# Trace AQ

**Tier:** B — real product, real differentiation, early but validated
**Domain:** Health & longevity — AI-powered air quality and wildfire smoke forecasting
**Also relevant to:** [Scientific infrastructure](../scientific-infrastructure/)
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Seed ($1.25M, 2025; Rusheen Capital Management, Thin Line Capital)
**Location:** Salt Lake City, UT (U of U Chemical Engineering / Atmospheric Sciences spinout)
**Website:** [traceaq.com](https://www.traceaq.com)

## Mission

Provide accurate, physics-based air quality forecasting — specifically wildfire smoke, dust, and anthropogenic pollution — to communities, healthcare systems, and corporations that need to make real-time decisions about outdoor exposure.

## Why it matters

Wildfire smoke is one of the fastest-growing public health crises in the western United States. Salt Lake City's geographic inversion bowl makes it acutely affected; but wildfire smoke now regularly blankets populations across the whole Mountain West, Pacific Coast, and increasingly the Great Plains. Existing public forecasts (EPA AirNow, NWS) dramatically underpredict unhealthy air events. Trace AQ's forecasts have predicted 400% more unhealthy air events than competitors during the 2025 fire season — meaning people who relied on public data were unknowingly exposed to dangerous air. Accurate early warning directly reduces respiratory hospitalizations, asthma attacks, and long-term lung disease.

## The hard problem

Wildfire smoke plume prediction requires coupling fluid dynamics (wind fields), combustion chemistry (emission factors by fuel type), atmospheric chemistry (secondary aerosol formation), and local micrometeorology — all of which interact nonlinearly. Standard models run on coarse grids; Trace AQ's physics-based approach with ML augmentation runs at finer resolution and updates faster. Ten years of U of U research went into the core model.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct deployment: the Trace AQ AERO API and Flow AQ mobile app give consumers, healthcare providers, and businesses actionable advance warnings. At scale, earlier warnings → reduced outdoor activity on dangerous days → reduced respiratory disease burden.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Atmospheric scientists, computational fluid dynamicists, ML engineers interested in physical modeling. Public health and climate adaptation angles. Strong Utah mission fit — the Salt Lake Valley is one of the most air-quality-challenged metro areas in the US.

## Ownership

Private, seed-stage. Rusheen Capital Management-led round; Utah Venture Hub and Energy Accelerator partnerships. Founders: Heather Holmes (Chemical Engineering), Derek Mallia (Atmospheric Sciences), Taylor (Kai) Wilmot.

## Caveats

- $1.25M seed is early — scaling forecasting infrastructure and customer acquisition is expensive
- The API/SaaS model is a competitive market (The Weather Company, AirNow, Tomorrow.io all have air quality products)
- Commercial model (API licensing) may cap impact; bigger impact could come from government/public health system adoption, which is a harder sell
- Forecasting accuracy advantage may erode as large players improve their own models

## Why this is not ⭐

The technology differentiation is real but not permanently defensible. The company needs to establish a deep moat (unique data, exclusive partnerships, or adoption by public health systems) before larger players close the accuracy gap. Right now it's a well-validated spinout with a meaningful product, but not yet category-defining.
