# Machine Intelligence

New hardware compute paradigms, new modeling paradigms, and infrastructure that makes AI work better, cheaper, and in more places. Includes AI hardware, novel architectures, embodied AI, edge inference, and AI-for-science platforms.

**A note on Utah:** Utah's machine-intelligence story has two layers. The *frontier* layer — Palladyne AI on embodied/edge inference, Recursion Pharmaceuticals (filed under [health-and-longevity](../health-and-longevity/), world-class ML but their mission is drug discovery), and university research programs — is less visible than the *applied* layer. Silicon Slopes has produced multiple billion-dollar enterprise SaaS companies (Qualtrics, Domo, Weave, Nomi Health, Instructure) that ship AI features to millions of users. Under the wiki's inclusion bar, both layers matter: applied AI at scale is not nothing, and Qualtrics shipping an LLM feature to 18,000+ enterprise customers is a real distribution mechanism for AI into actual workflows.

## Ecosystem context

- **Nomi Health** — Salt Lake City healthcare-payments platform with applied AI in claims and care workflows. Filed under [health-and-longevity](../health-and-longevity/) (planned) once that section is written.
- **Instructure** — Learning platform (Canvas). Applied AI in education tooling. Filed under [education](../education/).
- **Silicon Slopes ecosystem** — Dozens of mid-to-large SaaS companies with real applied-ML teams: Lucid Software, Podium, Pluralsight, Owlet, Vivint, Clozd. Not all earn individual entries, but the density of applied-AI engineering jobs in Utah is larger than the pure-frontier lens suggests.
