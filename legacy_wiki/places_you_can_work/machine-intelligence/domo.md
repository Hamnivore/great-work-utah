# Domo

**Tier:** B — real with significant caveats; outclassed, mid-tier, or commoditizing
**Domain:** Machine intelligence — Applied AI for business intelligence
**Type:** Company (public, NASDAQ: DOMO)
**Stage:** Established (founded 2010, public since 2018)
**HQ:** American Fork, UT
**Founded:** 2010 by Josh James (co-founder of Omniture, later Adobe Analytics)
**Website:** [domo.com](https://www.domo.com)

## Mission

Unify a company's data sources — sales systems, financial systems, marketing platforms, operational databases — and put natural-language, AI-driven analytics in front of business users who don't know SQL, so decisions can be made on real-time data rather than on stale weekly reports.

## Why it matters

Most mid-market and large companies run on a patchwork of SaaS tools whose data doesn't join cleanly. The result is that executives make decisions on weekly or monthly rollups, while the underlying business state has already moved. Domo pitches "real-time, LLM-queryable unified business data" as the answer. They integrate with hundreds of SaaS data sources, push everything into a unified model, and layer natural-language query and AI-generated dashboards on top.

The 2024–2025 pivot has been explicitly toward agentic AI — their "Domo.AI" platform lets business users build AI agents against their own data. Whether this category works long-term is still an open question (every BI vendor is making the same bet, and Snowflake/Databricks are squeezing from above), but Domo is one of the first movers on "agentic BI for mid-market."

On the wiki's inclusion bar: Domo is a real applied-AI company with real customers and a real product — not a frontier research lab, but a distribution vehicle for AI into mid-market decision-making.

## The hard problem

Unified business data is a messy problem because the underlying systems are messy. Schemas change, fields mean different things in different systems, and joining across them without introducing errors requires constant maintenance. Building an LLM layer on top that can answer questions correctly (not hallucinate) requires careful grounding in the actual data model, constrained generation, and visible source attribution. The industry-wide move from dashboards to conversational analytics is still early; Domo has to both build the product and educate the market.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct via deployment to enterprise and mid-market customers. Second-order: if "conversational BI" becomes a standard mode of accessing business data, Domo is one of a handful of companies that pushed the shift.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Data engineers, ML engineers building LLM-grounding pipelines, frontend engineers on analytics UI, product managers with BI domain expertise. Public-company culture, mid-market customer base. Good fit for engineers who want to work on applied LLM integration in a real product, not just a research demo.

## Ownership

Public (NASDAQ: DOMO). Founder Josh James serves as CEO. Mid-cap; the stock has had a volatile history — strong initial IPO, long underperformance in 2022–2024, renewed attention in 2025 as AI narratives re-valued BI vendors. Mission autonomy is moderate; public-company quarterly pressure is real.

## Why this is not ⭐

Crowded market (Snowflake, Databricks, Tableau/Salesforce, Power BI, Looker, ThoughtSpot, Sigma all playing adjacent moves). Domo is not the category leader in BI and has had a difficult public-market run. Applied AI, not research. A real Utah applied-AI employer worth considering for BI/data work, but not a category-defining company.

## Caveats

- Public small/mid-cap volatility
- BI is a crowded and commoditizing market
- Mid-market focus limits upside vs enterprise-focused competitors
- Applied AI — engineers seeking research-frontier work should look elsewhere
