# Palladyne AI

**Tier:** B — real with significant caveats; outclassed, mid-tier, or commoditizing
**Domain:** Machine intelligence — Embodied AI
**Also relevant to:** [Defense & security](../../defense-and-security/)
**Type:** Company (public, NASDAQ: PDYN)
**Stage:** Public (~75 engineers)
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT
**Founded:** 1983 (as Sarcos); pivoted to pure AI software 2024
**Website:** [palladyneai.com](https://www.palladyneai.com)

## Mission

Build AI software that lets robots and drones observe, learn, reason, and act autonomously — at the edge, without cloud dependency.

## Why it matters

Most AI requires a data center. Palladyne's bet is that the most consequential AI will run on the machine itself — in a warehouse, on a battlefield, in a disaster zone — where latency, bandwidth, and connectivity cannot be assumed. Their Pilot software (and its defense variant, SwarmOS) enables robots and drones to make decisions locally, adapt to new environments without retraining in the cloud, and coordinate in swarms. If edge-native embodied AI works, it changes what's possible in manufacturing, logistics, and defense in a way that cloud-dependent systems cannot.

## The hard problem

Running capable AI on constrained edge hardware — with limited compute, no cloud, and a need to adapt to novel situations in real time — is genuinely hard. The additional challenge of multi-robot coordination (swarm behavior without a central coordinator) is an open research problem. Palladyne has a patent portfolio and a decade of robotics experience to draw from, but the software frontier is still being defined.

## Mechanism of impact

A platform: their AI runs on third-party robots and drones (they explicitly support "most commercial robots and UAS"). If the software works, the impact multiplies across every platform that runs it, rather than being limited to hardware they build themselves.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

ML engineers comfortable with embedded/edge constraints, robotics engineers, and people interested in multi-agent coordination. The culture is likely practical-engineering-heavy — they came from building physical exoskeletons and are now writing software for real industrial and defense deployments. 75-person team means individual contributions are visible.

## Ownership

Public company (NASDAQ: PDYN). Shareholders hold formal control, which limits long-horizon mission autonomy. The pivot from Sarcos (hardware) to Palladyne (software) and the subsequent acquisition of defense hardware companies in 2025 suggests the direction is set more by capital markets than founding vision. Management team led by CEO Peter Klein (appointed 2023, not a founder).

## Caveats

- The company has pivoted twice in three years (exoskeletons → AI software → defense tech platform); mission coherence is uncertain
- Public markets create pressure for near-term revenue over long-term research
- "Embodied AI" is a crowded and competitive space nationally (Figure, Physical Intelligence, 1X, Boston Dynamics); Palladyne's edge is the defense contract pipeline and existing IP, not fundamental research
- Revenue ($11M TTM) is small for a public company; financial pressure is real
