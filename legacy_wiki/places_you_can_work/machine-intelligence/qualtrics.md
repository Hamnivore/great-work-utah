# Qualtrics

**Tier:** A — strong; not globally category-defining but excellent
**Domain:** Machine intelligence — Applied AI for experience management at scale
**Also relevant to:** [Tools for thought](../tools-for-thought/)
**Type:** Company (private after 2023 take-private by Silver Lake; was NASDAQ: XM 2021–2023)
**Stage:** Established (founded 2002, ~$1.8B revenue)
**HQ:** Provo, UT (+ Seattle, WA co-HQ)
**Founded:** 2002 by Ryan Smith, Scott Smith, Jared Smith, and Stuart Orgill
**Website:** [qualtrics.com](https://www.qualtrics.com)

## Mission

Turn the gap between what customers and employees actually feel and what organizations measure into something quantifiable, actionable, and improvable — through surveys, sentiment analysis, and increasingly, LLM-driven understanding of unstructured feedback.

## Why it matters

Qualtrics is the biggest software company ever built in Utah (SAP acquired it for $8B in 2018; it went public in 2021 at an $18B valuation; Silver Lake took it private in 2023 for $12.5B). Beyond size, it anchors Silicon Slopes — thousands of engineers in Utah on real production software, a recruiting pipeline from BYU and U of U, a founder-lineage that has seeded other Utah companies (Canopy, Podium, Filevine all have Qualtrics alumni).

On the machine-intelligence angle specifically: Qualtrics sits on one of the largest databases of human experience feedback in the world. Their AI-powered "XM/os" platform applies LLMs to summarize open-text responses, predict churn from signal patterns, route alerts to managers, and auto-draft responses. This is *applied AI at distribution scale* — 18,000+ enterprise customers, including a majority of Fortune 500. Every time an LLM summarizes millions of customer or employee comments into a few actionable themes, and a manager actually acts on them, that's AI making real-world decisions better. Not frontier research. But the surface area for real impact is large.

The reason this matters for the wiki: applied AI at scale is one of the actual mechanisms by which ML changes society. Qualtrics is one of the mid-size platforms through which it happens.

## The hard problem

Customer experience at enterprise scale generates unstructured text, audio, and video — hundreds of millions of feedback items per year across their customer base. Extracting reliable signal (separating genuine complaints from noise, detecting emerging issues before they spread, distinguishing sentiment that maps to action from sentiment that doesn't) requires careful ML engineering. The old approach was keyword-based. The modern approach is LLM-based summarization and classification. Both have to work at low cost per item, with audit trails, and within enterprise data-handling requirements (SOC 2, HIPAA for healthcare customers, FedRAMP for government).

## Mechanism of impact

Direct: AI features deployed into real decision loops at tens of thousands of organizations. Second-order: the category definition of "experience management" as a management discipline is partially Qualtrics's doing. Employees get heard more, customers get listened to more, organizations change what they do based on that feedback — the marginal impact per improvement is small, but the aggregate is real.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

ML engineers, applied scientists, data engineers, platform engineers, product managers — all at scale. The ML team works on LLM integration, classification, retrieval, and enterprise-grade evaluation. Enterprise SaaS culture — more process than a startup, more pace than a big-tech company. Good fit for engineers who want to work on applied AI with real users and are comfortable in the Mountain West.

## Ownership

Private since 2023. Silver Lake-led take-private ($12.5B). Ryan Smith (co-founder, ex-CEO) retains substantial equity and remains connected; also owns the Utah Jazz. SAP had acquired Qualtrics for $8B in 2018 and spun it out again via IPO in 2021. Mission autonomy is moderate — private equity ownership means clear financial expectations.

## Why this is not ⭐

Applied AI, not frontier AI — Qualtrics uses LLMs and ML models that other companies build. They are not training foundation models or doing novel architecture work. Private equity ownership changes the culture and pace in ways that haven't fully played out yet. They are extraordinarily important to Utah's tech ecosystem and to the applied-AI distribution story, but they are not the category-defining company in their space that an ⭐ would imply.

## Caveats

- Silver Lake (PE) ownership — operating cadence and hiring are affected by leveraged-buyout economics
- Applied AI, not research AI — engineers looking to push the ML frontier should look elsewhere
- Enterprise SaaS has matured; the market is segmented among Qualtrics, Medallia, SurveyMonkey, and in-house tools
