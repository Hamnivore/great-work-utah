# UTahQuantum (UTQ)

**Tier:** F — placeholder; concept-stage quantum integrator, no prototype yet
**Domain:** Machine intelligence — Quantum systems integration
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Self-funded; launched October 2025; prototype planned Q1–Q2 2026
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT
**Founded:** 2025 (Sumit Parashar, Neil Nickolaisen, Brig. Gen. (Ret.) Max Stitzer)
**Website:** [utahquantum.org](https://utahquantum.org)

## What it is

Utah's first self-described Quantum Systems Integrator (QSI): a firm trying to connect Utah universities (U of U, UVU, Weber State, USU, Utah Tech), industry, and government (47G, DoD partners) around real-world quantum adoption — encryption, data management, IoT, national security. Think of it as a systems integrator for quantum tech in the way traditional defense integrators connect component makers to government programs.

## Why it might matter

Most quantum computing work is still in hardware labs or cloud services; the integration layer — helping organizations understand what quantum is actually ready for and how to use it — is genuinely underbuilt. If Utah becomes a quantum hub (the U of U has a growing quantum physics program), a local QSI could play a real catalytic role.

## Why this is F tier

No prototype, no revenue, no disclosed investors. Three founders with enterprise/defense backgrounds, not quantum physics. The concept is viable but entirely unproven. Revisit when a prototype exists or a grant is announced.
