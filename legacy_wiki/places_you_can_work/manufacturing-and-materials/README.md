# Manufacturing & Materials

Making physical things better, cheaper, cleaner — advanced materials, novel manufacturing processes, industrial automation, and the boundary between atoms and engineering. Distinct from [biological engineering](../biological-engineering/) (living systems as feedstock) and [energy](../energy/) (power generation and storage).

**A note on Utah:** Utah is a real player in advanced manufacturing, especially composites and medical devices. Hexcel's Salt Lake City carbon fiber plant is globally consequential; Merit Medical's South Jordan HQ builds interventional devices used in clinical care worldwide; ACT Aerospace and the UAMMI cluster represent hundreds of smaller suppliers. The frontier-startup pipeline (metal additive, novel polymers, encrypted compute silicon) is real — EnduraCure on degradable electronics is the most interesting early-stage bet found so far. Utah is not the Boston or Palo Alto of materials science, but the state's aerospace-and-medical-device industrial base is larger and more impactful than most people realize.

## Ecosystem context

- **Texas Instruments — Lehi, UT fab** — $11 billion semiconductor fab under construction in Lehi; the single largest economic investment in Utah's history. Not a startup, but the presence of a major semiconductor manufacturer is reshaping Utah's manufacturing talent pool and supply chain. Expected 800+ direct engineering jobs.
- **UAMMI (Utah Advanced Materials & Manufacturing Initiative)** — Cluster organization, ~100 companies, 12,000+ jobs. [uammi.org](https://www.uammi.org)
- **U of U Powder Metallurgy Lab** — Installed Incus Hammer Lab35 metal 3D printer (2024). Cloud-based parameter-prediction research for metal AM is a genuinely interesting direction. Partners include Applied Materials, Electroninks, NXP, Northrop Grumman.
- **Origen Manufacturing, Advanced Composites Inc, Wilson Aerospace** — Other Utah-based composites suppliers in the mid-tier.
- **Renaissance Ag (Vineyard, UT)** — Hydroponic fodder systems are noted under [food-and-agriculture](../food-and-agriculture/); mentioned here because the vertical-farming hardware side has manufacturing overlap.
