# Sky Quarry

**Tier:** D — mission integrity questionable
**Domain:** Manufacturing & materials — Waste asphalt shingle recycling / heavy oil extraction
**Also relevant to:** [Energy](../energy/)
**Type:** Company (public, NASDAQ: SKYQ)
**Stage:** Public, commercializing (permit application filed June 2025 for PR Spring integrated facility)
**HQ:** Woods Cross / West Valley, UT (headquarters + PR Spring facility in eastern Utah)
**Website:** [skyquarry.com](https://skyquarry.com)

## Mission

Recycle used asphalt shingles — 15M+ tons per year in the US alone — and convert them into useful heavy oil, bitumen, and road-grade asphalt using a solvent-based extraction process (ECOSolv) that recovers 95% of material and 99% of solvent.

## Why it matters

Asphalt shingle waste is one of the larger construction waste streams almost no one thinks about. Most used shingles go to landfill. They are 30% bitumen by weight, mixed with fiberglass, aggregates, and additives. Extracting that bitumen is, in principle, a genuine circular-economy opportunity — you're pulling recoverable fossil products out of waste, reducing both landfill pressure and the need for new petroleum extraction for asphalt.

Sky Quarry's ECOSolv process claims 95% material recovery and 99% solvent recycling. Their PR Spring facility in eastern Utah (oil sands territory) is designed to process 2,000 barrels per day, with projected recovery of ~10 million barrels over 15 years from combined oil sands + recycled shingles.

The *world-changing* angle is genuinely interesting if you focus on the shingle recovery — solving construction waste recycling for one of the bigger waste streams is real. The angle is genuinely mixed if you focus on the oil sands side — the company is simultaneously extracting fresh heavy oil from PR Spring's tar sands. That is additional fossil fuel production, which is the opposite of climate-positive.

## The hard problem

Solvent-based extraction from heterogeneous feedstocks (asphalt shingles vary by manufacturer, age, and roofing application) is a real chemical engineering problem. Recovering 99% of the solvent across many extraction cycles requires careful engineering of the solvent loop, evaporation, and condensation. At larger scale, the logistics of collecting and transporting waste shingles economically is also non-trivial.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct deployment if the facility ramps as planned. The shingle recycling market is underdeveloped; Sky Quarry could be genuinely important here. The oil sands side is business-model ballast; it pays the bills but doesn't advance the mission narrative.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Chemical and process engineers interested in circular-economy applications. Anyone comfortable working on both waste processing *and* fossil fuel extraction — this is not a pure climate-tech environment, and the mission-integrity question is real. If you want to focus purely on the circular side, ask hard questions at the interview about how the oil sands business fits the longer-term roadmap.

## Ownership

Public (NASDAQ: SKYQ). Small-cap; share price and float are thin. Ownership concentration is moderate. The public-market pressure on a small-cap resource company is intense — revenue and deployment timelines matter for survival.

## Why this is not ⭐

The mission has two halves and they partly contradict each other. A S-tier entry here would need either (a) Sky Quarry exiting the oil-sands-fresh-extraction business and becoming a pure shingle-recycling company, or (b) evidence that the heavy oil byproduct of shingle recovery is displacing rather than adding to fossil fuel demand. Neither is established.

## Caveats

- Small-cap public company; share volatility and funding risk are real
- Mission ambiguity — shingle recycling vs. oil sands extraction are in tension
- Permit approval pending at PR Spring (as of mid-2025)
- ECOSolv recovery rates are company-reported, not independently verified at scale
- Oil sands extraction in eastern Utah has a long, contentious history — social license is not guaranteed
