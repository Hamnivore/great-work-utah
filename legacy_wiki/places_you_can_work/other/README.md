# Other

Genuine oddballs — organizations doing work that clears the wiki's bar but doesn't fit the other domains. Reserved for things that are so unusual in structure, mission, or context that forcing them into a first-order or second-order bucket would distort them.

**A note on Utah:** Utah produces exactly this kind of oddball. The state's religious, political, and environmental context creates organizations that don't exist elsewhere: the world's largest genealogy infrastructure operated by a religious denomination, a federal intelligence data center at Bluffdale, a landlocked saline lake whose collapse is one of the ongoing environmental stories of the decade. Not all of these are "companies one could join," but at least one is, and it matters enough to name.

## Ecosystem context

- **FamilySearch → UPDB pipeline** — The Genealogical Society of Utah (1894, now FamilySearch) created the genealogical records that became the Utah Population Database, which enabled the discovery of BRCA1, BRCA2, and a long list of disease genes. The clearest case study on the wiki of a non-obvious institution (a religious genealogy project) enabling world-changing work in an adjacent field (cancer genomics).
