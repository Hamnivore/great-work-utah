# Eden Technologies (Eden Tech)

**Tier:** C — clever retrofit approach to desalination, very early, founder-led
**Domain:** Other — water desalination efficiency
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Seed ($1M+ raised: $750K seed + $250K Utah Innovation Fund, as of 2023)
**Location:** St. George, UT (Utah Tech University spinout)
**Website:** [edentechinc.com](https://edentechinc.com)

## Mission

Double the freshwater yield of existing desalination plants by retrofitting them with a Reverse Osmosis Centrifuge (ROC) — a patented device that applies centrifugal force to the reverse osmosis process, increasing recovery and enabling zero liquid discharge.

## Why it matters

Water scarcity is a direct constraint on civilization — particularly in the American Southwest, Middle East, and North Africa. Desalination is the long-term solution for coastal and brackish water regions, but existing plants are expensive and inefficient (30–50% recovery rates, large brine waste streams). Eden Tech's ROC doesn't require building new plants: it retrofits into existing reverse osmosis infrastructure to achieve a claimed 2x freshwater yield and 15% recovery improvement. If that claim holds at scale, it unlocks existing desalination capacity at low marginal cost. Navajo Nation and Saudi Arabia tests suggest the technology is being taken seriously in two completely different deployment contexts.

## The hard problem

Reverse osmosis relies on pressure differential across a semipermeable membrane; centrifugal force changes the pressure gradient in a way that should allow more water extraction from the brine side. The challenge is membrane compatibility (centrifugal force creates different stress patterns), mineral scaling under zero-liquid-discharge conditions, and energy efficiency (centrifuge motors add power draw). At lab scale the numbers look good; whether they hold in full industrial-scale plants is unproven.

## Ownership

Private. Founders: Hunter Manz (CEO, Utah Tech University ME/Finance) and Zachary Manweiler. Utah Innovation Fund investment (first-time investor in a Utah Tech startup). Very founder-controlled.

## Caveats

- $1M seed is lab/pilot stage only; full industrial demonstration will require orders of magnitude more capital
- Claims (2x freshwater yield) are company-reported from small-scale tests; independent validation at industrial scale has not been published
- Desalination retrofit market has many players; the centrifuge approach is novel but unproven
- Founded 2020 by a 24-year-old with limited industry background — execution risk is real
