# Great Salt Lake

**Tier:** C — included for completeness; honest "look elsewhere" call
**Domain:** Other — Environmental system undergoing collapse, policy and technology challenge
**Also relevant to:** [Energy](../energy/) (lithium brine), [Food & agriculture](../food-and-agriculture/) (agricultural water demand), [Health & longevity](../health-and-longevity/) (dust exposure public health)
**Type:** Natural system / policy problem (not an organization)
**Stage:** Active policy and technical response (multi-decade)
**Location:** Northern Utah (the terminal lake of the Great Basin)

## Why this is in the wiki

The Great Salt Lake is not an organization, and no single company is responsible for it. It is in this wiki because:
1. The lake's collapse is probably the single largest environmental problem the state of Utah faces, and it is the kind of problem that a world-changing company could plausibly be built around.
2. There are currently no Utah companies that clear the wiki's bar as "working on this at scale." That is a gap, and readers considering how to apply their work to high-impact problems should know about it.
3. Adjacent opportunities (lithium extraction from Great Salt Lake brines, dust mitigation technology, water reuse and efficiency technology, saline ecosystem restoration) are all real commercial opportunities that haven't been fully seized.

## The situation

- The lake has lost approximately **73% of its surface area** since 1850 — from ~3,300 square miles at high stand to ~950 square miles at the 2022 record low
- The exposed lakebed contains arsenic, antimony, lanthanum, zirconium, and other heavy metals — these are now blowing into the airshed of 2.5M people along the Wasatch Front
- A 2023 BYU study warned the lake could fully desiccate within 5 years without major reductions in upstream water diversion. Since then, wet winters (2023, 2024) provided temporary reprieve; the underlying trend has not reversed
- Lake salinity is now high enough that brine shrimp (base of the ecosystem, supporting 10M migratory birds and a commercial brine shrimp industry) are threatened
- Primary cause: agricultural water diversion from the lake's tributaries (Bear, Weber, Jordan rivers) — roughly 70% of Utah's water consumption is agricultural, much of that alfalfa exported for beef production

## The opportunities (where companies could fit)

1. **Lithium and mineral extraction** — Great Salt Lake brine contains lithium, magnesium, and potash. If done with direct-lithium-extraction (DLE) techniques that return water to the lake rather than evaporating it, this could be a revenue-generating preservation strategy. Companies currently active: US Magnesium (long-established, not DLE); Lilac Solutions (Oakland, CA) — DLE via ion-exchange ceramic beads, completed a 7-month pilot at GSL in 2024 recovering 87% of available lithium at 99.97% purity, $145M raised, 10-year offtake with Traxys, commercial production target 2027; International Battery Metals (Houston) — DLE plant near Salt Lake City co-located with US Magnesium. Utah HB 453 (2024) bans the evaporation method of mineral extraction from GSL, making DLE the only legal path forward for new lithium ventures. The regulatory and technical conditions for a Utah-based DLE company are now better than they have ever been.
2. **Dust mitigation** — The Owens Lake precedent (LA's dewatered lake in California) required billions of dollars of dust-suppression measures that took decades to make effective. Great Salt Lake's dust problem is 10x larger. There is essentially no company working on this at scale.
3. **Agricultural water efficiency** — Alfalfa produced in Utah for export is the largest single water-consuming activity in the state. A company that made precision irrigation, alternative crops, or dried-hay export economically viable could reduce upstream demand. Some Utah agtech plays adjacent to this; none are directly addressing the Great Salt Lake linkage.
4. **Ecosystem restoration** — Brine shrimp aquaculture, microbialite (the lake's coral-analog bioherms) protection, wetland restoration. Mostly nonprofit territory today (The Nature Conservancy, Friends of Great Salt Lake, Great Salt Lake Collaborative).

## Why no ⭐

It's not a company. And there isn't yet a Utah company clearly on this problem at scale. If one emerges, it earns a star and goes in [energy/](../energy/) or [food-and-agriculture/](../food-and-agriculture/) depending on focus.
