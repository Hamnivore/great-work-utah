# Scientific Infrastructure

Tools, instruments, and platforms that make all downstream scientific progress faster. Includes measurement instruments, scientific software, computing hardware for science, and research data platforms. Distinct from [machine intelligence](../machine-intelligence/) (which targets AI capability itself) and [tools for thought](../tools-for-thought/) (which augment individual human reasoning). This domain is for tools that unblock entire research communities.

**A note on Utah:** This is a surprisingly strong cluster, concentrated at the University of Utah. SCI Institute has shaped scientific visualization as a field for 30+ years. Ripple Neuro builds the recording instruments that every serious invasive neuroscience lab uses. The underlying pattern: Utah does well in research infrastructure because the U of U has kept faculty-led research groups intact long enough for them to become load-bearing for whole fields. These are not venture-scale bets; they are long-horizon institutions.

## Ecosystem context

- **Center for High Performance Computing (CHPC)** — U of U's shared HPC resource. Supports SCI and most computational research at the university. Not a spinout target but a dependency for the above groups.
- **NIH HEAL Initiative** — Federal funding stream that has underwritten several of Ripple Neuro's stimulation system programs. Signal for where federal dollars are landing in neurotech.
