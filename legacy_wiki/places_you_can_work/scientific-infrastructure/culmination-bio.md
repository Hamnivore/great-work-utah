# Culmination Bio

**Tier:** B — genuinely rare data asset; business model still being validated
**Domain:** Scientific infrastructure — Clinical data platform / hospital-system biotech spinout
**Also relevant to:** [Health & longevity](../health-and-longevity/), [Biological engineering](../biological-engineering/)
**Type:** Company (subsidiary of Intermountain Health)
**Stage:** Operating; $10M funding round (Amgen Ventures + Merck Global Health Innovation Fund, 2023)
**HQ:** St. George, UT (Intermountain Health subsidiary)
**Founded:** 2022
**Website:** [culmination.com](https://www.culmination.com)

## Mission

Convert Intermountain Health's 40+ years of longitudinal patient data — clinical records, omics, tissue samples, diagnostic results — into a platform for discovering therapeutic targets, diagnostics, and care process improvements.

## Why it matters

Drug discovery fails primarily because the biological hypotheses tested in trials are wrong. Longitudinal patient data at the population scale — spanning decades, linking clinical outcomes to molecular data — is one of the best available tools for forming better hypotheses. Intermountain Health operates 33 hospitals and 385 clinics across nine states; Culmination Bio has access to 40+ years of de-identified records, more than 9 million archival clinical tissue samples, and ongoing prospective enrollment. Very few institutions in the world have this combination of scale, longitudinality, and molecular depth.

The counterfactual: without Culmination Bio, this data stays siloed inside a hospital system for internal QI projects rather than informing drug discovery pipelines. Merck and Amgen's investment signals that the data is genuinely useful to top-tier pharma.

## The hard problem

De-identification, linkage, and quality of clinical data at scale are unsolved engineering problems. Hospital records are built for care delivery, not research — they are structurally heterogeneous, missing data is systematic, and coding practices change over time. Turning 40 years of Intermountain records into queryable, analytically-grade data while maintaining privacy compliance (HIPAA + IRB) and research utility is a non-trivial data engineering challenge. The additional layer — linking clinical records to omics and tissue samples longitudinally — requires an identity resolution infrastructure that most health systems have not built.

## Mechanism of impact

Foundational tool: Culmination Bio sells data access, paired biospecimen access, and collaborative research partnerships (not drugs). The Merck collaboration (24 months, autoimmune disease focus) is the template. If the platform enables pharma and biotech to de-risk early drug discovery hypotheses using real-world longitudinal data, it compresses the early stages of drug development — the highest-failure-rate stages.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Computational biologists, clinical data scientists, bioinformaticians, and health-data engineers comfortable with EHR data at scale. Also regulatory/IRB specialists and pharma business development people who understand data licensing deals. The Intermountain parent brings institutional scale; the spinout structure enables moving faster than a hospital QI program.

## Ownership

Wholly owned subsidiary of Intermountain Health (nonprofit). Backed additionally by Amgen Ventures and Merck Global Health Innovation Fund ($10M). The nonprofit parent means no short-term VC exit pressure, but the subsidiary structure is still new — alignment between Intermountain's clinical mission and Culmination Bio's commercial data licensing mission will be a long-term governance question.

## Caveats / red flags

- The data asset is genuinely rare, but turning hospital records into drug-discovery-grade data is harder than it looks — early commercial partnerships may not produce the hypothesis validation that pharma partners need
- The $10M raise is modest relative to other data-platform plays; the company may need significantly more capital to build the data infrastructure at the quality level pharma partners require
- Intermountain Health's geographic focus (Intermountain West) means the patient population may be less ethnically diverse than is needed for generalizable drug discovery, particularly for diseases with population-specific biology
- As an Intermountain subsidiary, the company's independence and ability to make bold commercial moves may be constrained by the parent system's priorities
