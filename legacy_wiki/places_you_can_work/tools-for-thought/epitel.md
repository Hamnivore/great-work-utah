**Website:** [epitel.com](https://www.epitel.com)

## Mission

Move EEG seizure monitoring out of hospital EEG units and into ambulatory, at-home settings — via a small wearable patch that records continuous EEG for days or weeks, with AI-assisted review for neurologists.

## Why it matters

Epilepsy diagnosis and treatment depend on capturing seizures on EEG. The problem is that seizures are episodic — many patients never have one during a standard 1–2 hour clinic EEG. The traditional solution is multi-day inpatient EEG monitoring, which is expensive, hospital-bound, and inaccessible for many patients. Epitel's REMI (Remote EEG Monitoring Instrument) is a small, wireless, wearable EEG patch that records continuously for 10+ days while the patient lives their normal life.

This changes the diagnostic funnel for epilepsy: instead of hospitalization for monitoring, a patient goes home with a patch. The recorded data is reviewed with AI assistance to flag likely seizure events, which neurologists then confirm. This unlocks diagnosis for patients who currently cannot access inpatient monitoring (rural, underinsured, can't take time off work, children). It also enables longitudinal monitoring of treatment response, which has been nearly impossible at scale.

Wearable, always-on neurological monitoring is a real frontier. Epitel is one of the early movers in ambulatory EEG, and the category has multiple competitors (Epilog, Neurolutions, Ceribell for ICU variants), but the unmet need is large — ~50M people with epilepsy globally.

## The hard problem

Low-power, low-noise EEG from a few scalp electrodes — collected during normal daily activity with its artifact (movement, muscle, eye blink, electrical interference) — has to be cleaned, analyzed, and presented to a neurologist in a reviewable form. The AI seizure detection models need very high sensitivity (missing seizures is unacceptable) at manageable specificity (too many false alarms overwhelm reviewers). Clinical validation is expensive and slow. Insurance coverage for ambulatory EEG is patchwork.

## Mechanism of impact

Direct clinical deployment. Every epilepsy diagnosis enabled, every medication titration informed by continuous monitoring, every seizure cluster caught earlier is direct impact. Second-order: if wearable EEG becomes routine, it opens diagnostic and research possibilities for other neurological conditions (sleep disorders, migraine, psychiatric comorbidities).

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Biomedical engineers, signal-processing and ML engineers for seizure detection, regulatory affairs specialists, clinical-study operations staff. Small company; individual leverage is high. Good fit for engineers who want to work on wearable medical devices with direct patient impact in Utah.

## Ownership

Private. U of U alumni-founded. Venture funding (undisclosed publicly); standard early-commercial ownership.

## Why this is not ⭐

Early commercialization in a competitive space. The category (ambulatory EEG) has real market pull but multiple well-funded competitors. Epitel has FDA clearance and clinical traction but is not yet the dominant player. Worth watching; earns a star when they either establish category leadership or are acquired by a larger neuro-device company at a price that validates the market.

## Caveats

- Competitive ambulatory EEG market (Epilog, Ceribell ICU variant, Neurolutions, traditional Cadwell/Natus extended)
- Insurance coverage determines deployment ceiling
- Medical device regulatory overhead is real for a small company
- AI-assisted seizure detection has to earn neurologist trust case by case
