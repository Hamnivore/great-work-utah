# Blackrock Neurotech ⭐

**Tier:** S — category-defining; arguably the best place in the world to do this work
**Domain:** Tools for thought — Brain-computer interfaces
**Also relevant to:** [Health & longevity](../../health-and-longevity/)
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Private, growth stage
**HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT
**Founded:** ~2008 (spun out of University of Utah)
**Website:** [blackrockneurotech.com](https://blackrockneurotech.com)

## Mission

Restore lost abilities — movement, speech, vision, hearing, touch — by bridging the brain directly to machines.

## Why it matters

The Utah Electrode Array, invented at the University of Utah, is the most implanted and most studied intracortical BCI electrode in history — documented in dozens of patients for over 30,000 cumulative days. Blackrock manufactures and commercializes this technology. Their MoveAgain system, which translates motor-cortex signals into device commands, has FDA Breakthrough Device designation. Their next-generation device, Neuralace, is a flexible chip with over 10,000 channels — orders of magnitude more than current implants — and could enable a fidelity of brain-machine communication that doesn't yet exist.

The deeper implication: the same interfaces that today restore movement to paralyzed patients are the substrate for genuine cognitive augmentation. The path from "restoring lost speech" to "augmenting healthy human communication" runs through companies like this one.

## The hard problem

Neural interfaces degrade. Scar tissue forms around implanted electrodes, signal quality drops over months, and chronic stability has been the fundamental unsolved problem in BCI for 30 years. Blackrock's Utah Array has more long-term human data than any other system — understanding why it degrades, and how to stop it, is core to the mission. The Neuralace design (flexible, lace-like mesh) is their attempt to solve this by conforming to brain tissue rather than puncturing it.

## Mechanism of impact

Two paths: (1) direct clinical deployment — patients with ALS, spinal cord injuries, and locked-in syndrome regaining functional independence; (2) research platform — the Utah Array is used by hundreds of academic labs globally, making Blackrock a critical infrastructure layer for the entire BCI field.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Neuroscientists, electrical engineers, materials scientists, and signal processing engineers who want their work to reach human patients, not just academic papers. The company has been doing this for 20+ years in Utah — it's not a flashy coastal AI startup, it's a deep-tech company with a long game. People who find the hardware/biology interface genuinely fascinating.

## Ownership

Private, but ownership structure not publicly disclosed. Blackrock was majority-acquired by Minnetronix in 2021, making it a subsidiary — meaning its strategic direction is set by a parent company (Minnetronix is a Minnesota-based medical device manufacturer). This is a meaningful caveat: the mission may be constrained by what's profitable and executable within Minnetronix's portfolio strategy, not what's most scientifically ambitious.

## Caveats

- Minnetronix ownership limits full mission autonomy (see above)
- Neuralink, Synchron, and Precision Neuroscience are well-funded competitors pursuing similar or overlapping goals; the competitive landscape is intensifying
- Medical device timelines are long; the distance from current implants to "cognitive augmentation" is likely decades, not years
- Chronic stability (electrode degradation) remains an unsolved problem — the Neuralace is promising but unproven at scale
