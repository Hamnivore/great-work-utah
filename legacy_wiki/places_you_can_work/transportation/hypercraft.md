# Hypercraft

**Tier:** B — real defense customers, validated powertrain systems, but crowded market and early commercial stage
**Domain:** Transportation — Hybrid-electric powertrain systems / defense mobility
**Also relevant to:** [Defense & security](../defense-and-security/)
**Type:** Company (private)
**Stage:** Series A; $26M raised; $106M post-money valuation
**HQ:** Utah (Provo area)
**Founded:** 2021
**Website:** [hypercraftusa.com](https://hypercraftusa.com)

## Mission

Build software-defined hybrid-electric powertrain systems for military, performance, and industrial vehicles — standardized hardware that OEMs can deploy without having to develop propulsion from scratch.

## Why it matters

Electric and hybrid powertrains confer specific tactical advantages that diesel cannot match: sub-ambient acoustic signatures (quieter than ambient noise, enabling silent insertion missions), near-ambient thermal signatures (harder to detect with IR sensors), and the ability to export high-power electricity from the vehicle in the field. Hypercraft's 240 KW export capacity matters to a military that wants to run radar, communications, and directed energy from forward vehicles.

The company has validated across defense (Air National Guard, Baker Engineering), heavy-duty logistics (NATC), and performance/marine markets — 20+ vehicle platforms as of 2025. The Series A was led by Stalwart Ventures (Provo, UT) with strategic investment from SDF, a subsidiary of EDGE Group (Abu Dhabi — UAE's state defense technology conglomerate). EDGE Group interest signals that the Gulf defense market sees Hypercraft's powertrain as relevant to their own vehicle electrification programs.

## Technology

Hypercraft's product stack:
- **Series hybrid and full electric powertrains** — standardized architecture enabling OEMs to swap powertrains without redesigning the vehicle platform
- **HyperPack** — modular EV battery system
- **HyperLynk™** — software-defined control architecture; open ecosystem for third-party developers to build apps on, positioned as the "iOS" of vehicle propulsion

The standardized, software-defined approach is the core bet: instead of selling bespoke powertrains to individual OEMs, Hypercraft sells a platform that many OEMs can build on.

## What kind of contributor thrives here

Powertrain engineers, power electronics engineers, embedded software engineers (vehicle control systems), and defense program managers who understand military vehicle modernization programs. The company is early-stage, fast-moving, with a technical product that requires deep domain knowledge.

## Caveats / red flags

- Electric powertrain for military vehicles is a crowded field: Oshkosh Defense, AM General, Textron, and multiple startups are competing for the same modernization budgets
- The "software-defined powertrain" positioning is interesting but requires buy-in from OEMs to open up their vehicle architectures — a sales motion that takes longer than selling a complete vehicle
- EDGE Group / SDF investment raises geopolitical questions about IP sharing and technology export to Gulf customers — worth understanding before joining
- Commercial validation is real but still modest in scale; the path from 20+ platforms to a dominant market position is not obvious
