## Summary

A Utah professor with promising deep-tech research should usually start by separating three questions: should this become a company, can federal R&D funding move it forward, and is equity capital appropriate yet? Those questions point to different resources. The first answer may be [Nucleus MarketEdge](../resources/nucleus-marketedge.md), not a pitch meeting.

## First Door

[Nucleus MarketEdge](../resources/nucleus-marketedge.md) is the strongest first recommendation when the work is still in the translation phase. It is designed for professors, PhDs, and postdocs in Utah higher education who need market discovery, startup-formation context, peer structure, and feedback before locking into a venture path.

This is especially useful when the researcher can explain the science but not yet the customer, buyer, first market, or company-formation path.

## If The Research Needs Federal R&D Funding

[Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md) becomes the better next door when the professor or spinout can name a technical R&D uncertainty and may fit SBIR/STTR. It helps with agency-topic matching, proposal development, registrations, budgets, readiness, and review.

[Utah Technology Innovation Funding](../resources/utif.md) is more tactical. It is not a generic startup grant. It becomes relevant when a company has identified an eligible SBIR/STTR solicitation and is close enough to submission timing for microgrant or bridge support to matter.

## If The Company Is Ready For Investment

[Nucleus Fund](../resources/nucleus-fund.md) fits later than MarketEdge or Grow. It belongs in the conversation when the researcher has enough venture shape for diligence: protectability, customer evidence, commercialization route, team needs, and a plausible reason equity capital is the right instrument.

## Where This Can Go Wrong

The common mistake is sending every technical researcher to the same generic funding path. Market discovery, non-dilutive R&D funding, and equity capital solve different problems. A professor with an invention disclosure and no customer evidence probably needs MarketEdge-style translation before investor outreach. A company with a clear agency topic should not treat UTIF like early exploration funding.
