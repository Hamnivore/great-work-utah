## Summary

A Utah student founder should usually start with the entrepreneurship center attached to their own campus, then add outside mentoring when the question becomes customer discovery, business planning, cash flow, or founder operations. This answer is lower confidence because several student-resource pages are still imported stubs and need provider verification.

## Campus First

[University of Utah Lassonde Entrepreneurship Institute](../resources/university-of-utah-lassonde-entrepreneurship-institute.md) and [BYU Rollins Center](../resources/brigham-young-university-byu-rollins-center.md) are the obvious first doors for students at those institutions. Their imported pages describe entrepreneurship communities, funding, marketing and sales help, and start-a-business support.

Because both pages are still low-confidence CSV imports, a live recommendation should verify current programs, deadlines, eligibility, and application routes on the official provider pages.

## Add General Mentoring When The Business Question Is Basic

[Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md) is useful when the student needs basic business planning, viability, financial forecasting, pricing, or growth help. [SCORE Utah](../resources/score-utah.md) is useful when the missing piece is practical mentorship and a second set of eyes from experienced volunteers.

## If The Student Is Commercializing Research

If the work is tied to university research rather than a conventional student startup, the student may need the research-commercialization route instead: [Commercialize Research in Utah](../guides/commercialize-research.md), [Nucleus MarketEdge](../resources/nucleus-marketedge.md), or [Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md), depending on stage and SBIR/STTR fit.
