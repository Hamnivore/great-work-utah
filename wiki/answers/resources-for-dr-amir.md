> **Synthetic persona.** Dr. Amir is a composite test persona: a 29-year-old University of Utah PhD candidate with novel non-invasive imaging technology who wants to commercialize but has never started a business. He is not a real person.

## Short Answer

Start with [Nucleus MarketEdge](../resources/nucleus-marketedge.md) to test whether the imaging work has a real market and company path. Pair that with [Commercialize Research in Utah](../guides/commercialize-research.md) and Utah research-to-company examples so Amir can see what translation actually looked like here. Add [Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md) only after the R&D question and agency fit are clearer.

## Why

Amir's bottleneck is usually not "find investors." It is separating three questions that get collapsed: should this become a company, can federal R&D funding move it forward, and is equity capital appropriate yet? A fourth-year PhD with strong science and no startup vocabulary needs translation support before pitch meetings.

The Utah pattern is visible in public evidence: serious university work became companies, patents, and institutions when someone helped bridge lab credibility to market language. [Myriad Genetics and BRCA Testing](../work/myriad-genetics-brca.md) and [Utah Computer Graphics Program](../work/utah-computer-graphics-program.md) are useful anchors — not because Amir's path will look identical, but because they show Utah research can become durable economic work when commercialization is treated as its own discipline.

## Recommended First Calls

1. **[Nucleus MarketEdge](../resources/nucleus-marketedge.md)** — best first door when the science is promising but customer, buyer, formation path, and pitch story are still forming.
2. **[Commercialize Research in Utah](../guides/commercialize-research.md)** — route map for MarketEdge, Grow, UTIF, Nucleus Fund, regulatory relief, and government-customer paths.
3. **[University of Utah Lassonde Entrepreneurship Institute](../resources/university-of-utah-lassonde-entrepreneurship-institute.md)** — useful campus-side entrepreneurship programming while still a student; verify current eligibility on the official site.
4. **[Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md)** — add when Amir can name a technical R&D uncertainty and plausible SBIR/STTR agency fit.
5. **[Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md)** — parallel support for basic business planning, viability, and founder operations when the obstacle is practical rather than scientific.

## Caveats

- Amir should coordinate early with his PI and university technology-transfer office even when using outside programs. This answer does not replace institutional disclosure rules.
- Several campus and Startup State import pages remain stubs or low-confidence. Verify current programs, deadlines, and eligibility on official provider sites before relying on them in a live recommendation.
- Investor outreach is usually premature before market discovery. Sending every technical researcher to the same funding path is the common mistake this answer is trying to prevent.
