> **Synthetic persona.** Jordan is a composite test persona: a 20-year-old in Salt Lake City with a startup idea but no business yet, looking for first steps. He is not a real person.

## Short Answer

Jordan should start with [Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md) and [SCORE Utah](../resources/score-utah.md) for free, plain-language help turning an idea into a testable plan. If Jordan is a university student, add the campus entrepreneurship center — for example [University of Utah Lassonde Entrepreneurship Institute](../resources/university-of-utah-lassonde-entrepreneurship-institute.md). Younger founders can also explore [Teen Entrepreneur Support Center](../resources/teen-entrepreneur-support-center.md).

## Why

At pre-revenue, the risk is not missing the perfect investor. It is building conviction without customer evidence, basic financial literacy, or a mentor who can explain what "starting a business" actually means week to week. Jordan needs low-friction, low-jargon doors that reward curiosity rather than requiring a pitch deck.

Salt Lake City has both statewide counseling and campus entrepreneurship communities. The right first path depends on whether Jordan is enrolled in school, still a teenager, or exploring independently.

## Recommended First Calls

1. **[Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md)** — best statewide first stop for viability, business planning, market testing, pricing, and basic founder operations.
2. **[SCORE Utah](../resources/score-utah.md)** — volunteer mentors for practical feedback from people who have operated businesses.
3. **[University of Utah Lassonde Entrepreneurship Institute](../resources/university-of-utah-lassonde-entrepreneurship-institute.md)** — if Jordan is a U of U student; entrepreneurship community, workshops, seed funding paths, and launch support.
4. **[Utah Valley University Entrepreneur Institute](../resources/utah-valley-university-uvu-entrepreneur-institute.md)** — if Jordan is a UVU student.
5. **[Teen Entrepreneur Support Center](../resources/teen-entrepreneur-support-center.md)** — if Jordan is still in the teen-founder range and wants mentorship and community oriented to young entrepreneurs.
6. **[Find Business Services](../guides/find-business-services.md)** — when the question shifts from "should I start?" to "who helps with legal, accounting, or formation?"

## Caveats

- Several campus and teen-entrepreneur pages are still imported stubs. Verify current programs, eligibility, and application routes on official provider sites.
- Jordan does not need accelerators, angel groups, or SBIR strategy on day one. Those paths become relevant after a basic business hypothesis exists.
- If Jordan's idea is software-only with no Utah-specific angle, the wiki's strongest value is still practical formation help and local community — not deep-tech venture routing.
