> **Synthetic persona.** Maria is a composite test persona: a 38-year-old woman-owned agricultural operator near St. George in Washington County, scaling a small rural business. She is not a real person.

## Short Answer

Maria should start with [Women's Business Center of Utah](../resources/womens-business-center-of-utah.md) for free advising and women-founder community, add [Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md) for practical growth planning, and use [Utah Center for Rural Development](../resources/utah-center-for-rural-development.md) when the question is rural jobs, incentives, or county-level economic development. Local doors include [St. George Area Chamber of Commerce](../resources/st-george-area-chamber-of-commerce.md) and [Utah Department of Agriculture and Food](../resources/utah-department-of-agriculture-and-food.md).

## Why

Maria's needs differ from a pre-revenue software founder in Lehi. She is scaling an operating business in a rural county, which means community context, capital readiness, distribution, and local economic-development programs matter as much as generic startup programming. Women founders also benefit from support environments designed for their stage and context — not because the business questions are different, but because access and trust often are.

Southern Utah has its own chamber, city economic-development, and agricultural policy paths. A statewide answer that only points to Wasatch Front accelerators will feel irrelevant.

## Recommended First Calls

1. **[Women's Business Center of Utah](../resources/womens-business-center-of-utah.md)** — free advising, trainings, templates, and community; public pages list both Salt Lake City and Cedar City offices plus online routes.
2. **[Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md)** — grounded counseling on planning, cash flow, pricing, operations, hiring, and lender readiness for an existing operator.
3. **[Utah Center for Rural Development](../resources/utah-center-for-rural-development.md)** — rural grants, incentives, and economic-development programs through GOEO; verify current programs on the official site.
4. **[St. George Area Chamber of Commerce](../resources/st-george-area-chamber-of-commerce.md)** — local business network and Washington County visibility.
5. **[St. George City Economic Development](../resources/st-george-city-economic-development.md)** — city-level economic development context for the St. George area.
6. **[Utah Department of Agriculture and Food](../resources/utah-department-of-agriculture-and-food.md)** — state agricultural programs, licensing, and food-production context relevant to scaling an ag operation.
7. **[Utah Microloan Fund (UMLF)](../resources/utah-microloan-fund-umlf.md)** — possible capital path once the business case is clear enough for a microloan conversation; verify eligibility.

## Caveats

- [Utah Center for Rural Development](../resources/utah-center-for-rural-development.md), chamber pages, and several GOEO imports remain low-confidence stubs. Verify current grants, deadlines, and eligibility before making live referrals.
- This answer is for a scaling rural operator, not a deep-tech venture path. If Maria's agricultural work becomes a research-backed product company, she may also need [Commercialize Research in Utah](../guides/commercialize-research.md).
- [Suazo Business Center](../resources/suazo-business-center.md) may be relevant for multicultural entrepreneur support in Utah, but its wiki page is still an unverified import and is not promoted here as a primary recommendation.
