## Why

Operators like Priya are not looking for volume. They are looking for a mission fit specific enough to justify leaving a comfortable role. LinkedIn optimizes for title matching and recruiter volume. The wiki optimizes for *why this work matters*, what evidence supports it, and which Utah institutions are unusually strong fits for a particular background.

The right next step is usually not "apply everywhere." It is narrowing to a small set of places where Priya's experience — regulated products, ML infrastructure, manufacturing discipline, geoscience, embedded systems — would be rare leverage rather than interchangeable staff.

## Caveats

- Venture pages describe organizations and work; they do not confirm open roles, compensation, hiring urgency, or willingness to talk. Treat them as discovery anchors, not job postings.
- "Meaningful work" labels are contextual rankings for operators, not universal scores on the organizations themselves.
- Priya may also be valuable as a helper or fractional executive. The wiki's people and helper layers are thinner than its venture and resource layers; raising a hand may matter as much as browsing.
