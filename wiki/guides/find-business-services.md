# Find Business Services In Utah

**Status:** Draft
**Confidence:** Medium
**Audience:** founders, operators, researchers, service providers, helpers
**Focus:** business services, procurement, regulation, capital, workforce, international expansion
**Updated:** 2026-06-18
**Layout:** field-guide

## Overview

This guide is for founders who do not need a motivational resource list. They need to know what kind of professional or institutional help fits the problem in front of them.

Criteria for this first pass: public evidence, Utah specificity, practical next step, and whether the service changes the founder's path. The guide does not rank providers universally. It groups help by bottleneck.

## If The Bottleneck Is Basic Business Formation

[Utah SBDC](../resources/utah-sbdc.md) is the clearest broad first stop when a founder needs help with business planning, startup guidance, market strategy, pricing, financial forecasting, funding readiness, operations, or growth.

Use it for: first-time founders, rural operators, Main Street companies, manufacturers, and businesses where the immediate problem is making the company work rather than raising venture capital.

## If The Bottleneck Is Mentorship

[SCORE Utah](../resources/score-utah.md) is a better fit when the founder needs an experienced mentor or workshop rather than a state-program application. It can help founders talk through business planning, marketing, operations, leadership, and growth tradeoffs.

Use it for: founders with broad questions, owners who want ongoing guidance, and operators who need practical judgment from people who have built or run businesses.

## If The Bottleneck Is SBA Navigation

[SBA Utah District Office](../resources/sba-utah-district-office.md) belongs in the path when the question touches SBA funding programs, federal contracting certifications, disaster recovery, lenders, or SBA partner organizations.

Use it for: founders comparing loans with equity, veteran-owned or manufacturing businesses considering government contracting, and companies that need the federal small-business doorway rather than a generic advisor.

## If The Bottleneck Is Professional Services (Legal, IP, Finance, Regulatory)

The wiki now has first-class helper pages for firms founders hire directly. Match the bottleneck before the brand.

**Startup counsel and venture financings:** [Kirton McConkie](../helpers/kirton-mcconkie.md) for Utah corporate formation, venture financing, and securities work. [Parsons Behle & Latimer](../helpers/parsons-behle-latimer.md) for technology transactions, licensing, and emerging-company securities. [Wilson Sonsini (Salt Lake City)](../helpers/wilson-sonsini-salt-lake-city.md) for life-sciences and venture-backed companies that need national venture counsel.

**Patents and technology transactions:** [Workman Nydegger](../helpers/workman-nydegger.md) for patent prosecution and IP litigation. [Maschoff Brennan](../helpers/maschoff-brennan.md) for Utah deep-tech teams that need licensing, software/biotech IP strategy, or technology transactions.

**Accounting, tax, and audit:** [Tanner LLC](../helpers/tanner-llc.md) for growth-stage companies that need audit readiness, R&D tax credits, SOC/ISO support, or transaction advisory — not just annual tax prep. [Eide Bailly (Utah)](../helpers/eide-bailly-utah.md) when a national CPA footprint with fractional CFO/controller and R&D credit support is a better fit.

**Fractional CFO and finance operations:** [Preferred CFO](../helpers/preferred-cfo.md) or [NOW CFO](../helpers/now-cfo.md) when the company needs controller-level or CFO-level help before a full-time hire.

**FDA / medtech regulatory:** [RQM+](../helpers/rqm-plus.md) for medical-device and diagnostic founders navigating quality systems, clinical strategy, and reimbursement.

**SBIR/STTR and grant consulting:** [BBCetc](../helpers/bbcetc.md) when the bottleneck is federal nondilutive funding proposals, not equity capital.

Use [Nucleus Institute](../helpers/nucleus-institute.md) when the founder is still deciding whether the work should become a company at all, or needs Utah-specific commercialization routing before hiring outside counsel. For mentor-style help before hiring firms, see [Find An Advisor In Utah](find-an-advisor.md).

## If The Bottleneck Is Women-Founder Support

[Women's Business Center of Utah](../resources/womens-business-center-of-utah.md) is a strong fit for Utah women entrepreneurs who want free advising, trainings, tools, templates, and a community designed around women business owners.

Use it for: women founders at the idea, startup, or growth stage who need practical business help and a support environment that understands their context.

## If The Bottleneck Is Government Sales

[Utah APEX Accelerator](../resources/apex-accelerator.md) is the best first public resource in the current wiki for procurement readiness. It fits companies that can plausibly sell to defense, federal, state, or local government buyers and need help with registrations, certifications, bid discovery, solicitations, and government-market strategy.

Use it for: defense-adjacent startups, manufacturers, veteran-owned businesses, cyber or software teams with public-sector use cases, and suppliers trying to understand RFPs or RFQs.

## If The Bottleneck Is A State Regulation

[Utah Office of Regulatory Relief](../resources/utah-office-of-regulatory-relief.md) is the right page when the product may be valuable but state rules create a specific barrier. The founder should be able to name the regulation, the proposed demonstration, the customer benefit, the risk controls, and the evidence the test would generate.

Use it for: regulated-market startups, healthcare operations concepts, fintech-adjacent services, mobility services, title or real-estate services, and other companies that may need a sandbox rather than a generic advisor.

## If The Bottleneck Is Credit Access

[Utah Small Business Credit Initiative](../resources/utah-small-business-credit-initiative.md) belongs in recommendations for businesses that need lender-backed capital, not venture capital. It is especially relevant when a company is rural, very small, underserved, women-owned, asset-backed, equipment-heavy, or credit-constrained but can work through a lender.

Use it for: manufacturers, fabrication shops, Main Street growth companies, rural businesses, and companies with expansion or equipment needs.

## If The Bottleneck Is Workforce And Global Talent

[Utah Center For Global Talent](../resources/utah-center-for-global-talent.md) is useful when a company is facing workforce shortages and needs to understand global-talent pathways, immigrant workforce integration, credentialing issues, or employer-facing resources.

Use it for: healthcare, manufacturing, STEM, education, hospitality, logistics, and other employers that can hire responsibly but need better context and referrals.

## If The Bottleneck Is International Expansion

[International Trade And Diplomacy](../resources/international-trade-and-diplomacy.md) is the state-context page for companies looking beyond Utah and U.S. customers. The practical program path likely runs through World Trade Center Utah for trade missions, trade shows, workshops, and grants.

Use it for: growth-stage companies with exportable products, existing customers, clear market-entry hypotheses, or sector fit with current trade missions.

## Common Mismatches

Do not send a pre-product founder to international expansion support just because the product could someday be global. Do not send a regulated founder to generic startup advice if the actual blocker is a legal sandbox question. Do not send a government-sales company straight to investors when the company needs procurement readiness.

The wiki should name the founder's bottleneck before naming the resource.
