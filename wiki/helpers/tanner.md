## Summary

Tanner is a Utah-rooted CPA and consulting firm with headquarters in Salt Lake City and Lehi. The public evidence makes it most relevant to this wiki as an accounting, tax, audit, and advisory helper for growing companies that need more than basic bookkeeping.

Its official site lists audit, tax, advisory, outsourced accounting, transaction advisory, R&D tax credits, SOC, ISO, HITRUST, cybersecurity, IT assurance, cloud finance / FinOps, technical accounting, AI and business systems advisory, and startup consulting.

## Impact

Deep-tech founders can create finance and compliance problems early: R&D tax-credit documentation, grant or contract accounting, investor diligence, SOC or ISO readiness, cybersecurity questionnaires, revenue recognition, audit preparation, and acquisition diligence all become harder if the finance stack is informal. Tanner appears relevant when a Utah company needs CPA-firm depth rather than only fractional bookkeeping.

## How They Help

Publicly listed services include outsourced accounting, transaction advisory, quality of earnings, M&A advisory, M&A tax strategy, transaction readiness, IT auditing, cybersecurity consulting, penetration testing, cloud finance / FinOps, technical accounting advisory, AI and business systems advisory, public and private-company assurance, SOC 1, SOC 2, HITRUST, ISO audits, public and private-company tax, international tax compliance, transfer pricing, R&D tax credits, and state and local tax.

## Best-Fit Founders

Likely fits include Utah companies approaching a first serious audit, preparing for institutional financing or acquisition diligence, claiming R&D tax credits, managing multi-state or international tax questions, pursuing SOC/ISO/HITRUST readiness, or moving from founder-managed accounting to a more controlled finance operation.

The fit is weaker when the need is only inexpensive bookkeeping, a simple annual tax return, or an interim CFO rather than CPA-firm audit/tax/advisory support.

## Cost / Engagement Model

The public site does not state pricing. Founders should verify whether Tanner offers right-sized startup engagements, whether the work is audit/tax/advisory/outsourced-accounting, and which services require independence boundaries.

## Proof Points

The official site says Tanner is rooted in Utah with headquarters in Salt Lake City and Lehi. Its service navigation is unusually broad across tax, assurance, advisory, IT assurance, cybersecurity, R&D credits, SOC, ISO, HITRUST, and outsourced accounting.

## Good Matches

Tanner appears especially relevant when a founder asks, "Will this survive investor diligence, tax review, security review, or an audit?" That is a different need from "Can someone reconcile last month's books?"

## Cautions

Do not imply Tanner is available, affordable, or the right CPA firm for a particular startup without verification. Audit and advisory independence rules can affect what one firm can do for the same client. Founders should ask what work Tanner can perform, what work should be separated, and who owns day-to-day finance operations.
