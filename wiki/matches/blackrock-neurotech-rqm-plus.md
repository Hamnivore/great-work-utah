## Recommendation

RQM+ is a medtech CRO with public depth in regulatory affairs, quality systems, clinical trials, and FDA strategy for implantable medical devices. Blackrock Neurotech is a Utah BCI company manufacturing the most-studied intracortical electrode in history, with FDA Breakthrough Device designation for its MoveAgain system. The overlap — Class III implantable neural interfaces, long clinical timelines, and sustained regulatory engagement — makes this worth examining if Blackrock is building or augmenting its regulatory and quality-systems capacity.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Blackrock's MoveAgain system carries FDA Breakthrough Device designation, which means active FDA engagement, potential expedited review, and sustained regulatory interaction well before approval. Implantable neural-interface devices are among the most complex Class III device categories: the evidence base is small, chronic safety data is expensive to generate, and QMS rigor must hold across manufacturing and clinical study execution.

RQM+'s public materials describe support for medical devices, emerging medtech and startups, regulatory and quality, and clinical trials. It explicitly lists outsourcing, professional consulting, and staff augmentation for RA/QA execution — useful models for a growth-stage company managing a complex Class III development program without a fully scaled regulatory department.

## What Blackrock Neurotech Needs

Based on public evidence, Blackrock's regulatory needs likely include:
- Sustained FDA Breakthrough Device program interaction, including meeting preparation and response management
- Design control and quality-management-system support that meets Class III implantable-device standards
- Clinical protocol support for long-term post-market surveillance studies
- Regulatory engineers and RA/QA staff with implantable-device experience (own or contractor)
- Biocompatibility and materials-testing support for new device generations, including Neuralace flexible-mesh designs

## What RQM+ Appears To Offer

Based on public materials, RQM+ describes support for:
- Regulatory and quality, including FDA strategy, quality-management systems, design controls, and submission support
- Clinical trials, including protocol development and clinical study support
- Lab and material science, which could include biocompatibility testing relevant to implantable-device materials
- Emerging medtech and startups as an explicit segment
- Staff augmentation for RA/QA functions, which can bridge gaps without permanent hiring

## Risks and Unknowns

- RQM+'s public materials do not specifically name brain-computer interfaces, neural devices, or intracortical electrodes. Blackrock should verify whether the firm has direct experience with Class III implantable neural interfaces, or whether its implantable-device work skews toward cardiovascular, orthopedic, or other categories.
- Blackrock's Tether-majority ownership structure is unusual. A CRO partner should understand how governance and budget approval processes may differ from typical VC-backed medtech companies.
- Match strength is Plausible rather than Strong because no public evidence confirms an existing or prior relationship between the two organizations.

## Suggested Next Step

A connector or Blackrock's regulatory leadership could request a capabilities brief from RQM+ specifically scoped to Class III implantable neural interfaces, covering their bench and regulatory-staff experience with this device category, engagement models, and pricing ranges for early-stage regulatory support.
