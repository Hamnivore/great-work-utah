## Recommendation

Blackrock Neurotech is among the clearest University of Utah deep-tech spinout success stories in the state: the Utah Electrode Array invented at U of U, decades of clinical implantation history, and FDA Breakthrough Device designation for MoveAgain. Nucleus Fund publicly targets early-stage equity for Utah university-originated deep-tech ventures in fields including life sciences and medical-adjacent technology. On paper the lineage fits. In practice, Blackrock's April 2024 majority strategic stake from Tether ($200M through Tether Evo) likely reshapes whether Nucleus Fund capital is relevant, wanted, or even accessible — treat this as a plausible historical-fit match worth verifying, not an obvious next capital call.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Blackrock manufactures and commercializes the most-implanted, most-studied intracortical BCI electrode platform in history, with documented use across more than 30,000 cumulative implantation days and a next-generation Neuralace flexible-mesh device in development. That is precisely the kind of long-horizon, IP-heavy, university-born hardware bet Utah's innovation ecosystem produces and that generic startup capital often misunderstands.

Nucleus Fund's public positioning — early-stage equity, mentorship, commercialization resources, and networks for ventures emerging from Utah universities and research institutions — aligns with how Blackrock started: founded in 2008 by Florian Solzbacher and Marcus Gerhardt as a U of U spinout commercializing Richard Normann's Utah Array. For a company at that origin story, a Utah fund designed to bridge research institutions and global markets is a logical capital path to evaluate.

## What Blackrock Neurotech Needs

Based on the public venture page, Blackrock likely needs:

- Sustained capital for Neuralace development, chronic-stability engineering, and manufacturing scale for next-generation flexible arrays.
- Neural engineers, materials scientists, signal-processing and ML engineers, and regulatory staff for Class III implantable devices.
- FDA Breakthrough Device program engagement and long-horizon clinical-operations leadership.
- Corporate-governance and finance advisors who can work inside an unusual majority-owner structure.
- Strategic partners and talent pipelines within Utah's neuroengineering cluster (U of U neural engineering, SCI Institute, Ripple Neuro, Iris Biomedical).

## What Nucleus Fund Appears To Offer

From public sources, Nucleus Fund describes:

- Early-stage equity investment for Utah deep-tech ventures emerging from universities and research institutions.
- Focus areas that include life sciences, medical-adjacent technology, and other emerging deep-tech sectors.
- Mentorship, networks, commercialization resources, and operator matching.
- Connections to legal, financial, and marketing support through the Nucleus ecosystem.
- An application path through the fund's official dealflow portal.

## Risks and Unknowns

- **Tether majority ownership is the central capital-fit question.** In April 2024, Tether took a $200 million majority strategic stake through Tether Evo. That influx may satisfy Blackrock's near-term capital needs and change governance, budget approval, and which external investors are welcome — Nucleus Fund may be redundant or structurally awkward as an equity source today.
- Blackrock is a mature growth company with global clinical track record, not an early-stage spinout seeking a first institutional check. Nucleus Fund's sweet spot may be pre-scale university ventures; Blackrock may be past that window regardless of ownership.
- Implantable BCI development requires specialized medtech and crossover investors with Class III device diligence depth. Whether Nucleus Fund has portfolio experience at this regulatory complexity is not established from public sources.
- The Tether relationship introduces reputational and governance considerations some institutional co-investors avoid; Nucleus Fund's LP base and appetite for co-investing alongside or after a crypto-treasury majority owner is unknown.
- Nucleus Fund check sizes, terms, and current investment pace are not public. This page does not imply Blackrock needs Nucleus Fund capital, that the fund would invest, or that any relationship exists.

## Suggested Next Step

Before any application, a connector or Blackrock finance lead should determine internally whether external Utah early-stage equity is on the roadmap given Tether's majority stake and Neuralace funding priorities. If external capital remains relevant — for example, a subsidiary spinout, a specific program carve-out, or a prior relationship worth maintaining — confirm Nucleus Fund's stage and check-size window through the official site; otherwise, treat this match as archival context for how U of U BCI spinouts map to Utah capital paths rather than an active recommendation.
