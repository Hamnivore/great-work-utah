## Recommendation

BBCetc is worth checking for a Curza-like grant-heavy biotech leader who already has a plausible federal R&D path and needs execution support beyond first-step local guidance. Chad Testa's public Curza profile centers on antimicrobial-resistance work funded through NIH, DTRA, CARB-X, and impact capital, so the match is strongest around agency-specific proposal strategy, SBIR/STTR training, and post-award grant management.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Testa operates in a market where standard pharma financing is unusually weak: new Gram-negative antibiotics have high public-health value but difficult commercial economics. Curza's public page therefore describes a company funded primarily through government grants and mission-aligned capital rather than conventional venture rounds.

BBCetc publicly focuses on SBIR/STTR and federal R&D funding support. Its service list includes proposal assistance, agency-specific training, post-award grants/contracts management, TABA, and CMMC. That is a practical complement for a technical biotech team once it has moved beyond "should we apply?" into "which solicitation, which agency, what budget, and how do we manage the award?"

## What Chad Testa Appears To Need

Based on the public person and venture pages, Testa and Curza likely need:

- Continued non-dilutive funding pathways for AMR, biodefense, and infectious-disease R&D.
- Proposal execution help when federal solicitations have narrow technical, budget, or compliance requirements.
- Post-award management support if government contracts and grants remain a core operating model.
- Coordination between federal funding, impact capital, and University of Utah licensing or commercialization relationships.

## What BBCetc Appears To Offer

BBCetc's public materials describe:

- SBIR/STTR proposal and training support across agencies including NIH, DoD, NSF, and others.
- Research-grant assistance for innovative small businesses.
- Post-award grants/contracts management.
- TABA and CMMC support that may matter for defense-adjacent or DoD-funded work.

For Utah companies, this should be sequenced after or alongside local free support such as [Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md) and [Utah Technology Innovation Funding](../resources/utif.md), not treated as a replacement for them.

## Risks and Unknowns

- No public wiki source confirms that Testa, Curza, or BBCetc have worked together.
- BBCetc is national and paid; Curza should compare cost and scope against Nucleus Grow, UTIF, CARB-X resources, and direct agency guidance.
- Curza's largest public funding relationships may be DTRA, CARB-X, NIH, and impact investors rather than classic SBIR/STTR. The fit depends on the next specific solicitation.
- AMR proposals require deep science and biodefense context; BBCetc's general federal-funding capability should be checked against infectious-disease and DTRA-relevant experience.

## Suggested Next Step

A Curza grant or operations lead should identify the next target federal solicitation, confirm whether local Nucleus support is already sufficient, then ask BBCetc for a scoped conversation around agency fit, proposal responsibilities, post-award management, and experience with AMR or biodefense proposals.
