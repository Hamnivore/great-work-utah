## Recommendation

Coreform is a Utah engineering-simulation company commercializing isogeometric analysis (IGA) — research lineage tied to the computational-methods community around University of Utah institutes such as SCI — with non-dilutive DOE SBIR Phase II traction and a credible case for equity capital to scale simulation-software adoption. Nucleus Fund publicly invests in early-stage Utah deep-tech ventures emerging from universities and research institutions, including advanced manufacturing and software-adjacent technical bets. That combination makes Nucleus Fund a plausible Utah-based equity path if Coreform is ready to move beyond grant-funded R&D toward commercial scale.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Coreform attacks a durable bottleneck in computer-aided engineering: mesh generation and geometry defeaturing consume analyst time before any physics is solved. Its IGA workflow — running finite element analysis directly on fully featured CAD geometry — is technically ambitious, IP-relevant, and tied to a decades-long academic research thread. A DOE SBIR Phase II award coupling Coreform Flex to Idaho National Laboratory's MOOSE nuclear simulation framework signals that the technology is being evaluated in safety-critical domains, not only marketed as a workflow improvement.

Nucleus Fund's public materials describe exactly this profile: Utah-based, research-backed, technically deep, and needing equity plus commercialization support rather than a workshop or small grant. Coreform's open question about funding mix beyond SBIR — noted on the venture page — is the gap Nucleus Fund is positioned to fill if the company is at the right stage.

## What Coreform Needs

Based on the public venture page, Coreform likely needs:

- Equity capital to scale go-to-market, customer success, and application engineering beyond SBIR-funded development.
- Computational scientists, numerical analysts, FEM specialists, and CAD-kernel developers to extend solver capability on production geometry.
- CAE-industry advisors and aerospace, automotive, and nuclear-program relationships that convert technical wins into recurring revenue.
- IP counsel with simulation-software experience as the product line matures.
- A capital partner that understands long sales cycles, certification-sensitive customers, and the difference between research-grade solvers and production CAE adoption.

## What Nucleus Fund Appears To Offer

From public sources, Nucleus Fund describes:

- Early-stage equity investment for Utah deep-tech ventures emerging from universities and research institutions.
- Focus areas including advanced manufacturing, AI, and other emerging technologies — adjacent to engineering-simulation software.
- Mentorship, networks, and commercialization resources beyond capital.
- Operator matching and introductions to legal, financial, and marketing support.
- An official application path through an external dealflow portal.

## Risks and Unknowns

- The wiki venture page describes Coreform as adjacent to University of Utah research institutes rather than confirming a formal university spinout or licensing arrangement. Nucleus Fund eligibility may depend on how the fund defines "emerging from" a research institution — worth clarifying upfront.
- Coreform was founded in 2014; it may be further along than a typical pre-seed Nucleus Fund target even if it has not raised significant venture equity publicly.
- Simulation software competes against entrenched CAE incumbents and well-funded alternatives; Nucleus Fund's portfolio experience in CAE go-to-market is not established from public sources.
- Current funding mix, revenue stage, and ownership structure are open questions on the venture page; without that context, check-size fit is hard to assess.
- Nucleus Fund check sizes, terms, and diligence timelines are not public. This page does not imply the fund has agreed to invest or that any relationship currently exists.

## Suggested Next Step

A Coreform founder would confirm Nucleus Fund's definition of university-connected eligibility, then submit a fit inquiry through the official portal summarizing the IGA research lineage, DOE SBIR MOOSE coupling milestone, target customer segments (nuclear, aerospace, automotive), and the specific equity use — likely application engineering, enterprise sales, and solver hardening for certified workflows.
