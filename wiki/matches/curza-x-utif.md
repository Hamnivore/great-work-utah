## Recommendation

University-connected antibiotic developers with an active SBIR/STTR pipeline — like Curza, which has been funded primarily through government grants and impact capital rather than traditional pharma VC — should verify whether [Utah Technology Innovation Funding](../resources/utif.md) can offset proposal costs or bridge Phase I to Phase II. UTIF is a small but strategically useful state program when a federal solicitation is already identified.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Curza's public story is grant-heavy: DTRA and other non-dilutive funders appear central to advancing CZ-02 toward IND. That profile matches UTIF's design — microgrants after Phase I proposal submission and nonrecourse bridge loans for Phase I winners preparing Phase II — better than it matches generic equity funds.

Because Curza is a University of Utah College of Science spinout, the team likely already navigates federal registrations (SAM.gov, SBIR.gov) and Nucleus commercialization paths. UTIF sits in that same Utah innovation-funding stack as [Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md), making it a natural next door to check once a specific open solicitation is chosen.

## What Curza Needs

Based on the public venture page, Curza likely needs:

- Continued non-dilutive capital for preclinical and IND-enabling work on CZ-02 and follow-on ribosome programs.
- Proposal-support funding for SBIR/STTR submissions tied to AMR, biodefense, or infectious-disease solicitations.
- Bridge financing between Phase I award and Phase II proposal preparation if Curza wins federal Phase I funding.
- Utah program navigation alongside CARB-X, DTRA, and university licensing contacts already named on the venture page.

## What UTIF Appears To Offer

From public Nucleus program materials:

- Microgrants for eligible Utah small companies after successful submission of a Phase I SBIR/STTR proposal.
- Nonrecourse loans for eligible Phase I winners seeking gap funding before a Phase II SBIR/STTR proposal.
- Requirements that applicants identify a specific open federal solicitation and apply at least four weeks before the related federal deadline.
- Eligibility tied to Utah company status and standard SBIR/STTR registrations.

UTIF does not replace federal awards or large DTRA contracts; it reduces friction on the SBIR/STTR path.

## Risks and Unknowns

- Curza has raised substantial grant and impact capital ($40M+ per venture page). UTIF amounts are small relative to antibiotic development costs — treat as tactical, not strategic, funding.
- UTIF eligibility and award sizes change; verify the current application guide before planning a timeline.
- It is unclear from public pages whether Curza's current federal opportunities are Phase I, Phase II, or other mechanisms — UTIF rules differ by phase.
- This page does not imply Curza has applied to or received UTIF funding.

## Suggested Next Step

A Curza finance or program lead should pick the next specific open SBIR/STTR solicitation, confirm Utah registration and SAM/SBIR.gov status, then ask Nucleus whether UTIF microgrant or bridge-loan rules fit that solicitation's phase and deadline.
