## Recommendation

Use Dave Bearss and the Nucleus Institute as complementary routing points for Utah therapeutics translation. Bearss represents deep drug-discovery and University of Utah therapeutic-accelerator judgment; Nucleus represents the broader statewide front door for commercialization programs, SBIR/STTR support, early capital, and market-discovery pathways.

This is not a claim that Bearss is part of Nucleus or that Nucleus can route directly to him. It is a practical recommendation for founders who need to decide whether their next step is scientific translational mentorship, commercialization education, non-dilutive funding support, or early deep-tech capital.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Bearss's public profile sits in the academic-to-venture layer: U2TAH leadership, Huntsman Cancer Institute adjacency, and Halia Therapeutics as a clinical-stage example of Utah drug-discovery commercialization. Nucleus's public materials describe a different but adjacent layer: programs that help Utah researchers and founders diagnose the next commercialization door through Grow, MarketEdge, UTIF, Nucleus Fund, and related ecosystem support.

For a researcher with a promising therapeutic mechanism, those two layers are easy to confuse. Bearss is most relevant when the question is scientific and translational: target biology, drug-discovery credibility, IND-enabling path, and clinical-development realism. Nucleus is most relevant when the question is pathway selection: SBIR/STTR, market readiness, operator support, student talent, equity capital, or structured commercialization programs.

## What Dave Bearss Appears To Offer

Based on public wiki pages, Bearss appears relevant for:

- Academic drug-discovery and translational-program judgment.
- University of Utah therapeutics ecosystem context through U2TAH, HCI, and Halia.
- Founder-scientist pattern recognition around inflammation, oncology, aging biology, and clinical-stage biopharma.
- A concrete Utah example of moving a drug program from research into venture-backed clinical development.

## What Nucleus Institute Appears To Offer

Based on public Nucleus pages, Nucleus can help route founders toward:

- [Nucleus Grow](../resources/nucleus-grow.md) for SBIR/STTR strategy and proposal support.
- [Nucleus MarketEdge](../resources/nucleus-marketedge.md) for professors, PhDs, and postdocs testing commercialization readiness.
- [Utah Technology Innovation Funding](../resources/utif.md) for SBIR/STTR-linked microgrant or bridge support.
- [Nucleus Fund](../resources/nucleus-fund.md) for early equity capital when the company is ready for investment review.

## Risks and Unknowns

- The wiki has not verified a direct relationship between Bearss and Nucleus.
- Bearss's public availability for outside founders is unknown; do not imply he is an open mentor or intake channel.
- Nucleus program details, windows, and eligibility can change quickly and should be verified before making a live recommendation.
- Some therapeutics founders may need University of Utah PIVOT, U2TAH, HCI, or direct licensing channels before Nucleus becomes the right front door.

## Suggested Next Step

A therapeutics founder should first write down the blocker: scientific validation, IP/license status, SBIR/STTR proposal, market discovery, team formation, or equity capital. If the blocker is scientific translation, study Bearss's Halia/U2TAH path and seek the appropriate University of Utah channel. If the blocker is commercialization routing, start with Nucleus and ask which specific program fits the stage.
