## Recommendation

Use Florian Solzbacher and Blackrock Neurotech as the reference pair for Utah neurotechnology commercialization: a University of Utah spinout founder linked to one of the longest-running intracortical BCI platforms in the world. The match is most useful for founders trying to understand how university neural-engineering work turns into regulated, patient-facing hardware.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Solzbacher co-founded Blackrock Neurotech in 2008 with Marcus Gerhardt to commercialize the Utah Electrode Array, a University of Utah technology base with unusually deep clinical and research history. Blackrock's page describes the Utah Array as the most-implanted and most-studied intracortical BCI electrode in history, with documented use across dozens of patients and more than 30,000 cumulative implantation days.

That gives the pairing unusually strong signal: Solzbacher is not merely adjacent to Blackrock's market; his work sits at the conversion point between academic neuroengineering infrastructure and commercial BCI products.

## What Florian Solzbacher Brings

- Experience commercializing university-originated neural-interface hardware.
- Context around active implantable-device development, FDA-supervised clinical paths, and long-cycle device iteration.
- Network proximity to Utah's neuroengineering cluster, including the University of Utah and SCI Institute orbit.

## What Blackrock Neurotech Needs

Blackrock's public page points to needs in neural engineering, biocompatible materials, signal processing, regulatory engineering, clinical operations, IP strategy, and governance under a nontraditional majority owner. Solzbacher's relevance is highest where those questions require long memory of the Utah Array lineage and the BCI research ecosystem.

## Risks and Unknowns
