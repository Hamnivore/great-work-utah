## Recommendation

Maschoff Brennan's public service profile — patents, technology transactions, licensing, and commercial contracts across hardware, software, and manufacturing — maps well to the IP challenges a counter-UAS hardware and autonomy company like Fortem Technologies is likely to face. For a Utah-based growth-stage defense company evaluating IP counsel, Maschoff Brennan is worth evaluating as a candidate.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Fortem's products combine radar hardware, autonomous interceptors, embedded software, edge-fusion systems, and airspace management software. That stack is unusual: the protectable innovations likely span radar signal processing (software and hardware), mechanical and guidance-system inventions, autonomous interception methods, and integrated software platform claims. Most IP firms handle one or two of those well; fewer handle all of them.

Maschoff Brennan's public industry and service list covers manufacturing, software, artificial intelligence, and semiconductor — all of which have relevance to Fortem's sensor, compute, and autonomy layers. The firm also publicly lists technology transactions, licensing, and commercial contracts, which are relevant for a defense company that may partner with prime contractors, license its sensing technology to government customers, or negotiate development agreements.

No public source confirms the two organizations have a relationship. This is a capability-based match from public evidence only.

## What Fortem Technologies Needs

Based on public product and company descriptions, Fortem likely needs:

- **Hardware and system patents** for TrueView radar and DroneHunter interceptor designs, including signal-processing methods and mechanical capture systems.
- **Software and autonomy patents** for SkyDome Manager and any edge-AI or autonomous interception decision logic.
- **Technology-transaction and licensing counsel** for government contracts, program agreements, and potential licensing to allied-nation customers or prime contractors.
- **Portfolio management** across a mixed hardware/software patent estate as the product line matures.
- **Freedom-to-operate analysis** as the counter-UAS IP landscape becomes more congested.

Export-control implications (ITAR) are a distinct legal domain that would need export-control counsel; IP counsel and export-control counsel typically operate in parallel, not as one engagement.

## What Maschoff Brennan Appears To Offer

Based on the firm's official service pages:

- Patent prosecution across software, manufacturing, and hardware-adjacent industries.
- Technology transfers and transactions, commercial contracts, and licensing — directly relevant to defense procurement and partner agreements.
- IP portfolio management and opinions/counseling.
- Privacy and data-security work, which may become relevant if Fortem handles sensitive airspace sensor data.
- The firm has offices in Salt Lake City and Park City, keeping counsel close to Fortem's Lindon, Utah operations.

The firm's public industry list does not explicitly name defense or counter-UAS, so the depth of direct defense-hardware experience is not confirmed from public sources.

## Risks and Unknowns

- **Defense-specific experience**: It is not publicly confirmed whether Maschoff Brennan has experience with defense-sector patent prosecution or government-contract IP clauses (e.g., FAR/DFARS requirements around government rights in contractor-developed inventions). This is a meaningful gap to verify before engaging.
- **ITAR and export controls**: IP counsel and export-control counsel are different specialties. This match speaks only to IP; Fortem would need separate counsel or a firm with confirmed export-control capability for that layer.
- **Scale**: The firm's public profile does not describe a large national practice. For a growth-stage defense company expanding into international markets, the need for international prosecution and coordination may exceed a regional firm's comfortable bandwidth.
- **Conflicts**: Maschoff Brennan may represent companies operating in adjacent airspace, autonomous-systems, or defense markets. Conflicts clearance is a precondition.

## Suggested Next Step

A useful first conversation would ask Maschoff Brennan directly whether they have experience with defense-sector patent prosecution, government-rights clauses in IP agreements, and hardware-plus-software patent estates. The outcome of that call determines whether the firm is a strong operational fit or better suited to a more limited scope such as a specific patent filing or licensing negotiation.
