## Recommendation

Defense-tech hardware companies building proprietary sensing and autonomy IP — like Fortem Technologies with its TrueView radar, DroneHunter interceptors, and SkyDome stack — face a specific legal tangle: protecting the IP, negotiating dual-use licensing and government data rights, and structuring private financing without triggering IP or ITAR issues that derail later-stage diligence. Parsons Behle & Latimer's public combination of technology/IP-transaction depth and securities/capital-markets experience makes it a plausible Utah-based first call for that set of questions.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Fortem's core product is proprietary radar and autonomy technology. Its IP is central to the company — TrueView's detection capability, DroneHunter's kinetic intercept mechanism, and SkyDome's integrated management layer. As Fortem scales into both defense contracting and civilian infrastructure markets, the legal complexity includes: who owns jointly-developed improvements when the customer is DoD, how to license products into civilian markets without surrendering government-facing IP, and how to raise private capital alongside government-contract revenue.

Parsons Behle's public practice explicitly covers IP transactions, licensing and joint-development agreements, IP due diligence, and — on the financing side — emerging companies, angel, and venture financing, with a stated track record of representing Utah technology companies through capital rounds. That combination lands at the exact seam where a defense-hardware company's legal complexity concentrates.

## What Fortem Technologies Needs

Based on the public venture page, Fortem likely needs:

- IP and patent counsel for radar and autonomy system inventions, particularly in a government-contracting context where data rights and IP ownership in government-funded development are legally distinct from commercial IP.
- Technology-licensing and joint-development counsel if civilian infrastructure partners or international customers license the SkyDome or TrueView systems.
- Financing and securities counsel as private growth rounds are structured alongside defense-contract pipelines.
- Export-control awareness (ITAR/EAR) in any licensing or transaction work — whether provided by Parsons Behle or coordinated with ITAR specialist counsel should be confirmed.

## What Parsons Behle & Latimer Appears To Offer

From public sources, Parsons Behle & Latimer offers:

- Technology and IP transactions — licensing, joint-development agreements, IP diligence for M&A.
- IP protection — patent, trademark, and trade-secret strategy.
- Securities and capital markets — structuring venture financings for emerging companies, including early angel through to complex rounds.
- Utah presence with Mountain West regional reach — accessible without coast-level rates for early conversations.

## Risks and Unknowns

- Parsons Behle's public materials do not explicitly name defense, ITAR, government contracting, or DoD data rights as specialties. These are highly technical areas and specialist counsel may be required; this page does not verify Parsons Behle covers them.
- Export-control counsel (ITAR/EAR) is a distinct practice area; the match should not be read as implying Parsons Behle handles ITAR compliance unless the firm confirms this.
- Government-contracting-specific IP rules (FAR/DFARS rights in data, Bayh-Dole in funded research) require specialist knowledge; founders should ask explicitly whether the engagement partner has handled these.
- This page does not imply the firm has agreed to represent Fortem or that any relationship currently exists.

## Suggested Next Step

Fortem's general counsel or a senior founder would bring a specific IP-transaction or financing question — for example, a licensing negotiation with a civilian infrastructure customer, or structuring the next private round — to a Parsons Behle partner in the technology and IP-transactions group, confirming fit and experience with defense-originating IP before proceeding.
