## Recommendation

Ripple Neuro is a plausible learning match for Fred Lampropoulos's public founder-operator experience, especially for readers asking how a Utah medical-device company moves from specialized hardware into regulated, reliable, globally used products. Lampropoulos built Merit Medical from a South Jordan startup into a large public medical-device manufacturer. Ripple builds specialized neural recording and stimulation systems with a research base and a clinical-facing sister presence.

This page is not an introduction request or an endorsement claim. It is a judgment-layer bridge: Ripple-like companies can learn from the Merit Medical playbook around regulated manufacturing, quality systems, product-line expansion, and founder-led durability.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Ripple's hard problem is technical and operational: low-noise, high-channel-count neural recording and stimulation hardware used by neuroscience labs and clinical research programs. As Ripple's clinical ambitions grow through neuromodulation and Ripple Neuromed, the bottlenecks become more like the ones mature medical-device companies know well: quality systems, design controls, manufacturing reproducibility, regulatory sequencing, and customer trust.

Lampropoulos's public record at Merit Medical is one of Utah's clearest examples of long-duration regulated-device scaling. Merit manufactures disposable interventional devices across cardiology, radiology, endoscopy, and critical care; its official source describes roughly $1.3 billion in annual revenue, more than 6,000 employees, NASDAQ listing, and continued headquarters in South Jordan.

## What Fred Lampropoulos Appears To Offer

The useful perspective is not neuroscience-specific. It is founder-level operating judgment around building a regulated medical-device manufacturer over decades: sustaining quality, integrating acquisitions, launching products, hiring specialized engineers, and keeping meaningful manufacturing in Utah. The wiki does not verify Lampropoulos's current advisory availability after Merit Medical's CEO transition.

## What Ripple Neuro Appears To Offer

Ripple offers a frontier hardware context where that operating judgment could matter. Its Grapevine, Trek, and Summit systems sit in the neural recording and stimulation toolchain, and its official source points to DARPA/DoD neural-engineering programs and NIH HEAL-related neuromodulation work. If Ripple expands deeper into clinical-grade products, the gap between research instrument and regulated device becomes a central company-building question.

## Risks and Unknowns

- Ripple may not be seeking outside operating advice, and Lampropoulos may not be available for advisory work; this page verifies neither.
- Merit Medical's disposable interventional-device business is not the same as implantable or research-grade neural instrumentation; the transfer is operational, not technical.
- Ripple's clinical path, Ripple Neuromed relationship, and regulatory status need further verification before making high-stakes claims.
- Readers should not infer a current relationship, endorsement, investment, or board role.

## Suggested Next Step

A Utah life-sciences connector should treat this as a possible mentorship or pattern-transfer match, not a sales lead.
