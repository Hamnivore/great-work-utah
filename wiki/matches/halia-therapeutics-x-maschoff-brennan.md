## Recommendation

Clinical-stage biopharma founders with platform IP potential — like Halia Therapeutics' first-in-class allosteric NEK7 inhibitor — should talk to an IP firm that understands both drug-mechanism patent prosecution and the technology-transaction complexity that arises when a platform compound could be licensed, co-developed, or extended into multiple therapeutic areas. Maschoff Brennan's public biotech and life-sciences IP focus, combined with its explicit technology-transfer and licensing capabilities, makes it a plausible Utah-based first call for that strategy conversation.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Halia's core value proposition is its allosteric NEK7-inhibition mechanism. If that mechanism is as differentiated as the Phase 2a MDS data suggests, the IP protection strategy around the compound family, its downstream applications (MDS, obesity/T2D via semaglutide combination, neuroinflammation), and any licensing or co-development arrangements with larger pharma partners will be central to the company's valuation and its ability to raise later-stage capital.

Maschoff Brennan publicly lists biotech and life sciences among its industries, with patent prosecution, portfolio management, technology transfers and transactions, and licensing all explicitly in its services. For a Utah-based clinical-stage company working on a mechanism that could become a partnership asset, a Utah IP firm with that combination is more accessible than a national pharma IP firm billing at coast-level rates.

## What Halia Therapeutics Needs

Based on the public venture page, Halia likely needs:

- Patent prosecution and portfolio strategy for the NEK7 allosteric-site mechanism and any downstream compound modifications.
- Technology-transfer and co-development counsel if the company enters platform licensing discussions with larger pharma partners.
- Freedom-to-operate analysis across the MDS, inflammation, and semaglutide-combination program areas.
- IP-deal advice ahead of financing events that will put IP diligence in front of crossover and institutional investors.

## What Maschoff Brennan Appears To Offer

From public sources, Maschoff Brennan offers:

- Patent prosecution including international prosecution — relevant for a compound IP family that will need worldwide coverage.
- Technology transfers and transactions — the licensing and joint-development side if Halia negotiates a platform partnership.
- Portfolio management — relevant as the compound series grows across therapeutic areas.
- Biotech and life sciences listed among its industries on the official site.
- Data-privacy and regulatory-compliance counseling — a secondary need for a company running patient trials.

## Risks and Unknowns

- Maschoff Brennan is an IP firm, not a full-service pharma regulatory firm. Halia's FDA regulatory strategy and clinical-trial legal work would involve different counsel.
- It is not established from public sources whether Maschoff Brennan has deep drug-compound patent experience versus medtech device patents — the prosecution nuances differ at the claim level.
- Pricing is not public; clinical-stage founders should ask for scope and early-stage arrangements before committing to a full engagement.
- This page does not imply the firm has agreed to represent Halia or that any relationship currently exists.

## Suggested Next Step

A Halia IP lead or founder would present the compound family, the mechanism claims, and any current or anticipated licensing discussions to a Maschoff Brennan partner with life-sciences patent experience, to scope a freedom-to-operate and prosecution strategy session.
