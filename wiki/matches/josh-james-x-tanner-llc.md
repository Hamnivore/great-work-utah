## Recommendation

Use Josh James as the person-centered reference for a Utah enterprise software founder who has crossed into public-company or transaction-grade accounting needs, then use Tanner LLC as the local helper category to evaluate for audit, tax, SEC reporting consultation, and M&A diligence. This is not a claim that James or Domo needs Tanner today. Domo is already public and almost certainly has established audit, legal, and finance relationships.

The practical match is for founders and finance leaders trying to follow the harder part of the James playbook: not just building enterprise SaaS in Utah, but building financial reporting and diligence capacity that can survive an IPO, acquisition, public-market scrutiny, or a major strategic pivot.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

James's wiki page describes two unusually relevant proof points: Omniture went public on NASDAQ and was acquired by Adobe for $1.8 billion, and Domo later went public on NASDAQ as a Utah-built business intelligence company. Domo's venture page adds a current operating wrinkle: the company is a public cloud software company repositioning around Domo.AI and agentic business intelligence while competing in a crowded BI market.

That combination creates a specific accounting and finance problem for peer founders. At early stages, the issue is bookkeeping. At the James/Domo reference stage, the issue is credibility under outside scrutiny: public-company reporting, clean historical financials, accounting policies that hold up in diligence, tax positions, benefit-plan and audit obligations, and enough financial discipline to support either an acquisition process or continued public-company life.

Tanner LLC's helper page and source record describe a Salt Lake City accounting firm founded in 1940, with services including public and private company audits, reviews and compilations, M&A due diligence, SEC reporting consultation, tax, technology and data-security consulting, and advisory work. That service stack maps to the moments when a Utah SaaS company stops being evaluated only by product traction and starts being evaluated by the quality of its financial story.

## What Josh James Needs

Public wiki pages do not identify an unmet finance-provider need for James personally. The inferred need is from the operating context: Domo is a public company with an agentic AI product narrative, public-market pressure, competition from larger BI vendors, and ongoing investor-facing reporting obligations.

For a founder using James as a reference, the why-now is the moment when strategic ambition becomes constrained by finance readiness. A company may want to pursue an AI repositioning, acquisition, secondary liquidity event, or public-market path, but weak financial controls, messy revenue recognition, or unprepared diligence materials can slow or damage that path.

## What Tanner LLC Appears To Offer

Tanner's wiki page frames the firm as an established Utah public accounting firm rather than a bookkeeping provider. Its public service descriptions include assurance and audit work for public and private companies, employee-benefit-plan audits, M&A due diligence, reviews and compilations, SEC reporting consultation, corporate and partnership tax, advisory, and technology/data-security consulting.

For a James-like enterprise SaaS path, the relevant offer is not generic accounting. It is transaction-grade finance credibility: financial statements that can be reviewed by investors or acquirers, audit and tax support, and advisory capacity around SEC reporting or diligence-heavy events.

## Risks and Unknowns

- No public wiki source confirms Tanner has represented Josh James, Domo, Omniture, or any related transaction.
- Domo is a public company and may require national or Big Four audit capacity depending on scope, independence, investor expectations, and existing auditor relationships.
- Audit independence rules can limit which advisory services an accounting firm can provide to the same client.
- Tanner may be more appropriate for a Utah growth company preparing for outside scrutiny than for a mature public company that already has entrenched providers.
- This page should not be read as a recommendation to replace Domo's existing finance, audit, or legal advisors.

## Suggested Next Step

A Utah SaaS founder should read Josh James's page together with Domo and Omniture, then ask a stage question: "Are we approaching a financing, acquisition, IPO-readiness, or reporting milestone where our financials need to withstand outside review?" If yes, the first Tanner conversation should be scoped around the exact event: audit versus review, M&A diligence, SEC reporting consultation, tax planning, or transaction-readiness cleanup.
