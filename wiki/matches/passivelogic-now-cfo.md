## Recommendation

PassiveLogic is a Series C company with a complex cost structure spanning edge hardware, software, installation, and channel partnerships — a finance operation that likely needs disciplined cash forecasting, board reporting, and close processes. NOW CFO offers fractional CFO and controller services that could fill those gaps without requiring a full-time executive hire.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

A company at PassiveLogic's stage — post-Series C, multi-product, multi-geography, hardware-plus-software revenue mix — typically needs rigorous financial operations: a clean chart of accounts, a rolling cash forecast, a board package process, and KPIs tied to deployment milestones. NOW CFO publicly lists all of those as services. The match is logical because the need is operational and recurring, which is where fractional CFO engagements tend to deliver the most value.

## What PassiveLogic Needs

Based on public materials, PassiveLogic is likely managing revenue across hardware sales, software licensing, installation services, and potentially channel or ESCO partnerships — each with different margin profiles and recognition timing. Probable finance needs include multi-stream revenue modeling, cash-runway discipline given hardware COGS, board and investor reporting at Series C standards, close process management, and potentially finance-system implementation as the business scales internationally (Salt Lake City, Amsterdam, New Delhi).

## What NOW CFO Appears To Offer

Based on its official site, NOW CFO provides fractional CFO, controller, FP&A, cash-flow management, budgeting and forecasting, KPI and dashboard build-out, board and investor reporting, month-end close, systems implementation, and internal controls work. The firm describes more than 20 locations and more than 300 professionals. It is not a CPA firm and does not offer audit or attest services.

## Risks and Unknowns

The most significant unknown is whether NOW CFO's consultant bench has experience with hardware-plus-software ventures at Series C scale. PassiveLogic's finance complexity — hardware COGS, channel economics, energy-project accounting, multi-currency operations — is not typical for a fractional CFO engagement serving early-stage SaaS companies. The fit depends heavily on which NOW CFO consultant would actually be assigned. It is also unclear how NOW CFO would coordinate with PassiveLogic's existing CPA firm for R&D credits, international tax, or audit work.

## Suggested Next Step

A finance or operations lead at PassiveLogic should ask NOW CFO to identify which of their consultants have worked with Series B or C companies that have hardware COGS, channel sales, or multi-country operations, and request a scoped proposal for a specific near-term deliverable — such as a board package template or a 13-week cash forecast — before committing to a broader engagement.
