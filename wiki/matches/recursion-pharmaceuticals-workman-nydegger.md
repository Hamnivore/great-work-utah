## Recommendation

Workman Nydegger is the Intermountain West's largest IP-only law firm, with public experience across pharma, biotech, software, and medical/life-science domains — all of which overlap with Recursion's portfolio. For any Recursion program or spinout that needs Utah-based patent counsel for platform-adjacent discoveries, Workman Nydegger is a logical first name to evaluate.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Recursion's core assets are technical: a large proprietary biological dataset, automated experiment infrastructure, machine learning models, and novel chemical matter that may emerge from platform-driven discovery. Each of those layers presents distinct IP challenges — from algorithm and data-use patents to composition-of-matter and methods-of-treatment applications in pharma. Workman Nydegger publicly covers all of those domains under one roof: software/AI, pharma/biotech, and life sciences. A firm that works only in IP, staffed partly by attorneys with science and engineering backgrounds, is structurally suited to a client whose patent questions cross biology, chemistry, and computation.

Geographic proximity is a secondary factor, not a core argument. But a firm that is local to the Wasatch Front means easier coordination on filing deadlines, rapid access for invention disclosures, and shared familiarity with the Utah research ecosystem from which some of Recursion's scientific work originates.

## What Recursion Pharmaceuticals Needs

Recursion likely needs ongoing IP coverage across several layers simultaneously:

- **Platform and methods patents** for its phenomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and AI-model approaches, which may be protectable as novel methods of drug discovery even before specific drug candidates reach late stage.
- **Composition-of-matter and formulation patents** for drug candidates identified through the platform.
- **Software and algorithm patents** covering the computational tools and models that underpin the Recursion OS.
- **Portfolio management and international prosecution**, given that any candidate moving toward clinical development will need coordinated filing across jurisdictions.
- **Freedom-to-operate counsel** as the platform navigates an increasingly crowded AI-drug-discovery patent landscape.

Recursion is a public company with the resources to engage specialized counsel; the need here is fit and domain depth, not price sensitivity.

## What Workman Nydegger Appears To Offer

Based on the firm's public service and industry pages, Workman Nydegger appears to offer:

- Patent prosecution across pharma/biotech and software/AI domains — both relevant here.
- Post-grant proceedings and litigation defense — relevant if platform IP is challenged.
- Trade-secret strategy as a complement to patentable subject matter.
- International prosecution — important for any clinical-stage pharma program.
- The firm also reports recognition in Chambers USA (2026) and the IAM Patent 1000 for the Utah jurisdiction, which are independent reputational signals.

No public source confirms the firm has represented Recursion or any specific Recursion program. This is a capability match from public evidence only.

## Risks and Unknowns

- **Conflicts**: A firm of this size active in Utah pharma and biotech may already represent companies competitive with Recursion programs. Conflict clearance would need to precede any engagement.
- **Scale and capacity**: Recursion's IP volume at a public company stage may warrant a firm with a larger national footprint; Workman Nydegger's regional scale could be a limiting factor for the broadest portfolio needs, even if it remains an excellent option for specific programs or spinouts.
- **AI-patent expertise**: The intersection of ML/AI methods and pharma patentability is evolving rapidly. The firm's public materials describe software and pharma capability separately; it is not confirmed how much experience they carry at the specific intersection.

## Suggested Next Step

If Recursion or a Recursion-adjacent spinout is evaluating Utah-based IP counsel, the first step is a conflicts check and a scoping call in which the venture describes the technical layers it needs covered (platform methods, chemistry, software), the filing timeline pressure, and the jurisdictions required. That call is the minimum needed to determine fit before committing.
