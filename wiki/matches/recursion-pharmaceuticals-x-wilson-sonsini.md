## Why This Match Makes Sense

Recursion occupies an unusual position in the Utah technology landscape: it is a public company, a life-sciences company, and an AI platform company at the same time. That combination creates legal needs that span multiple distinct areas of law — all of which must be coordinated rather than delegated to separate specialists who do not talk to each other.

Wilson Sonsini's public materials describe exactly that coordinated multi-practice capability. Its biotech and medical-device practice pages describe work from formation through financings, strategic partnerships, IPOs, M&A, and regulatory matters. Its technology-transactions practice covers licensing and commercial contracts of the kind that anchor a pharma-partnership model. Its securities practice supports the continuing disclosure and governance obligations of a NASDAQ-listed company. The firm's Salt Lake City office, opened with the additions of former Utah Supreme Court Justice Deno Himonas and corporate partner Marc Porter, signals an investment in local legal depth rather than a satellite presence.

The strongest argument for this match is not cost or convenience — Recursion is large enough and sophisticated enough to retain any firm it chooses. The argument is that the complexity of transactions at this stage (multi-year research collaborations with large pharma companies, platform-licensing deals, co-development agreements) rewards counsel that already understands both the technology-company side and the life-sciences regulatory and IP side. Wilson Sonsini's public positioning explicitly covers both.

## What Recursion Pharmaceuticals Needs

Recursion's public materials describe a company with large pharma partnerships, a capital-intensive compute and lab infrastructure, more than 50 petabytes of proprietary data, and a clinical pipeline — all operating under public-company disclosure obligations. The legal surface area that follows from that description includes:

- **Business development and partnership agreements**: Deals with major pharmaceutical companies for platform access, co-discovery, data licensing, or co-development likely require bespoke commercial agreements that must be structured to protect Recursion's platform IP while making the economics attractive to a large counterparty. These are complex, high-value technology-transaction matters.
- **IP licensing and platform access deals**: Recursion's data and models may be licensed separately from any drug candidate. Licensing a 50-petabyte multimodal dataset or a supercomputing platform requires careful IP, data-rights, and confidentiality structuring that differs from a standard biotech compound license.
- **Securities compliance and corporate governance**: A NASDAQ-listed company faces continuous disclosure requirements, insider-trading policies, 10-K and 10-Q obligations, Form 4 reporting, and proxy matters. These are not startup problems; they require securities specialists.
- **M&A readiness**: A platform company with the profile Recursion projects may be an acquirer or an acquisition target; either scenario requires counsel with M&A experience in both technology and life sciences.
- **Strategic partnership structuring**: Large research collaborations may involve complex payment milestones, co-ownership of IP generated during the collaboration, regulatory obligations, and diligence rights — all of which require coordinated life-sciences and technology-transaction counsel.

## Risks and Unknowns

- **Firm size and national footprint for a large client**: Recursion is a public company with the resources to retain any major national or international firm. Wilson Sonsini has national scale, but the audience for this recommendation should recognize that at Recursion's stage, firm selection is driven by relationship depth, partner expertise, and deal familiarity — not merely geographic presence. The Utah office is a relevant signal, but not by itself a differentiating argument for a company that already has the capacity to engage counsel anywhere.
- **Conflicts**: A firm active in life sciences and technology across the national market may already represent companies that compete with Recursion programs or counterparties in transactions Recursion is evaluating. Conflict clearance is a prerequisite before any engagement.
- **Practice fit for specific transactions**: Wilson Sonsini's public materials describe broad life-sciences and technology-transaction capability, but the specific partner expertise relevant to Recursion's particular combination of AI-platform and clinical-stage pharma deals is not verifiable from public sources alone. The right question for any initial call is which partners have handled multi-year AI-platform licensing deals with large pharmaceutical counterparties.
- **The early-stage framing risk**: Wilson Sonsini's public messaging skews toward emerging companies and venture-backed startups. Recursion is neither emerging nor venture-stage. The audience for this match page should understand that the recommendation is about the firm's transaction and securities capabilities, not its formation or seed-stage services.
