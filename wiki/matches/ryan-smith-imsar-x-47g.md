Use Ryan Smith's IMSAR path and 47G together as a practical map for Utah defense-hardware builders. Smith represents the local founder/operator example: BYU electrical engineering, Wavetronix RF experience, and a Utah Valley company building low-SWaP synthetic aperture radar with in-house manufacturing. 47G represents the ecosystem wrapper around that kind of work: aerospace and defense market access, government navigation, demonstrations, supply-chain integration, talent, capital readiness, and convening.

47G's public page is built for companies in that same category. ## Risks and Unknowns

- The wiki does not show that Smith or IMSAR currently uses 47G, participates in 47G programs, or is available through 47G events.
- 47G is an ecosystem organization, not a procurement counselor for every government-contracting mechanic. If the next bottleneck is registrations, certifications, bid discovery, or solicitation review, Utah APEX Accelerator may be the cleaner first step.
