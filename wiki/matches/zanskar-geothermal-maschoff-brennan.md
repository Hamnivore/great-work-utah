## Recommendation

Zanskar's core asset is a proprietary geothermal exploration method that combines machine learning with seismic and geophysical data — the kind of technical, method-based IP that benefits from early patent strategy. Maschoff Brennan is a Utah-based IP firm with publicly listed expertise in clean technology, AI, and technology transactions, making it a plausible first conversation for Zanskar's IP and licensing questions.

## Why This Match Makes Sense

Zanskar's moat is computational: an inference method for subsurface heat and permeability built from proprietary data pipelines and earth-science models. That category of IP — software-implemented methods applied to physical systems, potentially combined with novel data acquisition or processing techniques — sits at the intersection of patent strategy, trade-secret protection, and technology licensing. Maschoff Brennan's public industry list explicitly includes clean technology and renewable energy, artificial intelligence, and software, and its service list covers patent prosecution, portfolio management, technology transactions, and licensing. Both sides of the match — the clean-tech AI method and the IP counsel who handles that category — appear to align.

## What Zanskar Needs

Based on public materials, Zanskar's likely IP needs include patent prosecution for its geothermal-exploration methods and data-processing workflows, trade-secret strategy for proprietary data sets and trained models, technology-transaction counsel if it licenses its platform to other geothermal developers, freedom-to-operate analysis against existing exploration patents, and potentially data-licensing agreements with seismic data providers or government datasets. The company's revenue model — whether it licenses the exploration tech, co-develops projects, or operates sites — is still partly open, and each path carries different IP structuring implications.

## What Maschoff Brennan Appears To Offer

Based on its official site, Maschoff Brennan provides patent prosecution (domestic and international), patent portfolio management, freedom-to-operate opinions, IP licensing and technology transactions, commercial contracts, privacy and data security, and regulatory counseling. Its industry list includes clean technology and renewable energy, AI, and software. The firm has offices in Salt Lake City and Park City, making it locally accessible to a Salt Lake City company.

## Risks and Unknowns

Geothermal exploration IP is a narrow specialty: claims covering subsurface-data processing, blind-system detection methods, and machine-learning inference applied to geological data are technically complex and may require patent agents or attorneys with earth-science backgrounds, not just software or clean-tech generalist experience. Whether Maschoff Brennan has that depth on staff is not confirmed from public sources. Additionally, the firm's pricing and engagement model for early-stage ventures is not publicly stated; a Series B company should verify whether patent prosecution timelines and costs fit within the company's current priorities.

## Suggested Next Step

A Zanskar founder or counsel should request an initial conversation with Maschoff Brennan focused on one concrete question — for example, patentability of the blind-system discovery method, or structuring a data-licensing agreement with a seismic data provider — and use that session to assess whether the firm has the technical background to handle geophysical-method claims before committing to portfolio-level engagement.
