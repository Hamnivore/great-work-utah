## Summary

3Helix's official website describes a Salt Lake City biotechnology company founded in 2015 that commercializes Collagen Hybridizing Peptide (CHP) reagents and platforms for research, histopathology, and collagen-damage applications. The site markets labeled CHP products for research use, references BASF's NeoHelix Regenerate personal-care launch, and states a mission to translate damaged-collagen science into medicines.

## Useful Claims

- Founded 2015; headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah.
- Company address listed in site terms: 675 Arapeen Dr, Ste. 302, Salt Lake City, UT 84108.
- CHP technology binds specifically to damaged/denatured collagen rather than intact triple-helix collagen.
- Product catalog includes research reagents such as B-CHP (biotin conjugate), F-CHP (5-FAM conjugate), R-CHP (Cy3 conjugate), and In Vivo sulfo-Cy7.5 conjugates, priced from roughly $218–$429 USD.
- Application pages cover histopathology, mechanical damage, collagen identification, and cell invasion.
- Site banner references BASF launching NeoHelix Regenerate based on CHP technology.
- About page mission: translate breakthrough science on damaged collagen into life-changing medicines; vision of treating diseases driven by extracellular biology.
- About page lists core team including CEO Mike Kirkness PhD, VP Toby Barrack MD MBA, and Operations lead Mallory Longacre MBA.
- Products are intended for internal research purposes unless separate commercial licensing is negotiated; terms prohibit diagnostic, therapeutic, or in vivo human use without authorization.
