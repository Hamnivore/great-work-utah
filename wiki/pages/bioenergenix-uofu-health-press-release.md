## Summary

A July 2014 University of Utah Health press release describes BioEnergenix as a University of Utah spinoff company co-founded in 2009 by biochemistry professor Jared Rutter. The release links the company to preclinical work on PASK (PAS kinase) inhibition as a potential metabolic-syndrome therapeutic strategy, using a drug candidate developed by BioEnergenix in rodent studies published in *Cell Reports*.

## Useful Claims

- BioEnergenix is described as a University of Utah spinoff company co-founded in 2009 by Jared Rutter, Ph.D.
- Allen Nickols is named as affiliated with BioEnergenix in rodent research described in the release.
- The company was developing a drug candidate intended to block PASK from modifying SREBP-1c, reducing hepatic fatty-acid and triglyceride production in mouse and rat models.
- In those preclinical models, insulin resistance and diabetes were partially reversed in diabetes-prone animals.
- Rutter stated hope to begin human clinical trials of a drug "in the next couple of years" as of the 2014 release date.
- The release frames the work as a public/private partnership between university researchers and the spinoff company.
