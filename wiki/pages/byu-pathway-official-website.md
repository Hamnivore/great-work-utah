## Summary

BYU-Pathway Worldwide's official website describes a global, low-cost online higher-education pathway operated in partnership with BYU-Idaho and Ensign College. Programs are 100% online, stack certificates toward accredited degrees, and emphasize affordability, flexible scheduling, and faith-centered community.

## Useful Claims

- BYU-Pathway reports 2025 annual enrollment of 88,937 students across 180+ countries (Facts & Stats page).
- Degrees and certificates are created and granted by BYU-Idaho or Ensign College; both are described as fully accredited U.S. institutions.
- U.S. tuition for students starting with PathwayConnect is listed at $86–$107.50 per credit, with 10–50% tuition discounts available to all students.
- Programs include three-year bachelor's degrees and certificates that can be completed in one year or less.
- The site states students have connected with 15,000+ remote jobs (2025 annual data).
- Admission requirements include willingness to live by the CES Honor Code, weekly gatherings during the first three blocks, and English proficiency assessment for non-native speakers.
- BYU-Pathway does not accept traditional U.S. federal financial aid but offers guaranteed tuition discounts and needs-based scholarships for degree-seeking students.
- Portuguese-language bachelor's degrees (applied business management and software development) launched as a limited release in Brazil in October 2025.
