## Summary

Church & State Marketplace is a historic 1893 building in downtown Salt Lake City reimagined as a coffee-fueled coworking space and independent-business marketplace by day (8am–5pm daily) and an event venue by night. The official site hosts a curated mix of small businesses — coffee shop, barbershop, tattoo studios, bookstore, plant shop, publisher, martial arts dojo, and more — under vaulted ceilings and stained glass.

## Who It's For

Downtown Salt Lake City entrepreneurs, creatives, and remote workers who want a distinctive shared building with independent retail and community energy — plus companies or groups needing a unique event venue.

## What It Provides

Daytime coworking and marketplace atmosphere; tenant spaces for independent small businesses; community events; and evening event-venue rental with historic architecture (stained glass, arches). The site invites visitors to work, shop, and meet on site.

## Cost

Coworking and marketplace access during business hours; event-venue pricing is inquiry-based on the site. No public membership fee schedule was listed during verification — confirm event and workspace terms directly.

## Best Fits

Creators and small-business owners who value downtown ambiance and cross-pollination with other tenants more than traditional incubator services. A weaker fit for founders needing structured advising, prototyping labs, or grant navigation.
