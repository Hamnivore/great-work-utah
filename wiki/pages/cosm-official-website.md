## Summary

Cosm's official website describes the company's shared-reality venue concept — large-format LED dome environments for live sports and entertainment — the CX System platform that powers each venue, and the company's technology lineage from its Evans & Sutherland and Spitz acquisitions. The technology platform site (tech.cosm.com) provides more detail on the dome rendering infrastructure including Digistar 7 software and the GPU cluster architecture.

## Useful Claims

- The site positions Cosm as building a global network of "shared reality" venues for live sports, concerts, and original content — without requiring a headset.
- Each full venue runs approximately 100 NVIDIA GPUs for real-time dome-scale rendering.
- The CX System platform is described as the standardized hardware and software stack that ships with every new venue, reducing deployment time from a year to four months.
- The Digistar 7 software, inherited from Evans & Sutherland, is positioned as the world's most-deployed planetarium rendering system with 70+ years of dome-engineering heritage.
- Venues are listed in Los Angeles, Dallas, and Atlanta (2026); the site references a global expansion target.
- The site notes the 2020 acquisitions of Evans & Sutherland and Spitz Inc.
- The funding announcement linked from the site notes $250M raised in 2024 from investors including Steve Wynn, Marc Lasry, and Dan Gilbert.

## Reliability Notes

Attendance figures, unit economics, and venue revenue should be cross-checked with independent sources before being treated as high-confidence evidence.
