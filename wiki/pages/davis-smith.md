## Summary

Davis Smith is the founder and public face of [Cotopaxi](cotopaxi.md), a Salt Lake City outdoor gear and apparel brand organized around an explicit poverty-alleviation mission. He grew up partly in Latin America, and according to public company materials, the brand name and mission connect directly to his childhood near the Cotopaxi volcano in Ecuador. Smith is a prominent example of Utah's emerging cohort of mission-driven consumer-brand founders.

## Impact

Cotopaxi is one of the most visible examples in Utah of a consumer brand built around a cause — specifically, directing a share of revenue toward poverty-alleviation programs in Latin America and beyond. The company's B Corp certification, Questival event series, and public commitment to social impact have made it a frequently cited case study for founders exploring how to build values-aligned companies without abandoning commercial discipline.

Smith's visible story — founder childhood in developing countries, brand that makes mission legible to consumers — has made Cotopaxi a reference point in Utah entrepreneurship circles for the idea that consumer brands can carry social architecture without becoming nonprofits.
