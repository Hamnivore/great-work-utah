## Summary

Energy Fuels' official website describes a critical-minerals company focused on uranium, rare earth elements, vanadium, titanium, zirconium minerals, and medical isotopes, with a U.S. supply-chain strategy centered partly on the White Mesa Mill in Utah.

## Useful Claims

- Energy Fuels says it is Denver-based and has a 45-year history in uranium.
- The company says it is the largest U.S. uranium provider and produces rare earth oxides.
- Energy Fuels says its White Mesa Mill in Utah is central to a U.S. critical-mineral supply chain focused on uranium, rare earth elements, medical isotopes, and vanadium.
- The website says Energy Fuels produced two-thirds of all U.S. uranium between 2017 and 2023.
- The company history page says Energy Fuels acquired Denison Mines' U.S. assets, including the White Mesa Mill, in 2012.
- Energy Fuels says it launched a new U.S.-based rare earth supply chain in 2021 and achieved a rare earths, uranium, and vanadium critical-mineral milestone in 2022.
