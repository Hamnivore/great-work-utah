## Summary

Fervo Energy's official website describes a next-generation geothermal company applying modern drilling technology — horizontal wells, fiber-optic sensing, and reservoir analytics — to deliver firm, 24/7 carbon-free electricity. The site highlights the Cape Station project in Beaver County, Utah, as its flagship deployment, alongside the company's financing milestones and technology approach.

## Useful Claims

- Fervo's mission page describes delivering firm, 24/7 carbon-free electricity by unlocking geothermal energy at scale.
- The site identifies Cape Station in southwest Utah as a 400 MW enhanced geothermal project, positioned as the world's largest next-generation geothermal effort.
- Fervo says it uses horizontal drilling and fiber-optic sensing borrowed from the oil and gas industry to create and characterize subsurface flow paths in hot rock.
- The site links the Cape Station project to Utah FORGE, the DOE-funded enhanced geothermal research site in the same region.
- Press materials on the site document a Series E raise and cumulative financing of approximately $1.5 billion across multiple rounds, with Google as both an investor and a power-purchase agreement counterparty.
- The S-1 filing notice (April 2026) and amendment (May 2026) appear in the site's news module; ticker FRVO on Nasdaq is referenced in related press coverage.
- Fervo describes its founders, Tim Latimer and Jack Norbeck, as drawing on backgrounds in petroleum engineering and reservoir science to apply oil-and-gas-industry toolbox approaches to clean energy.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official company source, strongest for technology approach, project descriptions, and mission framing.
