## Summary

Florian Solzbacher co-founded [Blackrock Neurotech](blackrock-neurotech.md) in 2008 with Marcus Gerhardt as a University of Utah spinout commercializing the Utah Electrode Array — the microelectrode platform invented by Richard Normann that is the most-implanted and most-studied intracortical brain-computer-interface electrode in history, with documented use in dozens of patients across more than 30,000 cumulative implantation days. In April 2024, the crypto firm Tether took a $200 million majority strategic stake in Blackrock through its Tether Evo arm.

## Impact

Solzbacher turned a decades-long University of Utah neuroengineering research program into a commercial BCI company at a time when the BCI sector barely existed commercially. The Utah Array became the foundational research tool for a generation of BCI scientists globally, which means Blackrock's commercial success is downstream of an ecosystem effect Solzbacher helped sustain. His presence in the Salt Lake City neuroengineering cluster — alongside the SCI Institute, Ripple Neuro, and Iris Biomedical — makes him a connective node for talent and research in that domain.
