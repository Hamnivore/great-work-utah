## Summary

The Community Alliance for Main Street (CAMS) is a grassroots volunteer organization revitalizing Heber City's downtown Main Street. Verified on the official site (2026-07-14): CAMS advocates for downtown stakeholders, installs public art, runs fundraising events (including Market on Main), and partners with local businesses and property owners as a Main Street America member.

CAMS complements—not replaces—city economic-development staff.

## Who It Helps

Heber City Main Street property owners, retailers, restaurateurs, and volunteers invested in downtown vibrancy, walkability, and community events.

## What It Provides

Advocacy between businesses, residents, and government; public-art and placemaking projects; event fundraising (holiday celebrations, markets, festivals); volunteer coordination; and downtown success stories shared via blog updates. Open to citizens ages 14+ per the FAQ.

## Cost / Eligibility

Volunteer participation is open to community members; event vendor or sponsorship costs depend on specific CAMS events. No membership fee is published on the site.

## Best Fits

Main Street retailers and property owners who want volunteer-driven placemaking, events, and downtown advocacy in Heber City.

Less useful for tech startups, manufacturers, or founders seeking capital or formal business advising off Main Street.
