## Impact

Fiber optic glass-to-metal seals are hermetic feedthroughs — components that allow optical signals to pass through a sealed metal housing without allowing gas, fluid, or radiation contamination to breach the enclosure. In neutron generator detectors, these seals must survive high-radiation environments while maintaining signal integrity over extended service life. The applications span oil and gas well logging (where neutron generators are used for formation evaluation), medical imaging (some neutron-based diagnostic tools), and potentially defense or national laboratory instrumentation.

The impact is niche and B2B: InnoSys, if it succeeds, supplies a critical component that enables neutron-based sensing systems to function reliably in harsh environments. This is classical precision components work — not visible, not glamorous, but often single-source and high-margin.

## What They Are Building

Based on available information, InnoSys manufactures hermetic glass-to-metal seals that provide optical feedthroughs for fiber optic cables entering and exiting sealed neutron generator housings. The technical challenge is achieving hermeticity — leak rates measured in helium equivalent — while surviving the radiation environment and thermal cycles of neutron generator operation. Glass-to-metal sealing at this specification level requires precise matching of glass and metal thermal expansion coefficients and controlled furnace processing.
