## Summary

Sky Quarry's official website describes the company's ECOSolv solvent-extraction process for recovering bitumen from used asphalt shingles and Utah oil sands, its PR Spring integrated facility in eastern Utah, and the company's investor relations materials as a NASDAQ-listed public company (ticker: SKYQ). The site covers both the recycling-focused and oil sands aspects of the business.

## Useful Claims

- The site describes Sky Quarry's ECOSolv process as achieving 95% material recovery and 99% solvent recycling from asphalt shingle and oil sands feedstocks.
- The PR Spring facility is described as designed to process 2,000 barrels per day, with projected recovery of approximately 10 million barrels over 15 years from combined feedstocks.
- The site positions asphalt shingle recycling as addressing over 15 million tons of annual US shingle waste, most of which currently goes to landfill.
- Sky Quarry trades on NASDAQ under the ticker SKYQ; investor relations materials on the site include SEC filings and press releases.
- A recycling permit application for the PR Spring integrated facility was announced in June 2025.
- The site describes co-founder David Sealock and the company's Utah origins.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official company source for a publicly traded small-cap company. Claims about ECOSolv recovery rates are company-reported; independent verification at operational scale has not been identified. Financial and operational disclosures are subject to SEC filing requirements, making the investor relations materials somewhat more verifiable than pure marketing content. Permit approval status, facility construction progress, and feedstock supply arrangements should be independently verified through Utah regulatory filings and public records.
