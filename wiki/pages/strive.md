## Summary

Verified on the official site (2026-07-14): STRIVE (Startup Training Resources to Inspire Veteran Entrepreneurship) is a free entrepreneurship training program delivered in Utah through the Veteran Business Resource Center at Salt Lake Community College, in partnership with Syracuse University's IVMF and NACCE. It takes veteran and military-connected founders through structured training from business concept to pitch.

## Who It Helps

Veterans, active-duty service members, reserve and guard members, military spouses, and graduates of other IVMF programs with an early-stage business idea.

## What It Provides

A phased program: (1) meet a VBRC business advisor and apply; (2) eight weeks of in-person/hybrid training covering market validation, viability, finance, and a final business pitch; (3) post-program advising, mentorship, and events through VBRC and the IVMF network. Agriculture STRIVE is available by request on the application.

## How To Access It

- [STRIVE program page](https://utahvbrc.org/strive) · https://utahvbrc.org/strive
- Email: [vbrc@slcc.edu](mailto:vbrc@slcc.edu)
- Apply via the STRIVE application linked from the official page; select Salt Lake Community College (SLCC)
- Startup State listing: https://startup.utah.gov/business-resource/strive/

## Cost / Eligibility

Free to eligible participants. Funded in part by a grant agreement with the U.S. Small Business Administration. Apply through VBRC and select SLCC on the application.

## Best Fits

Military-connected Utah founders with an early-stage idea who want cohort training plus ongoing VBRC advising — not for established companies seeking executive scaling programs.
