## Summary

The Utah Arch Research Group's official website describes the group's research focus on hardware architecture for machine learning and privacy-preserving computation, including work on fully homomorphic encryption (FHE) accelerators, ML inference hardware, and emerging compute substrates such as 3D nanofabric. The site lists current and past members, publications, funded projects, and contact information.

## Useful Claims

- The site presents the group's research agenda spanning FHE hardware accelerators, ML accelerators, and post-silicon compute substrates including 3D nanofabric and processing-in-memory architectures.
- Publications are listed at top computer architecture and systems security venues: HPCA, ASPLOS, ISCA, and IEEE S&P.
- Funding sources listed include NSF and DARPA, consistent with federally funded academic hardware research.
- Industry collaborations are referenced, connecting the group to design teams at major technology companies and emerging FHE hardware companies.
- PhD alumni are noted as flowing into hardware design roles at major technology companies including Apple, NVIDIA, Google, and Intel.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official university research group website. It is useful for understanding the group's research scope, publication record, and funding. Academic group websites frequently lag actual membership and active project status; the publications list at the linked venue proceedings is a more reliable indicator of current research output than the group website itself. Claims about research significance and commercial applicability should be evaluated against the underlying published papers rather than the website description.
