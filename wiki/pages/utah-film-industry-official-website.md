## Summary

The Utah Film Commission's website (film.utah.gov) is the state government's primary resource for film, television, and commercial productions considering Utah. It describes the state's film tax incentive program, available locations, crew base, soundstage capacity, and permitting resources. It is the authoritative source for the official structure of Utah's production-support infrastructure.

## Useful Claims

- The site describes Utah's film incentive program — a rebate or tax credit structure for qualifying productions — including eligibility requirements and the application process through the Governor's Office of Economic Opportunity.
- It lists Utah's major production regions: Park City, Salt Lake City, Kanab (southern Utah / "Little Hollywood"), Moab, and Monument Valley.
- The site provides location scouting resources and direct contact for the Utah Film Commission's production support staff.
- It references the state's union crew base, equipment rental availability, and post-production services.
- Historical production credits referencing Utah landscapes are documented across the site.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official state government source. It is useful for the official structure of Utah's film incentive program, permitting pathways, and production-support contacts. Claims about the competitiveness of Utah's incentives vs. Georgia, New Mexico, or New York require independent comparison — the Film Commission naturally presents Utah favorably. Crew base depth and production volume figures should be cross-checked with industry sources such as Production Weekly or FilmLA for context.
