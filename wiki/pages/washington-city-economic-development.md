## Summary

Washington City's Economic Development division supports business growth in one of Utah's fastest-growing metro areas. Verified on the live city page (2026-07-14; fetched via washingtoncity.org redirect): the department focuses on quality-of-life investment, well-paying jobs, sales- and property-tax base growth, and helping entrepreneurs start or expand retail and other local businesses.

The page emphasizes Washington City's location within the rapidly growing Washington County metro.

## Who It Helps

Entrepreneurs considering retail or local service businesses in Washington City, and existing operators planning relocation or expansion within southwest Utah.

## What It Provides

Municipal economic-development guidance on starting or expanding a business, regional growth context (population and market reach), and collaborative project prioritization aligned with city economic centers. Contact staff for site-selection and development questions.

## Cost / Eligibility

City economic-development consultation is generally free; specific incentives or fee programs are not published on the landing page. Contact the department for current business-support tools.

## Best Fits

Retail and local-service founders targeting Washington City within the St. George metro growth corridor.

Less useful for remote-only SaaS founders or businesses outside municipal development priorities.
