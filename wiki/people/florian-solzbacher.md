## Summary

Dr. Florian Solzbacher is a co-founder of [Blackrock Neurotech](../ventures/blackrock-neurotech.md), the Salt Lake City company that manufactures and commercializes the Utah Electrode Array — a University of Utah–originated intracortical brain-computer-interface platform described in public materials as the most-implanted and most-studied electrode of its kind. Public sources identify him as a founder alongside Marcus Gerhardt when the company was established in 2008 as a University of Utah spinout.

For the Great Work Utah wiki, Solzbacher represents the founder-operator side of a decades-long Utah deep-tech bet: hardware-first neural interfaces that have reached human patients and now sit at the substrate of a global BCI research field.

## Impact

Through Blackrock Neurotech, Solzbacher's public footprint ties to clinical and research impact at unusual scale for implantable neural hardware. Public coverage describes more than 30,000 cumulative patient-days of Utah Array implantation across paralysis and ALS studies, FDA Breakthrough Device designation for the MoveAgain motor-cortex BCI system, and a next-generation Neuralace flexible-mesh device positioned as a leap in channel count and chronic stability.

The impact case has two layers: direct restoration of motor and speech function for patients with paralysis or locked-in syndrome, and infrastructure impact — hundreds of academic labs use the Utah Array as a recording standard, making Blackrock a field-level substrate provider rather than a single-product company.
