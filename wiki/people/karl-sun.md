## Summary

Karl Sun is publicly identified as a co-founder and CEO of [Lucid Software](../ventures/lucid-software.md), the South Jordan, Utah company behind Lucidchart and Lucidspark. He co-founded Lucid in 2010 alongside Ben Dilts; the company subsequently grew to 70 million or more users globally and reached a reported valuation of approximately $3.2 billion following a $500 million Series E in 2021.

Before founding Lucid, Sun worked at Google in legal and product roles. His path — corporate tech to Utah-based SaaS founder — is a notable Silicon Slopes origin story, and Lucid Software under his leadership became one of the largest Utah software companies never to have gone public.

## Impact

Lucid Software's products serve tens of millions of users and a large share of Fortune 500 companies according to public company descriptions. If those figures hold up to independent verification, the company is among the top two or three Utah-headquartered B2B SaaS businesses by scale.

More broadly, Sun's success has contributed to the credibility of Utah's software ecosystem as a place where enterprise-grade, category-leading products can be built and scaled without relocating to the Bay Area. That signal matters for the next generation of Utah SaaS founders asking whether staying in the state is compatible with building at the highest level.
