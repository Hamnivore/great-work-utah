## Summary

Ryan Smith is the founder and CEO of [IMSAR](../ventures/imsar.md), a Utah Valley company that designs, builds, and supports low-size, weight, and power (low-SWaP) airborne radar systems for manned and unmanned platforms. This page refers specifically to the IMSAR leader — not Ryan Smith, co-founder of Qualtrics.

Public IMSAR material cited in the wiki describes Smith as holding BYU electrical-engineering degrees and having previously worked as a lead RF engineer at [Wavetronix](../ventures/wavetronix.md). IMSAR's official website says the company was founded in 2004, is privately owned, and operates from a 60,000-square-foot Utah Valley facility with corporate offices, R&D, and in-house manufacturing.

## Impact

Under Smith's leadership, IMSAR has become a visible example of serious Utah defense and aerospace work: multimode synthetic aperture radar, change detection, moving target indication, and maritime search capabilities packaged for Group 1–5 UAS and tactical aircraft. The company publicly states work with U.S. Department of Defense and Department of Homeland Security customers and has maintained AS9100D certification since 2018.

Smith's Wavetronix-to-IMSAR path also illustrates a local RF-engineering lineage connecting traffic-sensing and airborne-radar ventures in Utah Valley.
