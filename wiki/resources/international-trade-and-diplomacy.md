## Summary

GOEO's International Trade & Diplomacy work helps explain Utah's global business pathways and directs companies expanding into new markets toward World Trade Center Utah for trade missions, trade shows, workshops, and grant opportunities.

## Impact

International expansion is easy to recommend lazily and hard to execute well. This page is useful when a Utah company is already past local demand and needs help choosing markets, joining a trade mission, understanding export support, or finding state-backed international context.

For the wiki, it also helps avoid a common mismatch: not every growth company needs venture capital next. Some need customers, distributors, partners, export readiness, or a sharper international market-entry plan.

## Who It Helps

This resource helps Utah companies exploring export markets, international partnerships, trade shows, foreign investment connections, diplomatic channels, or market-entry support.

Best-fit sectors may include aerospace and defense, health and life sciences, advanced manufacturing, software, outdoor products, food and agriculture, and other companies with a real cross-border opportunity.

## What It Provides

The GOEO page describes Utah's diplomatic and international business-promotion work, including diplomatic visits, trade-mission partnerships, and strategic relationships such as the United Kingdom memorandum of understanding, the Région Sud letter of intent, and the Joint Utah-Mexico Partnership initiative.

For company-facing services, the page points businesses to World Trade Center Utah for trade missions, trade shows, workshops, and grant opportunities.

## How To Access It

Founders should start with the GOEO page for state context and then follow the World Trade Center Utah path for specific programs, trade missions, trade shows, workshops, and grants.

For live recommendations, verify current trade missions, grant windows, eligibility, and sector focus before suggesting a next step.

## Cost / Eligibility

The GOEO source page does not state a single cost or eligibility model. Program-specific cost and eligibility likely depend on the World Trade Center Utah opportunity, grant, trade show, or mission.

## Best Fits

The strongest fit is a company with paying customers or validated demand that can explain why a specific international market matters. It is less useful for a founder who is still validating a local product or who has no exportable product, partner thesis, or market-entry hypothesis.
