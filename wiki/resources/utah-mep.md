## Summary

Utah MEP is a statewide manufacturing-extension resource for Utah companies that want practical help improving sales, productivity, quality, compliance, employee morale, technology adoption, and production systems. Its official site frames the organization as a public/private resource network focused on helping Utah industry thrive.

For the Great Work wiki, Utah MEP is one of the strongest verified manufacturing-resource pages because it is specific to operators, not a generic startup community listing.

## Who It Helps

Utah MEP helps Utah manufacturers and manufacturing-adjacent companies that need operational help rather than general startup advice. It is especially relevant for companies with real production, quality, compliance, workforce, technology, or growth-process problems.

Strong fits include advanced manufacturing, food production, industrial products, medical-device suppliers, composites, aerospace/defense suppliers, and scaling hardware companies that need to professionalize production.

## What It Provides

The official site organizes its marketplace around sales, leadership, compliance, production, technology, and a demonstration lab. It also points to CONNEX Utah, calendars, success stories, training partners, manufacturing data, and contact paths.

Utah MEP emphasizes custom plans and access to professionals who have worked with hundreds of Utah manufacturers. The site reports statewide impact metrics and success stories, but those figures should be treated as provider-reported.

## How To Access It

Start at the official site and use the contact path or marketplace categories to frame the request. A good first inquiry should name the manufacturing bottleneck clearly: quality, throughput, compliance, ERP/process discipline, leadership, sales, technology, supply chain, or workforce.

## Cost / Eligibility

The public page does not show one simple fee schedule. Costs likely vary by engagement, training, grant support, or partner program. Eligibility appears centered on Utah manufacturers and industry companies, but a founder should verify the current cost and scope before beginning work.

## Best Fits

Utah MEP is a strong fit when the company already makes, assembles, produces, or is preparing to manufacture something and needs measurable operational improvement.

It is less useful for an idea-stage software company, a university researcher before company formation, or a founder whose primary need is SBIR/STTR proposal strategy rather than manufacturing execution.
