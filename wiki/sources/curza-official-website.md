## Summary

Curza's official website describes a small-molecule therapeutics company developing proprietary antibiotic classes focused on infectious diseases, with lead program CZ-02 targeting drug-resistant Gram-negative ESKAPE pathogens via an unexploited bacterial ribosome binding site.

## Useful Claims

- The site positions Curza as a small-molecule therapeutics company with several structurally distinct proprietary drug classes focused on infectious diseases.
- Lead program CZ-02 is described as a new class of broad-spectrum antibiotics focused on ESKAPE pathogens, especially drug-resistant Gram-negative bacteria.
- CZ-02 is said to be inspired by a natural product and to target an unexploited binding site on the bacterial ribosome.
- Additional program areas mentioned include ribosomal antibiotics for ESKAPE "superbugs" and antibiofilm antibiotics for infections such as tuberculosis, diabetic ulcers, Lyme disease, and cystic fibrosis-related infections.
- The site cites WHO framing of antibiotic resistance as a major global health threat.
- Company address listed: 650 Komas Drive, Suite 202, Salt Lake City, UT 84108.
