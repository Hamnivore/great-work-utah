## Summary

The U.S. Army's Dugway Proving Ground page describes the installation's mission, leadership, location, visitor context, and testing-and-evaluation role.

## Useful Claims

- The page calls Dugway Proving Ground the nation's leading test center for Chemical and Biological Defense.
- It says Dugway conducts testing and support for U.S. defenders, inter-agency partners, and allies to counter chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear hazards.
- It says Dugway provides testing, evaluation, training, and technical support.
- It gives a Dugway, Utah address and describes the post as remote, roughly 90 miles from Salt Lake City and about 50 miles from Tooele.
- The testing-and-evaluation section says Dugway has been on the front lines of chem/bio testing for more than 80 years and includes nearly 800,000 acres with laboratories, test chambers, and field test grids.
