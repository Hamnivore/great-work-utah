## Summary

Electric Power Systems' official website describes the company's aviation-grade battery and electric propulsion systems, including the EPiC battery platform targeting FAA certification for eVTOL and electric aircraft applications.

## Useful Claims

- The site presents the EPiC battery system as an aviation-grade energy storage and power management platform engineered for certification.
- EPiC 2.0 is positioned as delivering 90+ minutes of flight time, framed as a meaningful milestone for the electric aviation market.
- The site lists customer programs including NASA's X-57 Maxwell (first all-electric X-plane), Bell Nexus, Boeing demonstrators, SkyDrive, and Ampaire as platforms powered by EPS systems.
- The site notes EPS has passed major NASA thermal safety milestones, positioning this as validation of the battery's failure behavior under abnormal conditions.
- EPS is described as pursuing FAA certification for the EPiC platform — positioned as a distinguishing characteristic versus non-certified competitors.
- The company is based in North Logan, Utah, and has received a $69M state incentive package.
