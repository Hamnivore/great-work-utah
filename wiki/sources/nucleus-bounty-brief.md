## Summary

The brief asks teams to prototype an AI-powered matching platform for Utah's innovation ecosystem. The central bottleneck is not research quality alone, but market-problem match and the difficulty early-stage companies have finding the right operators, executives, students, mentors, advisors, and commercialization talent.

## Useful Claims

- The brief names life sciences, AI, defense / aerospace, cyber, energy, advanced manufacturing, fintech, and software as core startup sectors.
- Talent profiles should consider skills, domain expertise, stage preference, availability, risk tolerance, and mission alignment.
- Startup profiles should consider technology domain, origin, commercialization stage, immediate needs, and funding status.
- The demo should show at least two or three realistic matches: executive to deep-tech startup, student to research spinout, and operator to scaling company.
- Explainability and trust are explicitly important; users should understand why a match was made.

## Reliability Notes

This is a competition brief, not an ecosystem data source. It is reliable for judge expectations, demo requirements, and the matching problem definition. It should not be used as evidence that any specific person or organization wants a given match.
