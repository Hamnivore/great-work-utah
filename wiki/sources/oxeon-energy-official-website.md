## Summary

OxEon's official website describes the company as a designer and manufacturer of high-temperature solid oxide systems for industrial hydrogen and sustainable fuel production. The site highlights three proprietary system lines — solid oxide electrolysis stacks, plasma reformers, and Fischer-Tropsch reactors — and claims Mars oxygen production via the MOXIE instrument as a flagship validation of its electrochemistry in extreme environments.

## Useful Claims

- OxEon designs and manufactures solid oxide electrolysis stacks capable of operating in both electrolysis and fuel cell modes, plus plasma reformers and Fischer-Tropsch reactors for fuel synthesis.
- The company positions its technology for sustainable fuels, industrial decarbonization, and space exploration applications.
- The site claims OxEon produced oxygen on Mars through MOXIE aboard NASA's Perseverance rover — described as an unmatched in-situ resource utilization demonstration.
- Public metrics on the site cite 300+ peer-reviewed publications, 10k+ global citations, 50 patents submitted, and $100M in technology development funding.
- OxEon describes itself as an original equipment designer and manufacturer with in-house engineering, fabrication, assembly, and test capabilities.
- The company states its systems are validated through laboratory and field testing under relevant industrial operating conditions.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official company source. Mars/MOXIE claims align with NASA and DOE program documentation, but performance, funding totals, and commercial readiness statements on a company site are leads — not independent verification. Manufacturing scale, cost competitiveness, and hydrogen-economics viability require third-party techno-economic analysis and deployment data.
