## Summary

SchoolAI's official website describes an AI platform for K-12 (and expanding higher-ed) classrooms. Core products include teacher-controlled **Spaces** (personalized AI learning experiences), **Mission Control** (real-time visibility into student engagement and struggle), **PowerUps** (focused student AI apps), a browser extension, and professional learning for districts.

## Useful Claims

- SchoolAI markets itself as "AI that connects teachers with every student," with teachers designing every Space and staying in control of AI behavior and content boundaries.
- Product modules include Spaces, Mission Control, PowerUps, a browser extension, and district professional learning.
- The site cites ESSA Level III certification for a Jordan School District study (82 teachers, 13,882 student-AI conversations) reporting a 28% boost in student critical thinking and 2× higher-order reasoning engagement; independent review attributed to Instructure.
- A Stanford SCALE Initiative analysis of 150,000+ teacher prompts is cited, finding ~40% of messages focus on curriculum and standards and 50%+ create lessons, assessments, and feedback.
- SchoolAI research (Cynthia Chiong, Ph.D.) analyzed 23,000+ teacher-designed Spaces, reporting ~60% designed for reasoning rather than recall and 75%+ focused on core subjects.
- Trust and compliance claims include real-time crisis flagging, SOC 2 Type 2, 1EdTech certification, FERPA and COPPA compliance, and contractual prohibitions on training models on student data.
- Teachers can create a free account; districts can request demos.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official company source. ESSA Level III and research statistics are company-cited and should be verified against primary study documents before high-confidence efficacy claims. Classroom-reach numbers (often quoted in press releases as 1M+ classrooms) are not prominently stated on the homepage fetched; treat scale claims from funding announcements as separate sources. Competitive positioning against Khan Academy, Google Classroom AI, and other tutoring products requires independent market analysis.
