## Summary

Strider's official website and newsroom describe the company as a strategic-intelligence firm that uses AI over publicly available data to help organizations address state-sponsored IP theft, targeted talent acquisition, and supply-chain vulnerabilities.

## Useful Claims

- Strider transforms publicly available data into insights using AI and proprietary methodologies; it positions itself as a strategic/counterintelligence company.
- Founded in 2019; offices in Salt Lake City, Washington DC, Tokyo, and London; operations described across ~10 countries.
- The September 12, 2024 newsroom post states Strider closed $55M in Series C funding led by Pelion Venture Partners, with AXA Venture Partners and existing investors Valor Equity Partners, DataTribe, and Cyfr Capital; described as the largest single investment in the firm's history.

## Reliability Notes

The website and newsroom are official sources: reliable for self-description, leadership, locations, and funding announcements, but not neutral for claims about attribution accuracy, data volume, or market leadership ("leading provider"). Headcount (~158 in 2024) and total-raised (~$112M) figures come from secondary coverage and should be treated as date-sensitive. As a counterintelligence vendor, marketing language about capability should be read as positioning, not proof.
