## Summary

TELLUS's official website describes the company's low-cost networked air quality sensor platform, including the CoreDI calibration and data platform and the AirU Pro hardware launched in 2023. The site presents the company's calibration methodology, deployment history in Salt Lake City's valley air basin, and use cases for governments, schools, health departments, and researchers.

## Useful Claims

- The site positions TELLUS around a validated, low-cost sensor network model contrasted with expensive EPA regulatory-grade monitors ($100,000–$200,000 each).
- The CoreDI platform is described as the calibration and data infrastructure that transforms low-cost sensor readings into scientifically credible air quality data through co-deployment with reference instruments and statistical correction.
- The AirU Pro is presented as the current hardware iteration, launched in 2023.
- The company's founding lineage from the University of Utah College of Engineering is noted, with more than 20 years of academic calibration research underpinning the commercial platform.
- Salt Lake City's valley air basin — with documented wintertime PM2.5 inversion events — is cited as both the primary deployment environment and an ongoing validation test bed.
- Applications cited include real-time community air quality dashboards, epidemiological research support, and regulatory enforcement data.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official company source. The calibration methodology claims and 20-year validation heritage are meaningful signals of credibility but should be cross-checked against peer-reviewed publications from the founding University of Utah research team and against independent comparisons with collocated reference monitors. Revenue, funding, and competitive positioning claims should be verified against independent sources. The company is pre-Series A, which means some growth and scale claims may reflect aspirational rather than demonstrated scale outside Utah.
