## Summary

Valar Atomics' official website describes a company scaling nuclear energy for grid-independent industrial products — hydrogen, data-center power, heavy industrial power, and clean hydrocarbon fuels — through standardized high-temperature gas reactor (HTGR) designs built at scale on "gigasites." The Ward 250 test reactor program partners with the U.S. Department of Energy, San Rafael Energy Research Center, and the State of Utah, with a stated goal to go live before July 4, 2026.

## Useful Claims

- Valar Atomics positions itself as productizing nuclear power: one standardized HTGR design built many times on vertically integrated gigasites.
- The reactor architecture uses TRISO fuel, graphite moderator, and helium coolant — described as having strong safety and proliferation-resistance characteristics.
- Grid-independent product targets include hydrogen (via a sulfur-iodine cycle), data-center power, industrial power, and clean hydrocarbon fuels from hydrogen plus captured CO₂.
- The Ward 250 program is a test reactor effort tied to federal executive orders, partnering with DOE, San Rafael Energy Research Center, and Utah.
- Leadership listed on the mission page includes founder and CEO Isaiah Taylor, president Muhammad Shahzad, and chief nuclear officer Mark Mitchell.
- Press items linked from the homepage reference C-17 airlift of reactor components to Utah, community open houses in Emery County, and federal fast-track reactor testing initiatives.
