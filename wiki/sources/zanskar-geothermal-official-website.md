## Summary

Zanskar Geothermal's official website describes a Salt Lake City company building an AI-driven exploration platform to make geothermal site discovery systematic, data-driven, and repeatable. The site frames geothermal's core bottleneck as exploration risk and positions Zanskar's machine learning tools as the solution for identifying viable subsurface heat resources without requiring expensive exploratory drilling upfront.

## Useful Claims

- Zanskar's mission page states the goal of making geothermal power the most affordable form of widely deployable, 24/7 carbon-free generation on the planet.
- The company describes an exploration platform that interprets seismic, geophysical, geologic, and historical drilling data to map heat and permeability underground.
- The site credits founders Carl Hoiland (CEO) and Joel Edwards (CTO) with backgrounds in geoscience and data science, respectively.
- Zanskar claims a $115 million raise across multiple funding rounds from private investors.
- The site references the 2025 confirmation of a blind geothermal system — a resource with no surface expression — as a commercial prospect, described as the first such discovery in over thirty years.
- Zanskar is headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah, situating the company within the broader Utah geothermal and deep-tech ecosystem.

## Reliability Notes

This is an official company source. The blind-system discovery claim is corroborated by MIT Technology Review and CNN reporting but reflects a single data point; method generalizability should be tracked against future exploration outcomes. Funding figures and company assertions about exploration accuracy should be verified against investor announcements and independent geoscience review.
