## Summary

Energy Fuels is a Denver-based public critical-minerals company whose Utah relevance centers on the White Mesa Mill in San Juan County. The company's official website frames White Mesa as a key U.S. facility for uranium, rare earth elements, vanadium, medical isotopes, and other critical-mineral products.

For the wiki, Energy Fuels / White Mesa is important because it is serious work with real geopolitical weight and real controversy. It connects nuclear energy, domestic uranium supply, rare-earth processing, medical isotopes, mining, tribal and local community concerns, environmental regulation, and U.S. industrial policy.

## Impact

The strongest impact case is supply-chain resilience. Energy Fuels argues that U.S.-based uranium and rare-earth processing can reduce dependence on foreign-controlled critical-mineral supply chains while supporting nuclear energy and advanced technologies.

The caution is equally important: White Mesa is an industrial and environmental flashpoint, not just a clean-tech asset. A future stronger page should include regulator records, tribal-community perspectives, environmental documents, and independent reporting before presenting any settled judgment about net impact.

## What They Are Building

Energy Fuels says it is building a U.S.-centered critical-mineral supply chain around uranium, rare earth elements, medical isotopes, vanadium, titanium, and zirconium minerals. Its official website says White Mesa is central to that strategy and describes rare-earth oxide production and uranium supply as key activities.

The company history says Energy Fuels acquired Denison Mines' U.S. assets, including White Mesa Mill, in 2012. The website says the company launched a U.S.-based rare earth supply chain in 2021 and reached a rare earths, uranium, and vanadium milestone in 2022.

## Utah Context

White Mesa is in San Juan County, Utah. That puts the asset far from the Wasatch Front but close to some of Utah's most consequential questions about land, extraction, tribal sovereignty, rural jobs, environmental risk, and the energy transition.

Energy Fuels also makes Utah part of the national conversation about domestic uranium, rare-earth processing, and critical minerals for clean energy and defense supply chains.
