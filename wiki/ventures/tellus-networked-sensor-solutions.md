## Summary

TELLUS Networked Sensor Solutions is a Salt Lake City company that builds low-cost, calibrated air quality sensor networks for communities, governments, and researchers. Formerly named Tetrad Sensor Network Solutions, TELLUS spun out of the University of Utah College of Engineering around 2018, drawing on approximately 20 years of academic research in sensor calibration methodology.

The company's CoreDI platform and AirU Pro hardware (launched 2023) are designed to fill the gap between expensive EPA regulatory-grade monitors ($100,000–$200,000 each, sparsely deployed) and cheap but inaccurate consumer sensors. TELLUS has built an initial network in Salt Lake City's air basin — one of the worst wintertime PM2.5 pollution zones in the US due to temperature inversions — and appears to be seeking capital to expand.

## Impact

Standard EPA regulatory monitoring gives most cities 5–10 stations, producing air quality data with very low spatial resolution. A monitor showing good AQI three miles away tells a school, a neighborhood near a refinery, or a low-income community near a highway nothing about what those residents are actually breathing. TELLUS's approach deploys orders of magnitude more sensors, calibrated against collocated regulatory-grade instruments, to produce granular, scientifically credible air quality maps.

The impact compounds in multiple directions: better data enables better regulatory enforcement and pollution-source attribution; it lets residents make real-time behavior changes; it supports epidemiological research linking localized pollution to health outcomes. Salt Lake City's inversion problem makes it a high-stakes test environment — and a proof-of-concept site with national relevance.

## What They Are Building

- **CoreDI platform** — the calibration pipeline that corrects for drift, humidity, and temperature in low-cost sensors, turning cheap hardware into scientifically credible data
- **AirU Pro** — the most recent hardware iteration (2023), the sensor device that feeds the network
- **Sensor network infrastructure** — deployment, maintenance, and data pipelines for persistent community air quality monitoring
- **Data subscriptions** — governments, schools, health departments, and researchers accessing the network's data

The key technical differentiator is calibration rigor, not sensor hardware. Consumer air quality sensors are cheap but unreliable; TELLUS's 20-year methodological heritage is the moat.

## Utah Context

Salt Lake City's wintertime PM2.5 inversion problem makes Utah both the primary proving ground and the most natural first market for TELLUS. The valley's air quality challenges are well-documented and politically salient; TELLUS's deployment there gives them credible data and public visibility.

The Utah spinout trajectory — from University of Utah engineering research to a commercial sensor network — is a clean example of the academic-to-commercial path the state is trying to replicate across sectors.
